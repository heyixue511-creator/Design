![](images/05965177f30e6587dd81a626bf896373d8227b3c963a9d22a2cdad0fd10255f8.jpg) ## Kengo Kuma: Semi Constructive Kengo Kuma takes an abstract approach. He calls spaghetti "non-constructive,"in contrast to"constructive" macaroni. Macaroni hasa shape, spaghetti does not. Today's architecture must strive for non-constructive spaghetti, transcending constructive tangibility. So Kuma's object is to disorganize the constructive property of macaroni. Aiming to obscure the border between the two kinds of pasta, he finds a solution of endless unity with strings of spaghetti. His solution is articulate and meticulous in how it reflects his philosophyinarchitecture. The recipe for Kuma's macaroni is Cold Pasta with Scallops and Caviar, an interpretation that claims that a cool dish is the most appropriate for making a cool observation about the wavering border between the constructive and the non-constructive. Immerse the boiled pasta inice water, garnish it with slices of scallops and scoops of caviar and sprinkle it with plenty of virgin olive oil before serving.
