# Theory at Work International Style

![](images/436008f3c4d501caf5321d3c6650747bb6fdbbebb5a55976e948d9611f51eec1.jpg)  
Karl Gerstner Packaging for Teddymat, a laundry detergent brand marketed by Coop, a large union o Swiss retail chains, 1964. Here the formal motif of waves and foam link the products, turning each package into a flexible modular unit within a larger design system. Gerstner created this packaging while a partner in the ad agency Gerstner and Kutter. The same year, he wrote the cult classic Designing Programmes.

![](images/a6aab70f0c186627fadb03a2edb508e27230da447eaca28b510f480b4888af2d.jpg)  
Josef Müller-Brockm ann Junifestkonzert, one of a series of posters developed for a Zurich concert hall, the Tonhalle 1957. Through these posters Müller-Brockmann attempted to communicate the music of each particular concert using an abstract modernist visual language. This work exemplifies the rigorous minimalist structure of the International Style.
