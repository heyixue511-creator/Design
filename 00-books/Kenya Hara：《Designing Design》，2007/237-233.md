In 1983 MUJI went independent, opening its first street storefront in Tokyo's Aoyama neighborhood. Interior designerTakashi Sugimoto participated. MUJI's fresh, pure products were the result of product development in the service of quality and function, as well as simple packaging and the use of unbleached paper. Take, for example, the unlikely hit product “Broken Dried Shiitake Mushrooms." This product rewrote the book on the dried shiitake market, in which only those with perfect caps were considered saleable merchandise. The MUJI product is manufactured on a commercial basis using only broken shiitake or shiitake in poor shape, those that were once excluded.
