![](images/3e615fdb194c053756950ac55155949a77c70b22d128399436bb926375cd6de5.jpg) Graphic Designer; Representative, Nippon Design Center, Inc.; Professor, Musashino Art University Born in1958 Concentratingon identification and communication, Kenya Hara has made his expertise the design not of "things that are," but of "things that happen." He considers both making posters and sweeping them from the streets "communication." With his planning and production of the exhibition "RE-DESIGN: Daily Products of the 21st Century" he presented the wondrous design resources to be found in the context of ordinary daily life. The exhibition toured Glasgow, Copenhagen, Hong Kong, Toronto, Shanghai, Beijing and Shenzhen, and aroused public interest in each place. In 2001, Hara was enlisted as an advisory board member for MUJI, and since then has art-directed planning and advertising to espouse MUJI's new vision. In 2004, he planned and produced another thoughtprovoking exhibition: "HAPTIC—Awakening the Senses." This project, combating the contemporary design context, in which designers tend to be motivated by technology, manifested the huge design resource latent in human sensory perceptions. The exhibit introduced the possibility of discovering a new design field that would explore not only elements of plasticity, such as form, color and texture, but also the more complex and effective issue of how human beings “feel." Hara has also participated prominently in projects representing Japan, by designing the opening/closing ceremony program of the Nagano Olympic Winter Games as well as the official posters of EXPO 2005 Aichi. His main base is in Tokyo. He devotes considerable attention to the pursuit of future communication resources accessible in the culture of Japan Kenya Hara has garnered many awards in various fields and encompassing diverse activities, including advertising, signage, book design and identification. For the book he authored in "Design of Design," he received the 2004 Suntory Prize forSocial Science and Humanities, and he has received much attention as a storyteller who approaches the real problems of the day from the perspective of design.
