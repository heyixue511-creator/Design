## The Grid-Exercise

With this exercise I want to focus your interest on geometry, dealing very basically with grids and their value in design. Of course it needs some basic knowledge about grids,which can be found in any book on geometry.

Start with a simple 2– dimensional problem:

Design a regular plane tessellation that does not reveal its original grid.

(A plane tessellation is a way of dividing a plane into regular shapes that cover it withoutgaps)

“. . . a very abstract exercise, but it taught me to look at shapes.”(First Year Student)

The objective is to show the usefulness of grids and to discover them as practical tools. It should help you to understand that a design based on geometric structures reveals an aesthetic coherence. Restrictions are more helpful in teaching than complete openness.

## Approaches:

Start with pieces of paper on which you have copied a grid or try it on your computer. A grid can be considered as a plane with regular shapes. By cutting away a piece of one ofits shapes and addingthe cut off piece to another side you have found the clue. In no time you can produce many variations. Comparing the different solutions will raise many questions. Does it, as is the objective of the exercise, conceal its origin? What is a good and what is a bad solution, and why? Whatcriteria do you find for your judgement? What happens when you distort the grid?

Look for tessellations in your environment. Try inventing new ones. Can you think of practical applications?

## ·格栅练习

在本练习中，我将把重点放在几何学上:主要关注格栅的应用及其设计价值。当然了解关于格栅的基础知识是必要的，你可以在任何关于几何学的书上找到有关内容。

首先让我们看一个简单的二维问题：

设计一个平面曲面细分结构，不要露出其原本的格栅部分。

(平面曲面细分指的是一种把一个完整的平面分割成若干规则形状，并无缝覆盖的操作手法。)

“这是一种非常抽象的练习，但是它教会我怎样观察形状。”(一位一年级学生)

此项练习的目的在于展示格栅的实用性，并且发现其可作为有用工具的潜质。它可帮助你理解，一个基于几何结构的设计可以展现一种美学的统一感。在教学中，限制因素往往比完全的开放性对学生们的帮助更大。

方法：

在若干纸张上复制出格栅结构，或者在电脑上进行操作也可以。格栅可被看作是附有若干规则形状的一个平面。通过切掉其中一个规则形状，并把这个切掉的部分粘贴在另一边，你就会发现其中的奥秘。时间不够，你根本无法创造更多的变体。通过比较不同的解决方案，可以发现很多问题。这种方式既然是练习的目的之所在，是因为隐藏了原初形态吗？好的解决方案和坏的解决方案究竟是怎样的，为什么？你做出判断的依据或标准是什么？如果你使格栅结构变形，又会发生什么？

在周围的环境中寻找曲面细分结构，试着创造出新的结构来。除此之外，你还能想出实际生活中的应用案例吗？

## The Logo Exercise

This exercise can be considered as a continuation of the Grid Exercise, going into the 3rd dimension and tackling a practical design objective. The acquired skills of the previous exercise are helpful and can be supported by information on polyhedra or regular figures generally-that's all basic geometry.

The outcome of this exercise can be modified into an informationsystem using geometric constraints as a means of formal coherence and logic, Many questions are hidden in this exercise. What isgood and what is better?Why? Is it understandable to its recipient? And, when evaluating results, what general criteria can be found? Can they be applied to other design problems?

Design a logo2 – or 3 – dimensional(or even animated) for your institution, based on a grid.

## Approaches:

Try different grids. The simple ones, you will find, are the most helpful ones. The 3– dimensional challenge requires a spatial understanding. Find out where the computer can help you and where not. Discover your personal approach. See how others tackle the project. Discuss it together. Learn from each other and finally, looking at the results as a joint effort to master the same problem, you will be able to study different approaches and extend your personal vocabulary.

## ·图标练习

该项练习可被视为格栅练习的延续：本练习是三维练习，针对实际设计领域要求所编写。学生们在上个练习中掌握的技能可以派上用场，而且他们可以从多面体或其他规则形状的相关知识中获得帮助——所有这些都是有关基础几何学的知识。

本练习的最终成果可被使用几何约束手段的信息系统所利用，作为评价形式统一性以及逻辑方法的重要工具。

在这项练习中隐藏着很多问题。什么是好的，而什么又是更好的？为什么?对于接受者来说，它是完全可被理解的吗?在评估结果时，你可以归纳出什么样的标准?这些标准能否被用于解决其他设计问题？

使用格栅手法，为你所在的学校设计一个二维或三维的(动画效果的也可)LOGO。

方法：

可以尝试不同的格栅形式，你会发现，简单形式的格栅是最有用的。来自三维的挑战，其主要因素就是对空间结构的理解。找出哪些地方电脑可以帮上你的忙，哪些地方不能。发现适合你自已的研究方法，并观察他人是如何应对的。大家可以一起来讨论，互相学习和借鉴。最后共同达成全新的解决方案。通过这个过程，你将能够掌握多种不同的研究方法，扩展自己的知识储备。
