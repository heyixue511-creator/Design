[In a second letter to Way and Williams, 19 July 1895 (LB), Cockerell wrote as follows:] I beg to acknowledge, with thanks, your letter of the 6th inst. accepting Mr. Morris' proposals for the printing ofHand & Soul’. The price quoted does not include carriage to America, as we could only undertake to deliver the books to your agent in London. As to selling price I think that it should not be more than 1o/- for the paper copies & 3o/- for the vellum copies, seeing how very small the book will be. On the copies sold through our agency in London, Mr. Morris would expect a discount of 25% to cover discount to trade, printing of announcement etc., thus leaving you a profit of 2/6 on each paper copy sold, if you accept the selling prices suggested above. I do not think it will be necessary to refer to Messrs Ellis & Elvey, as the copyright has now elapsed. With regard to payment, I meant within a month after the issue of the book. If you will say how many vellum copies you are prepared to take, the number will thus depend on how many we can see our way to dispose of over here. Until the book is actually in the press, it would be well not to make any definite announcement to the public as to date of issue, price, number etc. though I think you may rely on having the book by November. ... P.s. Mr. Morris is afraid that he cannot undertake to print a larger book for you at present, as he has so many projects of his own which will occupy the press for some time.
