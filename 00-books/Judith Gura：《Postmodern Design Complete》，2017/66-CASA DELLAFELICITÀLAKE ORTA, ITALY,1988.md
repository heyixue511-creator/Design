# CASA DELLA FELICITÀ LAKE ORTA, ITALY, 1988 ALESSANDRO MENDINI STARS OF POSTMODERN DESIGN COLLABORATE ON A LAKESIDE FAMILY RESIDENCE. Described by the owner, Alberto ALESSI (see under "Edited Design"), as “a kind of postmodern manifesto," this extraordinary residence reflects the communal bent of its primary designer, ALESSANDRO MENDINI (also see under "Masters of Postmodern Design"), who often likes to take the role of design director, inviting others to participate in a project that he oversees and coordinates. This one, in a charming lakeside area near an old Roman road, called for a romantic, fairy-tale house—one that would not only fit comfortably into the surroundings but would satisfy the needs of the client. The list of collaborating designers was impressive, including Francesco Mendini and Giorgio Gregori, Mendini's associates in Studio Alchimia\*, and several architects connected with Alessi; ANDREA BRANZI (see under "Design") designed an outside fountairı, Riccardo Dalisi\* the gables, FRANK GEHRY (see under "Architecture") the conservatory, ALDO ROSSI (see under "Architecture") the tower, ETTORE SOTTSASS (also see under “Masters of Postmodern Design) a fireplace, and ROBERT VENTURI (also see under ![](images/4002f763379079da8bce68649e2e76cea11cb90f07f888a126cbf1f7cc3de71c.jpg) ![](images/f44700ded108608914ae836e2b126a1c8f36befadf47da41282be574fc5c2481.jpg) “Masters of Postmodern Design") the conservatory. The house was furnished, of course, with pieces designed by the participants and congenial collaborators, representing both Alessi products and the postmodernist aesthetic then dominating Italian design. Notwithstanding its high-fashion credentials, the results of the collaboration were more understated than exaggerated, more intellectually satisfying than visually aggressive, and altogether compatible with its designation as a "house of happiness." Two decades later, for another residence to be built, in the middle of a vineyard, Alessi declared himself "allergic" to other architects, and again chose Mendini to interpret his family's needs for a very personal, life-enhancing home.
