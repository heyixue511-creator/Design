# Graphic Design History A Critical Guide ![](images/9df4418b033e8f264c7baee62207043b7dfa6503e19a91f4ad19f34b44461209.jpg) Johanna Drucker Emily McVarish ![](images/22037f82aaf2d403a287aa36636999850abaf2a2fe560c2d2d22fad4e516762e.jpg) Johanna Drucker is the Robertson Professor of Media Studies and English at the University of Virginia. She has been on the faculty at Yale University, SUNY Purchase, Columbia University, and the University of Texas at Dallas, as well as holding a Mellon Faculty Fellowship at Harvard, a Fulbright Fellowship, and a Getty Fellowship. Her previous publications include The Alphabetic Labyrinth (1995), The Century of Artists' Books (1995), The Visible Word (1994), and Sweet Dreams (2005). She is also known for her work as a book artist and experimental visual poet. ![](images/278b77efcd6ab54508f798bbe26ca6c430fa2070e854533d61b6b0604aa0ab0a.jpg) Emily McVarish is Assistant Professor of Graphic Design at California College of the Arts where she teaches experimental typography, media theory, and topical studios. A writer, designer, and book artist, she has maintained a hybrid practice in San Francisco since 1990. Her work has been exhibited internationally, published by Granary Books, and collected by Harvard University and the British Library among other major libraries and museums.
