A distinguished graphic that builds data measures out of data was designed by Colonel Leonard P. Ayres for his statistical history of World War I, a book with several notable graphics all done by typewriter and rule. Constructing the data measures out of each American division's name (a numerical designation) turns what might have been a routine time-series into an elegant display. (Note that the cumulative design depends on the fact that none of the divisions returned before October 1918.) The triple-functioning data measure shows: (1) the number of divisions in France for each month, June 1917 to October 1918; (2) what particular divisions were in France in each month; and (3) the duration of each division's presence in France. ![](images/66b6fe34464064f8c3c34506674b339353a4c4272da7c6e0a17235b5d0a7afac.jpg)
