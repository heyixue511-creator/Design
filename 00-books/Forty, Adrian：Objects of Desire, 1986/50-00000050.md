## Above: Printing calico with an engraved wooden block, early nineteenth century. A slow and skilled process. From Book of English Trades, published by R. Phillips, 1823. Top right: Printing cotton from an engraved plate in a flat press, early nineteenth century. As well as allowing more detail in the engraving, plates were larger than blocks and production was therefore faster. From G. Dodd, The Textile Manufacturers of Great Britain, London, 1844, p.66. Design for printed fabric by Vivian Kilburn, England, c.1790. Designs of such detail were reproducible only with engraved plates printed on a press. 46
