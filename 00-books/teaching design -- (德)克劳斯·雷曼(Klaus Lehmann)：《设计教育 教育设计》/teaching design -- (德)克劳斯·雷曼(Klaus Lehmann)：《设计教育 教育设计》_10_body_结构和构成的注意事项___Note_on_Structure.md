# Note on Structures and Constructing

I begin with structures and constructions because it is an easy way to start exploring design. Constructing means putting elements together, which then form a structure. As Europeans understand it, a structure has a formal logic and meaning. Construction has been a leading principle in the education of architects and designers.Moreover, construction and structure have determined many formal achievements in the western world. Construction has shaped the aesthetics of classical functional design and architecture; it has become the new aesthetic and accepted as an international style.

In order to understand this I will quote, as a preface, a number of well-known personalities, examining their work and their attitude to construction. It's a personal selection, I agree, but I find it a useful stimulation to what I want to illustrate.

## The Firth of Forth bridge and Bakers expressionalistic functionalism

In the 19th century, to provide a means of transport between England and Scotland, a bridge became necessary. The first bridge over the Tay was considered a bold engineering achievement but proved to be a catastrophe, for in 1879 it collapsed during a storm throwing a train with all its passengers into the river. The commission that investigated the case found that one of the reasons for its collapse lay in structural errors, rescinded the order to Thomas Bouch to build a second bridge and gave it to Benjamin Baker. He was asked to provide a new design, which eventually was built. Being a project of national interest, there was a lively public discussion about it and Baker went to great efforts to justify its design, which he made public. 我以结构和构成作为开篇，是因为这对于探究设计来说是一个相对简单的开端。构成意味着将各类元素聚合在一起，最终形成一个结构。根据欧洲人的理解，每种结构都具有一种形式上的逻辑和意义。构成则是在建筑师和设计师的教育过程中最为重要的原则。另外，西方建筑设计的许多成就都是由结构和构成两方面所决定的。构成塑造了古典功能设计和建筑的美学基础。它已经成为一种新的美学要素，并被认为是一种国际风格。

为了更好地理解这一点，我将以序言的方式引述一些名人的话语，审视他们的作品和他们对于构成方面的态度。我承认这种选择多少有些主观，但是我发现这对我要举例说明的内容是一种有效的辅助手段。

## ·福斯湾大桥和本杰明·贝克富于表现主义的功能主义

19世纪时，为了使英格兰和苏格兰之间的交通变得更便利，修桥的必要性就凸显出来了。第一座横跨泰河(theTay)的桥被普遍认为是一项伟大的工程与成就，但是最终却演变成了一场灾难，因为1879年这座桥在一场风暴中垮塌，致使一辆满载乘客的列车落入河中。该事故的调查组发现，导致桥梁垮塌的原因之一是该桥存在结构上的缺陷，因此撤销了由托马斯·布奇主持建造第二座桥的命令，改由本杰明·贝克斯负责。他被要求提供全新的设计方案，最终工程按照该方案施工。由于该工程涉及到国家利益，公众对此的讨论非常热烈。贝克斯竭尽全力认真完成了设计工作并将方案公之于众。他详细说明了采用这套方案中的架构会使桥梁更加牢固，并展示了他选用坚固的管道作为加a construction, in modern terms, to make its structure transparent andunderstandable.
