Mr. Fujii's work aims to extract the essence of an object from within an extremely simple image. Although he had already done quite a few of these kinds of large-scale landscape photos, which didn't strike me as very refreshing at first, I decided to accept his proposal. I had a hunch that we would not be shooting an outright landscape photo, but a horizon that wouldn't even be approachable without the concept of emptiness, functioning as a vessel that people could fill with their own ideas. This is no coincidental horizon. This is a perfect horizon line, impeccably separating the picture plane into upper and lower segments. Where would we be certain to be able to find a line like this?
