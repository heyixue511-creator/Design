# APPENDIX C KELMSCOTTPRESS EXPENSES ![](images/fbff03e7bcd12dd7e067099cada360af1c9cfde71eb356cde84572228cd55226.jpg) HE document transcribed below came to light after this book was completed. At the Sotheby sale of Sydney Cockerell's books and manuscripts, 10 December 1956, lot 99 was described as‘KELMSCOTT PRESS Ex-PENSES,I89I.MS. ACCOUNT BOOK in the handwriting of William Morris, his daughter May, and his pressman William Bowden, bound in at the end are some leaves fromn Morris's diary for 1892 with a few entries in his hand, red morocco, the original limp boards of the account book preserved, in slip-case smn. 8vo 1891-2'. The buyer was the firm of Bernard Quaritch, which in turn sold the document to Arnold Yates, an English collector. In 1971, near the end of his life, Yates allowed an acquaintance to make a photocopy of it for Dr Joseph R. Dunlap of New York, who has recently given me a copy. A note in Cockerell's hand appears on the flyleaf: ‘This was the original cash book of the Kelmscott Press. It was begun on Jan 17 1891 and the entries as far as Mar 7 are in the hand of Willian Morris, who was then disabled with a gouty illness. The entries for Mar 14 are by his daughter May, and those for Mar 21 & 28 by the overseer Mr Bowden. The remaining entries to July 7 are by Morris. At the end are bound some leaves from WM's diary of 1892, which was but little used.' On the facing page are three notations that frequently appear in Cockerell's volumes of Morris-related documents: Cockerell's signature; the private shelf-mark ‘Morrisiana 28'; and an indication that the expense book was no. 3o8 in the Morris centenary exhibition at the Victoria and Albert Museum in 1934. On the cover of the book is a label in Morris's hand that reads Kelnscott Press | - Expenses - | January 1891.' Cockerell, following his usual practice, has pasted a signature of Morris (probably clipped from a cheque) over a calendar on the cover verso. At the end of the expenses, on a separate page, is a list, not in Morris's hand, which consists of the nanes of Morris's printers, though it cannot necessarily be assumed that all of them were employed by Morris during the first half of 1891. The following names appear in the list: Tibbett [probably J. Tippet], [L.] Tasker, [Thomas] Binning, [R.] Eatley and [G.] Heath, Collins jr [two Collinses worked at the Kelmscott Press], Carpenter, Barks, Eatley and [illegible: Freestone?], H. Gage Cole. When the staff of the press was photographed in 1895, all but three were still employed by Morris. The names of three other printers – Stephen Mowlem, H. Howes, and Mrs Pine – appear in the list of expenses. The three diary entries by Morris alluded to in the Sotheby catalogue are for 2o January, 3 March, and 4 March. The latter records in stock paper | big 5 | reams | small 3o.- | & retree'. Pasted into the last pages of the document is Quaritch's receipt to Yates for £6o (dated 15 January 1957) for the expense book. In the following transcription I have recorded all the information in the book but have made no atempt to reproduce its actual form. The expense book serves as a useful supplement to the later ledger in the Morgan Library, because precise documentary evidence of the Press's activities is relatively sparse before Cockerell, an unusually assiduous record-keeper, became the second Secretary of the Kelmscott Press in the summer of 1894. Perhaps the most illuminating entry is that of 2o May, which tells us that Morris bought two manuscripts of the eleventh and twelfth centuries ‘for study for type': it is characteristic of Morris as a collector that he purchased early books and manuscripts primarily as an aid in designing his typefaces. ## 1 January: [William] Bowden, a proving press and various oddments (£13 18s 9d.) 1 January: [Edward] Prince for punches (£35) 12 February: mould for paper, [Joseph] Batchelor (£7 15s. od.) 12 February: Batchelor, 70 reams of paper (£12) 16 February: Cutley[?], rent of premises (£1 14s. od.) 21 February: a clock for office (6s.) 24 February: sundries (1os. 4d.) 27 February: frames &c (£11 2s. 2d.) 27 February: matrices &c (£19 14s. 3d.) 27 February: type (£25 4s. sd.) 27 February: ink &c (£2 3s. 8d.) 27 February: sundries (8s. 3d.) 28 Februarγ: wages W.
