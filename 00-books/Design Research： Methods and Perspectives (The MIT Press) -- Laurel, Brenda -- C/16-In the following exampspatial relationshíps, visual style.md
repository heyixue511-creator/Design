electronic book review, (ebr), is an online literary journal dedicated to promoting print/screen transformations and weaving new modes of critical writing into the web. Since 1995, it is one of the primary sites for creative, media-specific literary criticism and electronic literature. www.electronicbookreview.com

Left: The splash page, showing the most recent postings. Below: The "weavemap" is an interface to the entire contents of ebr, one which is generated dynamically according to the reader's selections. The weavemap's topography indicates each essay's multi-levelled affiliation with the site's various "threads" or themes. Each essay is also represented by a word-string, a 16-letter placeholder written by the editor. The wordstrings combine with the colored weave to create a visual-verbal tapestry, an always changing representation of the site's contents. Below, top: in default mode, the contents are sorted by date. Below, bottom: the reader sorts the contents by the thread "music sound noise."
