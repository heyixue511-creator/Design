# MARTINE BEDIN b.1957 FRENCH DESIGNER WHO FOUND THE EXTROVERTED MEMPHIS DESIGN PALETTE SUITED HER MEDITERRANEAN SENSIBILITY. TOP Cucumber vase, ceramic, for Memphis, 1985. RIGHT Super Lamp, from the first Memphis collection, 1981. Bedin's name is not as well known as many of her colleagues, but the Super Lamp she designed for the first MEMPHIS collection (see under “Edited Design") became one of the collective's bestknown and widely published works. A foreigner among the Italians, and one of only two women in the group (the other her childhood friend NATHALIE DU PASQUIER), Bedin was born in Bordeaux, France, and grew up in Corsica. She began her architectural training in Paris, but in 1978 received a scholarship and went to Florence to continue her studies In Italy, Bedin met leaders of the Radical Architecture movement and became interested in their innovative ideas. She worked on a Düsseldorf project with Superstudio\*, and in 1979 Superstudio founder Adolfo Natalini\* invited her to Milan, where she met ETTORE SOTTSASS (see under “Masters of Postmodern Design" and "Living with Postmodern Design") and MICHELE DE LUCCHI. When Memphis was founded, Bedin was invited to join. The new design vocabulary that the group was exploring, which replaced the spare, minimalist color palette with extroverted hues, appealed to Bedin's Mediterranean sensibility. After returning to her studies in Paris, she traveled back and forth to Italy to maintain her connection with the avant-garde designers, and began to design pieces influenced by their ideas, concentrating on small-scale objects that could travel with her. When Sottsass visited her in Paris, he admired a lamp design in her sketchbook and invited her to work in his studio. Bedin moved to Milan, and developed the sketch into the Super Lamp, which would be introduced by Memphis ın i98ı. With a bright blue fiberglass chassis and rainbow-colored bulb holders, it moves on industrial wheels and resembles a child's pull toy. The lively colors and playful air may be the secrets of its success, since it falls far short as a functional light source. After the Super Lamp, Bedin became interested in exploring ideas for mobile furniture, and designed several other pieces that could move. She also contributed more lighting to the Memphis collections; the Western, Holiday, and Charleston designs are attractive floor lamps, but none garnered the same attention as her debut piece. ![](images/6aec28364b91f3f3095503310510d9e34a47e042a9766c980693a2255ffb53b4.jpg) ![](images/921740e4734f91f0416c1d8ccb2f10ba7f009d7b00d5da8650063c360b8701ba.jpg) Bedin remained in Sottsass's studio for three years, working on her designs and supporting herself by writing for a French publication.
