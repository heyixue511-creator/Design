![](images/93b6ea8413ba9c1c929e17f16e35f9d571a019e4371aa2ec8f3f11b2049a9b17.jpg) 1.5 Affordances of Doors. Door hardware can signal whether to push or pull without signs. The flat horizontal bar of A (above left) affords no operations except pushing: it is excellent hardware for a door that must be pushed to be opened. The door in B (above right) has a different kind of bar on each side, one relatively small and vertical to signify a pull, the other relatively large and horizontal to signify a push. Both bars support the affordance of grasping: size and position specify whether the grasp is used to push or pull—though ambiguously. 1.6 When Affordances Fail.
