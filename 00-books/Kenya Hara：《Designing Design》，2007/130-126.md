Paper and water, naturally mutually attractive, run counter to one another here. The droplets move down the complex paper structure like pinballs, but silently. It's a very haptic scene. As long as the diameter of the droplet doesn't exceed5 mm, it's relatively tenacious at maintaining its globular shape. But once it grows to over 7 mm, it starts moving like an amoeba, more squirming along than rolling. At one point the droplets may divide, and at another junction, unite. What an exquisite sight.
