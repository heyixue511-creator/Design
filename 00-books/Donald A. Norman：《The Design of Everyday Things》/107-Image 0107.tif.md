The second is the mapping problem. For example, when there are many lights and an array of switches, how can you determine which switch controls which light? The switch problem becomes serious only where there are many of them. It isn't a problem in situations with one switch, and it is only a minor problem where there are two switches. But the difficulties mount rapidly with more than two switches at the same location. Multiple switches are more likely to occur in offices, auditoriums, and industrial locations than in homes (figure 4.4). ## WHICH SWITCH CONTROLS WHICH FUNCTION? Switches for unrelated functions are often placed together, usually with no distinguishing marks to help the user know which switch controls which function.
