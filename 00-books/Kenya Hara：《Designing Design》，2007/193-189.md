In making choices for the museum's identification, I gave careful consideration to unifying the symbol, logo and signage into a single, memorable image, with the architecture's louvers as the foundation. The symbol for the museum (see page 190) is a figurative arrangement drawn from the louvers as they appeared on the architectural blueprint. The mark is an accumulation of lengthwise lines. In a book I can only show you a static image, but the salient point of the work is thatas a motion graphic, it is always moving, like ripples in water. On the website, the image is in constant motion, with occasional changes in the motion pattern. Because the museum site is like a seaside park, I focused on images that might relate to such an environment: rippling waves and leaves trembling in the breeze. The entrance sign is distinctive. Arranged like two metal combs separated by open space, it exhibits a mysterious translucency because of the added translucency of the parallel placement of the two layers.
