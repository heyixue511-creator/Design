September A Konstruktivistischer Kongress (Constructivist Congress) takes place in the Bauhaus city of Weimar, attended by Arp, Taeuber, Schwitters, Tzara. and Van Doesburg (revealed here as the dadaist Bonset). Tzara addresses the gathering on 23 and 25 September, delivering what many take for an elegy: Dada "drew the world's attention to death, to its constant presence among us," and now is destroying itself.Yet the speech has moments of great optimism.Tzara seems to affirm that, like all organic communities, Dada renews itself through decomposition: "[One] characteristic of Dada is the continual separation of our friends."
