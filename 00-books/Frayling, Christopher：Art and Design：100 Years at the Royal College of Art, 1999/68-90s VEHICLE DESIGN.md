# 90s VEHICLE DESIGN Sponsorship for the course was affected by the recession in the early 90s. However, student numbers were on the increase, and projects began to move away from the hi-tech concerns of the 80s towards more social consciousness. Environmental issues were explored, resulting in studies of urban vehicles, low-emission cars and micro' transport that used less road room. These concerns were evident in the work of Niels Loeb and Ivan Lampkin, who examined the implications of electric cars. The 90s also saw a diversification in types of transport – yachts, helicopters and motorcycles were all studied. Concept studies included Stefan Sielaff's sculptural form, the 'Induction Drive'. Cultural concerns became increasingly important, and there was a strong interest in organic and biomorphic forms. Chris Svensson, who had a meteoric rise to fame from graduate to the Ford Ka launch of 1996, played with the language of organicism in his contributions to the vehicle. This also inspired Ford's 'New Edge' design philosophy, which led to an emphasis on roundedness in their vehicle design.
