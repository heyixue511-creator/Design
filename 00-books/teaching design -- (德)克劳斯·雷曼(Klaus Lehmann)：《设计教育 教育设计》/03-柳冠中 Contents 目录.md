I have visited China repeatedly since 1984. In the 1980s my student Liu Guanzhong, after spending three years as a guest student in Stuttgart, established the first design course in China at the Central Academy of Art and Design, which is now part of Tsinghua University, in Beijing. His educational achievements have opened many doors for my activities in China. I am grateful for what I have been privileged to experience and learn in China and I am indebted to the many people who have enriched my professional and personal life. My activities in China have led me to reflect about different cultures and otherways of thinking and to view my own culture in a new context. It was Liu Guanzhong who openedmany doors for me in the educational field, for many of his students are now in teaching positions all over the People's Republic of China. They made me welcome in their institutions and I am happyto be their guest.

In the course of my activities in China, I have realized that Design is topical in China-you might call it fashionable-and that numerous design courses have been established in the general drive to support the need for educational and commercial development. I have realized that the Chinese government is makingagreat effort to provide facilities, buildings, and labs for education-the hardware. However, I also found that there is a deficiency concerning professional practice, methods of teaching, and methodology-the software. This has led me from actually teaching design to writing about it. In China there are many teachers of design and students who are open to learning about western designteaching. In my book, I want to explain European attitudes, their historical background, and my personal and practical teaching know-how. It is written for those who are concerned with studying and teaching design and are open to learning about it and attempts to make European culture understandable. I also want to encourage them to evaluate these ideas critically and adopt whatever they find suitable for their own practice within their own culture. Teachingdesign is a very responsible job; it is not only a matter of transferring skills, of how to generate a shape and make it suitable for practical use, and of understanding marketing.

自1984年以来，我多次访问中国。20世纪80年代，我的学生柳冠中在斯图加特学习三年后，在北京的中央工艺美术学院(今清华大学美术学院)开设了中国第一个工业设计系。他在教育领域所取得的成就为我在中国的讲座活动提供了诸多便利。在中国获得机会，去经历和学习到这么多东西，真是荣幸之至，并且我感谢使我的工作和生活变得充实而丰富的人们。我在中国的活动让我从一个崭新的视角思考不同的文化，同时使我从不同的角度审视自己的文化。柳冠中在教育领域为我打开了全新的世界，他的学生在中国各地从事教育行业。当我访问他们所在的学校时都受到了热烈欢迎，我也非常高兴到这些学校做客。

我在中国做讲座期间，意识到设计在中国是一个经常被讨论的、时尚新潮的话题。许多设计课程设置的初衷就是支持与设计相关的教育和商业的发展。同时我也意识到，国家对设计教育的硬件设备、建筑设施以及实验机构的建设做出了很多努力。然而，我也发现在软件方面，如专业练习、教学方法以及方法论等方面，存在一定的缺陷。于是我有了编写一部关于这方面著作的想法。中国有许多教授设计的教师，学生们也都愿意学习有关西方设计方面的知识。在本书中，我将与读者分享欧洲学者的态度、相关历史背景以及我个人的实际教学经验和知识技能。本书将为研究设计和教授设计的人以及设计学习者提供帮助，并尝试让欧洲的文化变得简单易懂。我也想鼓励他们去批判性地评价这些观点，并在自己的文化里，利用其中恰当而合适的部分。设计教学是一项有责任感的工作，它并不只是简单地传授技能，而是形成一种模式，使之能更好地付诸实践应用，并成为理解市场与需求的有效手段。
