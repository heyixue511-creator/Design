In another cartoon, the occasion of the Paris exposition was used to criticize both the constant threat that the United States represented to Mexico's industry and the government's failure to enact protective legislation. Once again, playing on the ambivalent meanings of the verb exponer, the cartoon argued that Mexican industry was already expuesta (put at risk) vis-à-vis the American industry (personified by a tiger in the cartoon), and this was in fact an exposición permanente (a constant risk). 16 In the same way, the various foci of the fair were used to caricature Mexico's internal political life.
