Hieroglyphic script and modern – how do these go together? A 3,ooo year-old script from the childhood of humanity, which has long been superseded and can be nothing more to people today than a pleasing ornament. – But only because we cannot recognize the little pictures quickly enough, nor grasp their sense. Modern people are especially accustomed to reading pictures. We watch films, and we like them all the more when they have little text. We read whole novels from films as we did earlier from books; films go much faster – an expression or a movement must not first be described with many words; on the detour through reading the individual ideas follow much slower than they do in the actual processes that are being described! We also know foreign lands better from photographs than from long descriptions, which we do not take the time to read. In short, we read pictures everywhere because it is quicker. – At the same time, a picture script is beginning to be formed, although not everybody is yet aware of it – there were also illiterates in ancient Egypt. Fundamentally, it is of interest to everybody that there are innumerable millions of black people in the interior of Africa, who may one day burst forth; and it will interest them even more to know that this is not at all true, and that black people only constitute roughly one twentieth of the whole population of earth. Above all, the individual groups can be more quickly compared with each other and the relationships can be better perceived, if this picture has been studied in detail. – In this way other quantities can be represented, for example the division of the same people by

One need only know that a certain sign represents a certain quantity; if a sign is repeated, say, three times, then it means that three times the quantity is shown. So quantitative relationships can be directly depicted and grasped, without having to make the detour through those despicable numbers. So it follows that, in this case, picture script is more rapidly readable than numerals and letters This picture script is much closer to the concrete facts of the matter that it conveys, much closer than alphabetic script. That is its charm, which it works on many people, in particular on those unspoiled by too much education, and consequently also on young people. – These pictures should not be too naturalistic. An over-precise adherence to details would distract from the true content of the picture, which lies in the quantitative relationships. It should also be remembered that we are dealing not only with pictures, but also with kinds of 'characters' in a script; this means that we must see to it that the individual characters could be reused in other circumstances, in order to facilitate rapid reading through recognition.

It is particularly important for rapid reading that a unified and rational method be generally maintained for the layout and representation.
