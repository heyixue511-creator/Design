## Acknowledgments ![](images/752ada40551121e01df0a0cb8518635d99a2ed6e32daeeb421904d6f6e5e0b90.jpg) I am because we are. Publishing a book like Decolonizing Design is a collective enterprise. There are so many people who have contributed to this process through their conversations with me, their everyday and extraordinary actions of support and nurturance, or just the exemplar of their being in the Universe. First, I acknowledge my ancestors, whose intergenerational sacrifices, resilience, and determination for me to have a better future drive my commitment to be an ancestor to others. I recognize my great-grandmother, Katrina Middlebrook (aka Grandmugs); my grandmothers, Delores Elizabeth Tunstall (aka Mugs) after whom I am named and Bette Helms; my grandfathers, John E. Tunstall III and George Helms II; and my mother, Leatha Laverne Tunstall. I acknowledge my close biological family: mothers, Theresa Tunstall and Aunt Jillane Tunstall; fathers, Raymond Tunstall and Uncle Sonny (George Helms III); sisters, LaTina Tunstall and Natasha Tunstall-Smith; and brother, DuShawn Tunstall. I acknowledge those directly involved in the development of the book. Thank you, Victoria Hindley, MIT Press acquisitions editor, for asking if I wanted to write a book and guiding me through the acquisitions process. Thank you, Mary Ann Lee, Goddess of My Schedule, for creating and protecting my time to write the book. I also acknowledge the four anonymous peer reviewers whose comments improved the manuscript immeasurably. I have undying gratitude to Salah Bachir, Jacob Yerex, and Max for lending me the 199 house as my writer's retreat, our in-depth conversations about the book and everything else, and with Nida, being chosen family, complete with evening dinners, 7:30 pm Jeopardy, and nightly movies. People say that writing is an isolating activity, but it was a communal experience due to you all. Thank you, beta-readers and those who gave me permission to share our overlaps in experiences with the world: Gabe Gonda, who reminded me to keep the human-interest element when I was doubting myself; Lillian Allen, Jason Baerg, Deirdre Barron, David Brown, Kestin Cornwall, Camille Georgeson-Usher, Caroline Langill, Stephanie McRae, Peter Morin, and Howard Munroe. Infinite gratitude to you, Reanna Sartoretto, as the professional fact-checker, whose diligence and care helped me feel confident about everything that is written in the book. I have deep appreciation and admiration for the design dream team of Ene Agi for the illustrations, Sadie Red Wing for the cover design, and Silas Munro and Brian Johnson of Polymode for the brilliant book design. I wanted to use this book to assemble the most talented Indigenous and Black designers. Thank you for making this book even more meaningful through your contributions. Finally, I want to acknowledge my former and current students and colleagues across the United States, Australia, and Canada who have taught me why decolonizing design is important to their liberation as makers who care deeply about the world. Again, I am because we are.
