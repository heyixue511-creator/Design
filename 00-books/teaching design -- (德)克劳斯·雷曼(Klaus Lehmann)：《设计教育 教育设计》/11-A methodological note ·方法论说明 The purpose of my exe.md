## A methodological note

In my teaching of foundations I follow a principle also known by science: a problem in practice is analysed and translated into a general or universal question. It then is dealt with in an abstract manner, by experiment, as an exercise. Its results, in a third step, are evaluated, producing criteria. Its findings have a universal character and can be applied in practice to other problems, so providing a methodology for solving practical and formal problems.

I introduced the exercises as a foundation to project work, where the objective isto tackle a live project. Project work demands a lot of research, requires the student to master conceptual approaches, functional requirements, user requirements, and methods of manufacture, to consider ecological implications, human factors, or to meet marketing objectives. Mostly it leaves little room for experimentation or research into formal and aesthetic values.

The design-shape, appearance, and emotional value-are the gist of foundation exercises. Foundations therefore, forgetting functional requirements, aim to concentrate on the exploration of form and structure. Foundation exercises are meant to provide the student with a repertoire of formal options for practical applications. They are developed, unlike the exercises of the Bauhaus foundation course with its more artistic background, from practical problems in the design process. 我采取了一种实用主义方法。我的教学经验告诉我，只有非功利性的、抽象的、普遍性的练习模式才能将人的创新能力最大化地激发出来。在参与项目工作时，大家经常面临很大的压力，此时就需要一种之前曾获得的、既定的全能操作模式发挥作用了。而获得这种模式就是基础教学的最终目的。

## ·方法论说明

在我教学设计基础的过程中，我都遵循以下科学的原则，即在实践练习中遇到的问题要经过详细分析，转化为一种可以普遍存在的问题。然后它就可以使用一种以实验为代表的、更加抽象的方式来进行进一步练习。接下来开始评估实验的结果，并创立新的标准。实验成果可以适用于各种情况，并在实践中解决其他类似的问题，因此这种做法为解决实际问题与正式问题提供了一套方法论。

我把练习当作是进行项目工作的基础，目的是为了承接真实的项目。项目工作需要做很多前期调研，要求学生们掌握主要的认知方法、功能要求、用户规章以及制作方法，思考人的因素是否会对生态产生影响或是否达成营销目的等。大多数情况下，进行形式与美学价值研究或实验的余地非常有限。

设计形状、外观和情感价值是基础练习的本质内容。因此，除了功能要求之外，基础教学把重点放在形式和结构的开发与研究上。基础练习旨在向学生提供关于实践应用范围选择的正确方法。不同于包豪斯更富于艺术特征的基础课程的练习模式，这里的基础练习通过在设计过程中发现实际问题来发展完善自身的系统。正是由于它们普遍适用的特征，学生们可以集中精力研究形式价值和视觉规则，训concentrate on formal values, on visual laws, train his creativity, and developcriteria for rational judgement. Foundation exercises are also the testingground for individual approaches, in which the student learns to find his ownandpersonal methodology.

## The purpose of my exercises

The problems I have encountered in my own practical design work have led me to develop exercises that focus on simple and prototypical issues. They are designed to encourage fresh visual exploration-perception. By experimenting practically students build up a personal and general repertoire-learning by doing. Through presentation and discussion they areable to develop criteria for judgement of the form and structure of the work and to develop verbal skills and the ability of critical thinking-reflection.

“Man soll Denken lehren, nicht Gedachtes.”

“You should teach thinking and not what has been thought.”

German art critic Cornelius Gurlitt(1850 – 1938).

## Recapitulating—why foundations are important

—because of fast changing technologies, markets, and fashions.

—because they provide the“thinking and designing repertoire”, the me thodology to tackle both present AND future problems.

—because they are the most valuable, the most sustainable, and thereforethe most fundamental part of design tuition.

## ·练习目的

在进行实际设计工作时，所遇到的所有问题让我决定编写一些聚焦于简单而富有代表性议题的练习，目的是鼓励全新的视觉探索与认知行动。通过亲手做实验，学生们形成了适用于个人和普遍情况的知识储备，即在动手中学习。通过做演示与讨论的过程，他们能够发展并归纳出评价工作形式和结构的一套标准，并且获得发展自身语言能力和批判性思维能力的机会。

“一个人应该讲授思考的方法，而不是别人思考的结果。”德国艺术批评家科尼利厄斯·古尔利特(1850—1938)

## ·总结——为什么基础如此重要

—因为技术发展、市场变化、时尚潮流的变化十分迅速。

—因为它们可以提供丰富的“思考和设计的知识储备”，以应对各种当下以及未来可能会遇到的问题。

—因为它们是最具价值、持续时间最长，因此也是设计教育中最基本的组成部分。
