# VIEWING THE WORLD FROM THE TIP OF ASIA Where All Cultures Are Accepted The other day I wrote down the names of all the cities and towns abroad that I've visited and found they number nearly a hundred. Several have left an indelible impression on my mind and I always find a new place fresh and stimulating. Travel revitalizes me and foreign countries are a perpetual source of inspiration. But I've never thought of living in any of them. I regard Tokyo as my place. New York is creative, but to respect it, it seems I have to look at it from a distance. I'm attracted to Beijing and Berlin, overflowin g with signs of transfiguration into new metropolitan identities, but I can't find any reason to settle there. For me it feels natural to make up my mind to face the world from that tip of Asia, Tokyo. Tokyo is a city full of curiosity. Tokyo is more passionate than any other city in the world when it comes to collecting information about other cultures. And it is a city that carefully assimilates that information and exercises its industrious intellect to really understand what's happening in the world. It seems to me that behind this there is a consciousness at work that says that where we standis not the center of the world, and furthermore, that there is no such thing as the center of the world.
