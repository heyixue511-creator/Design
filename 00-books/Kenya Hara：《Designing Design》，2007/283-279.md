MUJI newspaper advertisement, 2005. a simple living room table for the youngster and a table that can be used in the older couple's bedroom. By making a table with the simplest design possible, we create a table that will adjust to a variety of living environments and will be suitable to any stage of life. This is "quality" as MUJI sees it. The white porcelain bowl featured in the tearoom poster series is one of MUJI's rice bowls. These vessels are produced for MUJI in the small city of Hasami in Nagasaki Prefecture, Japan's traditional porcelain production center. They are strikingly simple, but it is the kind of simplicity that embodies scrutiny of not only today's dietary culture, but also the consideration of all sorts of dining situations. These photos feature a combination of a national treasure and a rice bowl, but are hardly an advertising gimmick. They illustrate collaboration between two creations of different eras that share the same aesthetics. The series photographer Yoshihiko Ueda is an expert in firmly depicting the beauty hidden within an object.
