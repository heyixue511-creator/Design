It is therefore to his innovations in this quarter that we must look for connections between the design of the pots and the method of manufacture. The particular problem that compelled Wedgwood to adopt new designs for his pots was the need to find a way of creating variety without increasing the costs of production, and without having to accept irregularities and inconsistencies in workman ship.
