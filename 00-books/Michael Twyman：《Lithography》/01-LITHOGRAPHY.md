## ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS

It is impossible to acknowledge all those who contributed to the conception of this book, but my special thanks must go to my teachers at Reading University, many of whom it has since been my good fortune to count as colleagues. I am grateful to Professor Anthony Betts, whose guidance of a general kind has been of lasting value, Frank Ormrod, who introduced me to the practical side of lithography, and H. V. Redman, for his support and encouragement at a vital stage. I am particularly indebted to Professor L. D. Ettlinger, whose teaching of Renaissance art history inspired me to take an interest in the work of the past and prompted me to undertake a modest piece of research of my own. If this book has any merit at all it is largely due to his example.

I am grateful to the staff of many libraries and print rooms and particularly to Mr. Turner Berry and Mr. James Mosley of the St. Bride Printing Library, Mr. Harry Carter and Mrs. Thrussell (who cared for the John Johnson Collection while it was at the University Press, Oxford), and successive Librarians and the staff of my own university library. All have been most helpful and have offered generous facilities over a number of years. I should also like to thank Dr. B. Degenhart of the Staatliche Graphische Sammlung and Dr. R A. Winkler for the kindness they showed me while I was working in Munich.

For permission to reproduce items in their collections acknowledgements are due to the Librarians or Curators of the following: Bibliothèque Nationale, Cabinet des Estampes, 71-3; Bodleian Library, John Johnson Collection, 92, 95-7, 99–101, 103-4, 107-12; British Museum, 1, 65–7, 74–5, 89, 115-16; Cambridge University Library, 93-4, 98, 105, 122–3, 137–46, 149-54, 156–7; Ministry of Defence Library, 2, 6–10; Reading University Library, 34-5, 38, 40, 42–9, 55-7, 78–9, 117–18; St. Bride Printing Library, 3-5, 12–33, 37, 39, 41, 50–4, 58–64, 69–70, 76–7, 84-5, 106, 158; Victoria and Albert Museum, 11, 102, 119, 136, 155.

Mr. Harry Carter, Mr. S. A. Kitzinger, Mr. H. V. Redman, and Dr. Berthold Wolpe have read the book either in typescript or at proof stage and have all made valuable suggestions. Mrs. Caroline Lessiter, Mr. O. C. Giles, and Dr. A. Menhennet have helped with translation problems.

My thanks are also due to Mr. I. McLcan and the staff of the Photographic Department of Reading University Library for their patience and skill in meeting my requirements for the illustrations, and to the Publisher and Printer for the care they have taken in the preparation and presentation of this volume.

Finally, I must thank my wife for her understanding and tolerance, and for nursing me through the final stages of the work.
