## 190–192.

2001 Comics Weekly, 1982

Editor: 2001 Editorial Board

This magazine featured a mix of manga and local comics. The Japanese comics it carried, such as Doctor Slump and Crying Killer, were the most popular manga at the time. Contributors included Chu Jo-yee and other relatively unknown local artists.

## 193-196.

Yuk-long Manhua Biweekly, 1984

Producer: Kie Man-kin

This magazine had two stars: Wong Yuk-long, the comics publisher and artist, and Yuan Dai-yong, the era's highest-paid caricaturist. Each cover featured a drawing of Wong Yuk-long with a different celebrity, which attested to Wong's own celebrity. The biweekly magazine primarily ran lighthearted stories and humorous comics, but since Wong was trying to build his relationship with the entertainment industry, it also included regular coverage of entertainment news as well. It also introduced the drawing style of kung fu and adventure manhua artists to a more mainstream audience.

197–198.

Hai-dai Chau Pictorial, 1983

Art/Script: Dong Pui-sun

Hong Kong comedian Charles Ng inspired the look and attitude of this comic's main character, while the stories took a humorous approach to social issues and personal relationships. The comics in this short-lived, self-published collection first appeared in the Sun Daily's manhua column.

## 199.

One Is Nice, 1983

Art/Script: Tou Sam

This lighthearted and humorous series was first published in the newspaper Oriental Daily. The action revolves around a little boy similar to A-giu, who appears in the 1990 comic Three Little Guys.
