But the acquisition of such “outward and visible signs of an inward and spiritual grace" represented a final step in the successful, though largely unconscious, adaptation of religious imagery to the advertising tableaux, the modern icons of a faith in mass consumption. The relative power of such visual clichés to evoke numinous associations becomes apparent when we compare them with the awkward efforts of advertising texts.
