Building data measures out of the data increases the quantitative detail and dimensionality of a graphic. The stem-and-leaf plot constructs the distribution of a variable with numbers themselves: ![](images/8a510dedd89c058e793d7f91377155b64e41a0fe8d872b49471e4e5c832498a6.jpg) The idea of making every graphical element effective was behind the design of the stem-and-leaf plot. In presenting his invention, John Tukey wrote: "If we are going to make a mark, it may as well be a meaningful one. The simplest—and most useful—meaningful mark is a digit."2 Here, too, the data form the data measure.
