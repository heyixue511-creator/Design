## 81.

The Voice of Good Companion Pictorial, 1956 This was another illustrated magazine assigned by Hong Kong Catholic schools. At first, it was printed in one color and was mostly text, for after-school reading. It later became a full-color illustrated book with less text and less focus on religion.

## 82-85.

## Children's Literary Collection, 1956

Published by Children's Paradise, these children's books were mostly text, but they also included many illustrations. Targeted at students in the fourth and fifth grades, it included stories from famous literature and fairy tales, as well as some original stories and historical tales.

## 86–87.

Silver Light Daily Sunday Color Pictorial, 1956 Silver Light was the first daily produced with offset color printing; every Sunday it published a cartoon supplement edited by the Bao brothers. Featured comics included the Bao’s Little Angeli and Bobby, as well as American comics such as Walt Disney's Mickey Mouse and Donald Duck.

## 88–91.

## Children's Corner Pictorial Fortnightly, 1956

This imitation of Children's Paradise, published by World Publisher, was one of the original's main competitors, although it never achieved the same level of success. In 1978, the magazine was redesigned, and it adopted the name Little Pilots (Fei-gei Chai) Organization from a popular children’s television program. The magazine tried to adapt to kids’ fickle tastes, but the changes were not successful, and it folded soon after the redesign.
