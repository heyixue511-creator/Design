In 1984 the American firm SWID POWELL brought a new focus on gracious dining in a fast-food era by commissioning dinnerware and flatware from a marquee-name roster of postmodernists (and late modernists). Their urbane products were more restrained and less whimsically postmodern, however, avoiding any taint of trendiness or kitsch. CLETO MUNARI, another Italian producer, introduced a collection of sterling silver objects by twenty high-profile architects in 1987, and later added a collection of fine modern jewelry by architectural stars. The jewelry used strong color and assertive forms in historicist-influenced pieces that were unlike anything else on the market. Textile designers welcomed the new freedom of expression, either adapting the eyeopening graphics of MEMPHIS designs or drawing motifs from pop sources and ethnic cultures. New approaches to pattern and texture were made possible with computerized looms.
