The fringe of dashes in the dot-dash-plot can connect a series of bivariate scatters in a rugplot (since it resembles a set of fringed rugs—and covers the statistical ground): ![](images/8d16d213ff06fb90c52fd10cfa0ee732a88943866e8c6b886ad944b980adcd17.jpg) Reflecting the one-dimensional projections from each scatter, the dashes encourage the eye to notice how each plot filters and translates the data through the scatter from one adjacent plot to the next. Sometimes it is useful to think of each bivariate scatter as the imperfect empirical representation of an underlying curve that transforms one variable into another. In the rugplot, the sequence of variables can wander off as appropriate. The quantitative history of a single observation can be traced through a series of one- and two-dimensional contexts.
