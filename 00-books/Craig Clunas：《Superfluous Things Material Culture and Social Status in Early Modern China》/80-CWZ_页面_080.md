There can be little doubt about the basically social aims of Wen Zhenheng's proj ect, and little doubt , too, that the prominence given to material culture and to consumption behaviour in the literature of the late Ming period reflects a generalized intuition , if not an explicit awareness, of the part which things could play in a hierarchically structured but highly stressed society.
