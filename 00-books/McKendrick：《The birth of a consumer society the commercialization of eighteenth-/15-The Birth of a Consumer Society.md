# The Birth of a Consumer Society

The Commercialization of Eighteenth-Century England By Neil McKendrick, John Brewer, and J. H. Plumb

“Of the several books on the emerging field of consumer history which have appeared in recent years. . . . The Birth of a Consumer Society is the best."—The Journal of Consumer Affairs

“Their provocative essays not only force a reconceptualization of eighteenth-century society but also suggest the need for futher research of the political, social, and cultural changes surrounding the consumer revolution."—Winterthur Portfolio

“. . . important and engrossing. . . ."—The Sunday Telegraph

This important and pioneering book analyzes the changes in attitude and thought, prosperity, standards of living, and British law that were necessary to the birth of a consumer society. Neil McKendrick describes the way eighteenth-century English entrepreneurs (including Josiah Wedgwood) designed, marketed, advertised, and distributed consumer products; John Brewer looks at the commercialization of politics; and J. H. Plumb considers the commercialization of leisure activities, surveying such topics as the history of the pet industry, the fad for indoor plants, and the effect of the growth of a consumer society on the lives of children.

NEIL McKENDRICK, Lecturer in English Economic History at Cambridge University, is editor of Historical Perspectives: Studies in English Thought and Society in Honour of J. H. Plumb.

JOHN BREWER, Professor of History and Literature at Harvard, is author of Party Ideology and Popular Politics at the Accession of George III (1976).

J. H. PLUMB, Master of Christ's College, Cambridge, is author of the now-classic text England in the 18th Century.
