We can approach 'antique' objects, and antique bronzes, in the Ming context from another angle, by looking at some of the contexts in which they were deployed. One of the most striking facts which emerges from texts about elegant interior design at the period is that ancient objects were more than occasionally 'used' in a functional as well as a rhetorical or social sense . Zhang Dai tells the story of the chance excavation of the tomb of the Bronze-age magnate Duke J ing of Qi, which yielded three plain bronze platters and two great decorated vases, which he describes as 'archetypal pieces of the Three Ages'. He concludes, 'I hear they are now in someone's ancestral temple in Anhui province.
