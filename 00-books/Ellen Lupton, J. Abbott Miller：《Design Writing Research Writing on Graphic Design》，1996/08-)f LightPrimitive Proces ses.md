The work of Ed Fella has broadly influenced recent developments in type design. Fella's posters for the Detroit Focus Gallery, produced between 1987 and 1990, feature damaged and defective forms—from thirdgeneration photocopies to broken pieces of transfer type. These imperfect elements are meticulously assembled by hand into freeform compositions. Fella's experiments inspired other designers to construct digital fonts with battered features and hybrid origins. Collection Cooper-Hewitt, National Design Museum.
