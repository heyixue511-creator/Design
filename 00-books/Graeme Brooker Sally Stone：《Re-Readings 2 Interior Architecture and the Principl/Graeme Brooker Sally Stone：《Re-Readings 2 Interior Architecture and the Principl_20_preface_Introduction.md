## Introduction

Often described as the details of making interior space, tactics is a term we use to describe a more substantial way of creating particular identities utilising a number of spatial devices. These instruments, used in the construction of interior space, range from the everyday elements that designers are familiar with, such as floors, walls and ceilings, through to more esoteric materials such as light, surface and movement. All of these elements of space may be utilised by the designer in a variety of ways in every project. This is in order to create a very distinct and particularised set of environments for a variety of different uses, often for numerous occupants, some of which will not even be known.

The six main tactics outlined in this book are: Plane (façade, walls, floor and soffit), Object, Light (artificial and natural), Surface, Openings, and Movement. It is no coincidence that these components form what could be considered one of the basic mechanisms of the interior: the room. We consider the room to be a mechanism as it can be considered as the constructed entity of a series of systems, procedures, materials, structures and devices, all of which will be both physical and cerebral. The tactics that we describe in this chapter are the basic elements that form the constructed room, an assembled enclosure that directs our behaviours in both subtle nuanced fashions as well as in instructed

Tactics are the complicit elements that form methods for the way that people behave inside interior space. In a particular room the way any element is arranged, deployed and detailed will invite the occupants of the space to act in a very particular manner. For instance a handrail, or the balustrade of a landing or walkway, will impact upon the occupant of the room, in a very specific way. Its shape and form, its material condition, the messages that it conveys, can invite the hand for its use in a certain way, and in turn steady its user as they move vertically between spaces, or as they stand on the edge of a void. A stair, detailed in a particular material and organised in a particular manner will not only lend a visual image to a space, it will also create an internal logic and rhythm for its user as they move between floors.

When orchestrated all together, the effect of a series of well-thought out and highly organised tactics of the interior can lead to space that is enduring. Their nuanced coordination ensures an unforgettable interior experience, where light, movement, surface, planes, objects and openings are combined to create memorable and extraordinary interior space.
