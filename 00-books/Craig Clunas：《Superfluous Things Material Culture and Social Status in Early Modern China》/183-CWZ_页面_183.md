His work is worth preserving more for the noble nature of his lineage and for his own martyr's death (since by now the Manchu emperors lauded as paragons of loyalty those who had given their lives to oppose the conquest), rather than for its intrinsic value. This very effeteness and unfitness of the Ming elite, of which Wen Zhenheng was such an archetypal representative, in fact became an important plank of Qing state ideology; as in an extension of the venerable 'Mandate of Heaven' theory of state decline, it was argued that not j ust the Ming ruling house but the entire Han Chinese ruling class had lost the legitimate mandate through factionalism, internal dissension and an unseemly degree of attention to selfish aesthetic gratification at the expense of practical affairs.
