# 70s METALWORK & Under the leadership of Robert Goodden, the Department of Silversmithing and Jewellery was intensely skill orientated. The main aim of the course was the art and craft of gold and silversmithing', in addition to related work in other materials. Students produced a variety of objects, ranging from tea sets and cutlery to home ornaments and jewellery. The late 60s and 70s saw the rise of the artist-craftsperson, in which traditional concepts and forms of metalwork and jewellery were redefined, and ideas were prioritized over the inherent value of materials. Characteristic of the mid-70s was an interest in using unorthodox, nonprecious metals and materials, such as paper, glass, wood and plastic, and in experimenting with colour, for example in Eric Spiller's work. Industrially developed materials, such as titanium, also found favour with RCA students, as seen in the jewellery of Edward de Large, Ann Marie Shillito and James Ward.
