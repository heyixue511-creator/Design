The same year he arrived in Norway he began designing glassware for the Christiania Glasmagasin in Oslo. From 1892 to 1910 he designed metalware embellished with decorative enameling for the David-Andersen company in Oslo, such as his exquisite Dragonfly bowl (1908). Gaudernack's designs epitomized the Art Nouveau style with their radiant enameling and sinuous and stylized forms derived from both flora and fauna. He received widespread acclaim for the skilful execution and imaginative style of his delicate yet sumptuous designs, which rivalled any work emanating from the main centres of Art Nouveau such as Brussels or Nancy. In acknowledgement of this, Gaudernack was awarded a silver medal at the 1900 Paris “Exposition Universelle et Internationale” and (with David-Andersen) a Grand Prize at the 1904 “Louisiana Purchase Exposition” in St. Louis. In 1910, he established his own workshop in Oslo and continued to design and produce objects using silver, filigree and enamel. Two yearslater, he became the first principal instructor for goldsmithing and enamelling at the Statens Håndverks- og Kunstindustriskole (National College of Art and Design).
