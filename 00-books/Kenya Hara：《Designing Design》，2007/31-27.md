![](images/428204ee3d448ced4b6ab82c9af393c326bac790beb2f2dbca4ef490e561648f.jpg) The photo shows Ban's re-design of toilet paper. The square paper tube forms the core of the roll. Because the core is square, the paper rolled upon it ends up shaped like a square as well. Placed into the dispenser and pulled out for use, the square roll resists, with a clunky sound of “kata-katakata." The conventional round roll, on the other hand, moves along smoothly, lightly pulled to the refrain of“suuh-suuh-suuh." The traditional design gives you more paper than you need. The square toilet paper, on the other hand, generates resistance, functioning to reduce the consumption of resources and also deliver the message: economize. ![](images/bf43cc9bfbeeebb08ee94b05fdf7403b57f39f110a5558d455c161350fbffd6a.jpg)
