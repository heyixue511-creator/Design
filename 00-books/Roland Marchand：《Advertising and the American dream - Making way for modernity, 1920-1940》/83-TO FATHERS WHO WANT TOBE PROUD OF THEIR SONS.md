Even attempted smiles on the part of proud but anxious fathers could be distorted almost into grimaces by clenched-fist determination. “Do you want your son to be keen-witted, quick-thinking, resourceful—or don't you care?" asked a Lionel Electric Train ad. The tone of voice was borrowed directly from the ad men's own “locker-room" harangues against “quitters." Obliquely alluding to depression circumstances, the Lionel ad reminded each father, “A lot depends on you. . . . Do all you can now to keep life from giving him an ugly break."
