## Sculpture The Italian Antonio Canova, working for an international clientele, flattered Napoleon with grand, ideal nude (now in the courtyard of the Brera Museum), and in a memorable image of Napoleon’s sister, Pauline Borghese. The seminude Pauline with Grecian profile reminiscent of a Venus, is recumbent on classicalrevival furniture copied from ancient Roman design. The Englishman Joseph Wilton and the American Horatio Greenough contributed in their own ways to the same rediscovery of the ideals of classical sculpture, a movement that started as a slight on the frivolities of the Rococo and ended as one of the continuing revival movements of the nineteenth century. The international quality of the style is shown in the works of Bertel Thorvaldsen, who, like most fellow neoclassical painters and sculptors of the time, rendered his subjects with gentle emotional sentiment and soft surface texture. Whether fairly or unfairly, the nineteenth century is not usually seen as a great century of sculpture.
