The famous Victor trademark showing a fox terrier listening intently to "His Master's Voice" was designed by Francis Barraud, an English artist. The original painting depicted Edison's cylinder machine. After the Edison company turned it down, Barraud sold the idea in 1899 to the Gram-a-phone company and painted in its Berliner disk machine.
