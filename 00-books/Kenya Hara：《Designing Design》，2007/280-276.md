Later, led by Sen-no-Rikyu (1522-1591), the space as well as the utensils and manners of the tea ceremony were pushed to an extreme: profound simplicity and silence. This is a simplicity that induces an image. The communication method of expressing ultimate simplicity and calling into it an image or a meaning is the basis of the concept of emptiness that I described in the horizon section. Exchanging images via the medium of emptiness is a characteristic common to all art forms of the time, from the religious thought of Zen to the lyrical Noh drama established around the time of the tea ceremony, but in the birth of the tea ceremony this traitis particularly striking.
