The American expatriate writers Gertrude Stein and Natalie Barney were admirers of her work. Somerset Maugham, Wyndham Lewis, and the "black magician” Aleister Crowley were among her friends and suitors. Her striking beauty. captured by Berenice Abbott in memorable photographs, led her into other tumultuous relationships, notably with the architect Jean Badovici, for whom she designed a bachelor apartment and for whom she built the first of her two houses. –At the end of World War II. she found the houses she had designed vandalized, and her furniture, her sketchbooks and plans destroyed. With the energy of a person half her age, she resumed designing projects–for a prefabricated cultural center and a vacation complex—and built a new home in the south of France. But her international fame had faded, and she spent her last years in solitude, almost forgotten. –It is Peter Adams achievement in this engrossing biography to recreate the worlds Eileen lived in, from the fin de siècle to the late twentieth century, from the convivial times shared with her famous—and eceentrie—friends in the Art Deco period to the stark. lonely years spent working out plans for pioneering modern houses. Adam traces the evolution of her theories in drawings and important articles, translating and reproducing much work never published before or largely inaccessible. He also provides a catalogue raisonné of all her known furniture–a necessity for collectors and lovers of Art Deco at its nest. –Peter Adamn has won many awards for his BBC-TV films on Richard Strauss, David Hockney.
