We thought sunglasses were the perfect resorttool. This project has a similar vision. The photos of the security guard, the corporate receptionist, or the salaried worker on the footbridge, each holding a soft-serve cone, precisely reflect the reality in which “resort" becomes incarnate in the ordinary. This team's research first pursued the question of how to design the creamy, semisolid substance known as soft ice cream. In analyzing the production method, the group discovered the mechanism that creates the ice cream's shape. They focused on molding the sweet treat based on different nozzle forms and different motions of the hand holding the cone. With a variety of forms and hand motions (both intentional revolving and random movement), they came up with completely innovative soft-cream designs. The ultimate solution was a cone of ice cream in which the shape of the cone on the bottom exactly replicates that of the cream on top. (The convoluted cone is repeated upside down.) It's a design of superb metaphor, leading the ordinary to an ephemeral“resort."
