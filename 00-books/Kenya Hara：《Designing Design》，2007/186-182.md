This is the only project that I know of in which the same design is applied to a shop's card and its exterior walls. With the same dot pattern, the card is like a part of the architecture. And the moment the customer touches it, the image of the exterior walls is called to the tips of his fingers. Shopping bags and giftwrap are two other important media by which to communicate the store's concept to its customers. As anyone who has shopped at a prudently run brand retailer must have learned from the experience, the key to a brand image is careful selection of the quality of the details that touch the body. So we developed a paper that conveys a rich sense of touch to the fingertips (see page 183). The perfected "M wrap" feels very pleasant to the touch. Do you recognize in the paper the pattern of the conceptualmodel? I also designed the line of shopping bags. The photo shows the Matsuya logo in white emerging from a pattern of small pale grey dots on white paper. The same pattern is used for the giftwrap. The material is inexpensive craft paper, but thanks to the dots, it has the perfect texture. Careful readers might have already noticed that this pattern is linked to the dot pattern of the walls.
