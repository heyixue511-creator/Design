![](images/548ec39e045b54a4250decd315935e7a561321708d2ac62084c4efe70df2a3d0.jpg) Gunnar Cyrén, Nobel glassware range for Orrefors, 1991 ▶ Gunnar Cyrén, Pop goblets for Orrefors, 1965–1966 ## gunnar cyrén (6. 931 ävle, Sweden) ![](images/0547daf7b17446c89b264e0eb1bde19eced48f8f23819eb44c7d089302c2260c.jpg) Gunnar Cyrén studied metalwork at the Högre Konstindustriella Skolan (Higher College of Industrial Arts), and also at the Konstfackskolan (University College of Arts, Crafts and Design), Stockholm, receiving journeyman certificates in both goldsmithing and silversmithing in 1951 and 1956 respectively. He also briefly trained at the Cologne Werkschulen in 1954. After the completion of his studies, Cyrén began working in 1959 as a designer for the Orrefors glassworks. In contrast to the prevailing Scandinavian taste for rusticity in the early 1960s, Cyrén designed glassware that was distinguished by its precision, refinement and use of virtuoso craft skills. This approach to design is exemplified by his elegant overlaid glass bowl decorated with bear motifs (now in the Nationalmuseum, Stockholm). Cyrén's later iconoclastic Pop goblets (1965–1966) with their colourfully striped stems and opalescent bowls were similarly radical, yet perfectly encapsulated the joyful and youthful exuberance of the period. For Cyrén, design has always been a very private activity and his work often displays “a personal touch” that has on occasions been at odds with the constraints of industrial mass production. Designing either costly one-off pieces (using Graa/ technigues and decorative cutting) or glassware for small-scale serial production, Cyrén has concerned himself with obiects that not only function well, but also exhibit a "charming" personality. In 1966 Cyrén was awarded the Lunning Prize and during the 1970s began designing for Dansk International Designs and the Gefle Porslinsfabrik. In the early 1970s he also began designing glassware for industrial production and from the mid-1970s worked as a silversmith, at times combining both glass and silver in a single obiect. During the 1980s his glassware designs continued to display the restraint and elegance for which he had become so well known. In 1988 he received the Prince Eugen Medal and in 1991 he contributed glass tableware (with green, amber or gold coloured stems) to the specially commissioned Nobe/ service, which commemorated the 90th anniversary of the Nobel Foundation. Today, as “the innovative grand old man of glass”, Cyrén continues to design original and beautiful glassware for the Orrefors glassworks. ![](images/0b45d1e83886a6d3a91b22021212b71d3a368e8f1bd7128bbbbde384cfc8a3ee.jpg)
