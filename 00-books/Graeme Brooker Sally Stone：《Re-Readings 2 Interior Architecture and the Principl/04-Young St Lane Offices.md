# Young St Lane Offi ces

Location Edinburgh, Scotland

Former function Mews houses and Lawyers’ offi ces

New function Commercial offi ce space

Designer Malcolm Fraser Architects

Remodelled 2008

A substantial building may not require significant remodelling if the structure is still intact and the new function will fi t relatively easily into the existing container of the walls. The adaptation may be an ingenious collection of quite modest and unpretentious moves that use the structural integrity of the existing to support the new elements.

Malcolm Fraser Architects adapted three derelict mews buildings and a Victorian lawyers’ offi ce in Edinburgh into light and spacious open-plan offi ces by adding a series of new metal roofs. The original Craigleith stone walls of the buildings, from which much of Edinburgh was constructed, had great inherent strength. These were

cleaned and new large paned windows were inserted into the existing openings; the deep plan of the new interior was provided with plenty of natural light and ventilation.

However, the fl oor-to-ceiling heights of the original mews cottages were woefully inadequate for 21st century use. Contemporary offi ce space needs provision for under-fl oor cabling, controlled lighting and suffi cient ceiling height to ensure that the open space of the deep plan is comfortable. The required space was achieved fl oating a new roof over all of the structures. This served to tie them together, thus creating a single integrated entity which not only facilitated the offi ce

space but also modestly signalled the change of use. The new roof was constructed from grey zinc, perfectly refl ecting the predominant colours of the area. The vertical circulation was placed in a new glazed structure that was attached to the rear of the building. This timber and steel staircase tied the different levels together. A small and contained courtyard garden, accessed by a stone staircase, completed the project.

This is a simple project that unassumingly uses the natural qualities of the buildings and the area to facilitate the needs of new users.
