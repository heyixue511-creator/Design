## I would like my book to be read on four levels:

In my“Notes” to the practical exercises, I describe their background, their historical origins, their present-day implication and intention. I then describe the“Exercises” that I havedeveloped and found useful and prototypical. They are the result of my methodology and the core of my book. In the chapter “Approaches”I aim to give pedagogical hints to support the teaching and with occasional “Quotations“, set in italics, I describe the students reactions. With my appendix “For further reading” I want to encourage and stimulate further interest and research with a commented bibliography.

Mybook is to be read visually. With its many illustrations, it is very personal, useful, practical, and Ihope, lively.Should you think thatthe described basic exercises arevery simple, you are right. But my experience tells methat it its better to tackle a simple problem in depth than a complex one superficially. I hope that the reader will recognize that it can be read on different levels and point out directions for pedagogical ideas andhistorical research. Should you think that my English language is modest, you are right. It is not my mother tongue but I use it for international understanding and do not hide my German origins. I want it to be published in China-in English and Chinese-andwould be pleased if it could find interest outside Chína as well.

But first of all I dedicate my book to my many friends in China who have encouraged me to write this accountof my activities.   
Klaus Lehmann

# What students in China ask me

Chinese people are very proud of their traditions. They can be proud of achievements that havedominated Asian life and culture for centuries. Confucius, more than 2000 years ago, named the ethical standards that still govern Asian life today. Confucianism today is supported byofficial politics and Confucius Institutions are being established in an effort to establish a Chinese identity. China is in the wake of industrial success and thepolitical ambition to become a prominent member of the political and industrial world. It was Deng Xiaoping who opened China after years of isolation, opening it to the western world and stimulating a new era of industrial progress. He put China on the world map again. Western civilisation has since swept into China, rousing expectations and creating opportunities, but at the same time causing irritations and problems of identity for the Chinese.

In my activities in China I have always stressed the fact that I can merely report what has been going on in Europe and try to explain the ideas behind these events. I have also warned my audience not to adopt Western thinking and behaviour uncritically. This I consider would represent an attempt to curry favour with Western values, in a wayself-imposed colonialism. It would bea poor sign for Chinese culture, which has after dark ages of isolation, managed to appear on the world map again and must be considered a great achievement.

Their concern. It is the ever-recurring question of how to marry Western technologyand Chinese values-a general, imprecise, but a very emotionally perceived problem. The official attitude-Western technology and Chinese values-is too simple: the Chinese have already adopted Western values just as the Western world has reflected and now is learning from Asian thinking. The integration of practical achievements,technology, style, or industrial culture always involves values, which are the driving forces behind it. Technological achievements and values are interdependent and cannot be separated.

中国人对自己的传统感到十分自豪。他们引以为豪的成就，几个世纪以来对整个亚洲的生活方式和文化都产生了巨大的影响。两千多年前孔子的道德标准与规范至今仍然影响着亚洲人的生活。时至今日，孔子的“儒家思想”已经获得了官方的支持，而在世界各地建立的孔子学院则旨在建立中国的身份认同感。因为中国的工业发展已经取得了巨大成就，而且中国目前正致力于成为世界政坛与经济领域的强国。在与外界隔绝多年后，邓小平开始了改革开放，并开启了一个工业大发展的新时代，是他把中国重新带上了世界的舞台。西方文明从那时起，开始风靡中国大地，带来了期望、创造了机会，然而同时，也带来了中国人的烦躁不安，以及对自我身份认同感的怀疑。

在中国做讲座的时间里，我一直强调一个事实:我只能向大家介绍欧洲所发生的事情，并解释这些事情背后所蕴含的意义，仅此而已。同时我也警告听众们不要不加批判地全盘接受西方的思维和行为方式。关于这一点，我可能是代表了一种以自愿接受的殖民主义视角，对西方价值观进行阿谀奉承的尝试。这对在经历了多年的与世隔绝后，力图重新与世界接轨，并创造新成就的中国文化来说，并不是一件好事。

对于他们来说，如何将西方的科技与中国的价值观结合起来，是一个经常需要面对的、无法回避的问题，也是一个普遍的、不精确的，但却深植于人们情感之中的问题。官方对这个问题的态度似乎非常简单：中国人目前已经接受了西方价值观，就像西方世界正在学习亚洲人的思维方式一样。实践成就、技术、风格或工业文化的整合总是要包含价值观的融合，而它又是所有结合过程的幕后推手。技术的成就和价值观是相互依存、不可分割的。

在讲座中我让中国听众们客观地评估西方价值观中有什么适合中

In my lectures I have left it to my Chinese audience to evaluate critically what is suitable for their own culture, encouraging the young generation to find a Chinese way and telling them that it would be their challenge to master it. On second thoughts-and this occupies my thinking-I found this insufficient and therefore have added a few reflections.

The transformation of traditional cultural values into a modern industrial society that wants to claim its position in a global context, needs, I feel, an acknowledgement of one's own culture-of what I am, of what has left its prints on me, of what has coined my thinking and what governs my life today. Identity is at the bottom of it all.

Identity must be redefined, not within one's own culture but in relation to competing cultures, reflecting what others think aboutyou, how they see you, and whatthey expect from you. It should sharpen what is original, what is, in commercial terms, “the unique selling point” of Chinese culture in the global competition ofcultural values.

Identity needs self-awareness.You cannot get it through your own efforts; just as in personal life, you need appreciation from outside to develop it. In the search for oneself the individual always finds himself“in a social context” according to the American sociologist Amitai Etzioni, and this can also be said about national identities.China will, so I hope, carefully watch how the world sees it from outside and the world willreadjust its picture of China. Is identity, I ask, the iconography of Chinese symbols in Chinese writing, the cloud pattern on the Olympic torch, the calligraphy of its past masters on modern posters, traditional Chinese porcelain copies, Xi Ching teapots, lacquer work, theacrobats, or the Pekingopera? Yes, it is all of these-but it is not. I feel the Chinese have plundered their past by merely copying and turned this so often into what is surely no more than airport art. China has not, unlike Japan, managedto give its past a modern expression. China has developed industrially in a breathtaking way and faces a fascinating challenge to master the situation culturally.

The architectural highlights that China is proud to show to the western worldare mostly designed by Western architects. I can see no sign yet of an originalChinese architecture that is up to international standing. I put it down to国文化的，鼓励年青一代的中国人去寻找具有中国特点的发展道路，并告诉他们坚守这条道路对他们来说，是一种巨大的挑战。事后我又想了想，感觉只有这些并不够，因此我决定再增加一些自己的新思考。

在如今的工业化时代，传统文化价值的转变需要在全球化的背景下宣扬自己的位置。我认为这种转变是对一个人所属文化的一种致意，它体现出身份、文化信息在这个人身上留下的烙印所包含的意义，以及制约个人想法和指导个人生活的因素。而身份则是所有因素立足的基础。

身份必须要重新定义，而且不能被限制在自己的文化之内，需要与其他文化建立联系，并反映其他人是如何理解一个人、如何看待你，以及他们期待从你身上得到什么。它需要明确什么是原创的，并且在文化价值的全球竞争中，什么是一个人的“独特卖点”(商业术语)。

身份需要一种自我意识，但只通过自己的努力也不能获得。这就像在个人生活中，一个人需要外界的肯定和欣赏，才能获得自我意识。美国社会学家埃米泰·埃兹奥尼说，在寻找自我的过程中，一个人总是感觉自己处在社会化的环境之中。而这也适用于国家认同的过程。因此我希望中国能认真观察世界是如何看待自己的，世界也会以全新的视角来审视中国。我想问：识别，就像中国书写中符号化的汉字、奥运火炬上的祥云、现代海报上古代名家的书法、中国西秦的茶壶、紫砂茶壶、漆器制品、杂技或是京剧一样，是中国象征的一种表现形式吗？是的，表面上是这样，但实际上不是。我感觉中国人仅仅通过复制掠夺了自己国家的传统元素，并把它们变成了一种庸俗不堪的“机场艺术”。与日本不同，中国想要以现代的表达方式展现自己的历史与传统。中国的工业发展非常迅速，然而其面临的保持文化的完整与独立性的挑战，也是前所未有。

中国人向西方世界展示的现代建筑大多都是由西方建筑师所设计。到目前为止，我还没有发现中国有任何原创的建筑在国际社会上崭露头角。我将在随后的独立章节中详细说明本人对建筑的意义以及fundamental attitudes about the meaning of architectureand its aestheticalimplications-a chapter by itself, and a challenge too.

I can, I admit, offer no shortcut and know that it will be a process that will need time, patience, and careful methods of cultural analysis. It will need a dialogue, a culture of discussion, and an intercultural exchange.

I would like my critical notes to be understood as a personal and constructive contribution to an ongoing cultural process in China, an appealto the many students I have had the privilege to meet and teach. I take their “everrecurring questions”very seriously and answer them with a philosophical note on cultural exchange.

But I am sure that if Chinese design wants to be more than a marketing instrument it needs a strong cultural foundation. Without it Chinese design will remain a regional fashion.

其美学内涵的基本态度，换句话说，这也是一种挑战。

对此，我承认不能提供任何的捷径，我也清楚，实现这一点需要时间、耐心和有效而细致的文化分析方法，同时还需要对话、关于文化的讨论，以及文化间的交流。

我想让我的客观性批判言论对中国目前的文化发展做出个人的建设性的贡献，使那些我有幸见到并传授知识的众多学生们感兴趣，并得到启发。我十分重视他们所提出的“持续不断发生的问题”，使用了文化交流中的哲学注释来回答每一个问题。

不过我确信，如果中国的设计事业想要成为市场营销的手段，就一定需要一个强大的文化基础。如果没有这个基础，中国的设计就不会被世界接受，只能局限在有限的区域之内。

# Cultural differences-the way we think and act

## How I came to think about this

When I began teaching in China in about 1985, it was an adventure for me. I was unprepared, loaded with western ideas, filled with curiosity and admiration for an old culture that has survived whilst other classical cultures like the Greek or Roman have vanished. I was filled with many prejudices too, but had an open mind. In thecourse of my first teachings I found that Chinese students reacted differently to what I had experienced compared with students in western societies. That puzzled me. I began to think and found that it had to do with history, culture, social context, perception, and language. Now I know that the fundamental way of processing information is not universal but depends on culture, and that the need for research into cultural acting and behaviour becomes obvious.(Ulrich Kühnen)

Naturally| was eager to make myself understood, first by discussing the matter with my Chinese friends and colleagues, but that was helpful only in a minor sense. I found I had to go back to the formative factors of eastern civilisation and eastern thinking. So it turned out to become a cross-cultural study that has occupied my thoughts since then.

I now know that I am only dabbling in this matter, realizing that the more I know, the more I don't know. And each timeI return from China and my efforts at cross-cultural understanding, I am filled with new questions. I had to learn that the critical dialogue, a basic element in Western thinking, is not considered a virtue in China, but a serious danger to social harmony and cultural reception. It is not only a matter of content, but a matter of procedure. It is about what other people expect from youand how to approach the partner you want to communicate with. It has explained to me the hesitation of Chinese students entering a critical discussion, their lack of a challengingattitude toward authority (Jin Li) emphasized bythe Chinese tradition of respect for the teacher, whom you are never expected to criticise.

My findings arevery cryptic and personal but they have led me to change my teaching approach. But I want to describe them in the hope that theyfoster mutual understanding in the process of educational exchange. Ialso want to encourage students who have studied both in China and Western countries to be aware of their chance and challenge; being familiar with two cultures, they are of great value in a global society. They are the transmitters of cultural exchange and learning and therefore of inestimable importance to us all.

## Different approaches to education

In China there is a long tradition of learning and there is hardly a country in which education is valued more highly. I know of family efforts being made nowadays to get the son or daughter to a renowned university. But the methods of education are different. In Western societies education emphasises the freedom of the individual to act and think for himself and to be responsible for that. In eastern societies, education consists of learning abouta complex social network, its morals and behaviours holistically, always geared tothe community and its harmony. In Western thinking dialogue and experiment are the motive force, the method of learning, and therefore the model of Western teaching. In Asian societies critical dialogue is avoided for fear of disturbing social harmony.
