What about macaroni then? After the grain is reduced to powder, this kind of pasta is shaped into manmade forms. Macaroni is an object that has already undergone some preparation, so because we're just giving form to a powdered material, it doesn't really matter what shape we give it. Therefore, the process of shaping it can be called design, right? But the macaroni shapes we’re all used to have not deviated from their traditional forms, nor do they seem to have gone through frequent style changes as have cars and clothing. Why?Because in shaping macaroni, there must be an interesting interface design that unites the act of eating and the raw materials for food. Is the designer present in the process?
