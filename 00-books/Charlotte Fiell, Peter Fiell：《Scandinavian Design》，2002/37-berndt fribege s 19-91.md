Berndt Friberg, stoneware vases and bowl forGustavsberg with "hare's fur" glaze, 1964, c. 1961, 1959, 1978& 1963 ![](images/3237e57907a8b4cb210d220c32ffa6e97d91bbd5ac7627e94567cb3f093549c4.jpg) > Berndt Friberg, stoneware vase for Gustavsberg, 1954 # berndt fribergSwedish 1999-1981) The Swedish ceramicist Berndt Friberg apprenticed as a thrower at the Höganäs-Billesholms stoneware factory from 1915 to 1918. He also studied at the technical school in Höganäs and from 1918 trained at various ceramics workshops in Denmark and Sweden. In 1934 he joined the Gustavsberg ceramics factory, where for the next decade heworked as a thrower for Wilhelm Kåge. In 1944 he became a member of the Gustavsberg Studio and subsequently designed his own ceramics for the company until his death in 1981. During this period he produced numerous one-off stoneware vessels that were distinguished by their delicately coloured – soft greens, pale ochres, dusky blues – Oriental-style semimatt “hare's fur” glazes. The forms were influenced by those of earlier Far Eastern wares. Although inspired by Oriental antecedents, Friberg's work was typically Scandinavian in its refinement – colour, texture and form were carefully balanced in order to realize objects that had a compelling natural beauty. His hand-thrown ceramic designs produced at the Gustavsberg Studio were awarded three gold medals at the 1947(VIII), 1951 (IX) and 1954(X) Milan Triennale and the Gregor Paulsson Trophy in 1960. His work was also shown on numerous occasions at Nordiska Kompaniet as well as other exhibitions held in Stockholm, Gothenburg, Karlstad, Höganäs, Malmö, and Växjö. Like his mentor Wilhelm Kåge, Friberg produced stoneware pottery that exemplified the superlative quality of Scandinavian craftsmanship. With his sensitive handling of materials and profound understanding of their formal and aesthetic potential, Friberg set an important benchmark in the design and composition of studio ceramics. ![](images/ce9ee96990d606243c1233aeea7ad0c91a0077ee39def19e8c618778cca44fcc.jpg)
