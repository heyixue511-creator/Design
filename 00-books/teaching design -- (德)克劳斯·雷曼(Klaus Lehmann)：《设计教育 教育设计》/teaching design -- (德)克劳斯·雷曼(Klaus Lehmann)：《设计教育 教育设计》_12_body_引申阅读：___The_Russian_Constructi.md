## The Russian Constructivists-discovering the symbolic power of construction

In the short but influential movement of Russian Constructivism between 1920 and 1927 Rodchenko played a key role. He was a member of theprerevolutionary avant-gardeand after the 1918 revolution saw the opportunity for building the environment for a new socialist society. Though the goals were not very clear, the vigorous theoretical discussions between the Productivists, who considered construction as a more technical term related to engineering, and those for whom construction was an artistic term became an interesting issue. The radicals advised the artist to enter a polytechnic institution in order to be useful in industry. Rodchenko opposed this by stating that artists, in contrast to engineers, “know how to see it”and gave the“artist-constructor” a new dimension.

Heconsidered his abstract and experimental work as a research into material, facture, colour, space, time, form, and technique. Rodchenko must have been a gifted teacher and, in modern terms, a universal designer, pioneering photography and typography and inspiringhis students towork towards the goal of a proletarian society. Though few of his designs have been massproduced, their morals make his work authentic. I know that by incorporating Rodchenko into my essay on construction I am abit off the road, for Russian Constructivism is an artistic movement, driven by the fascination with engineering and technology. His work has had great symbolic power-its semantics far surpassing its utility.
