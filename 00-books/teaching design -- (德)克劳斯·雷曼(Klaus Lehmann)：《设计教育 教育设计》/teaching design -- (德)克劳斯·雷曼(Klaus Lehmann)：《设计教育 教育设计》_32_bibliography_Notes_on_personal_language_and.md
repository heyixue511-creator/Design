# Notes on personal language and style

When young people decide to study design, they usually have been drawn to it by something that has impressed them, bysomebody who encouraged them, or by discovering what they think their own talents are and wanting to develop them. That is all legitimate, but their knowledge of what design is or what designers do, is often very rudimentary or even nonexistent.

They know that design is about products or buildings or books or posters and have already preconceived pictures in their mind. They expect that when studying design they will get rules and recipes for their professional work, not knowing that they will have to study, select, and evaluate what is offered to them, and turn it into a new and personal experience. When they start studying, they are already loaded with pictures, preconceived ideas, views, attitudes, and a closed set of conventions that prevent them from develop creativity and opening themselves to new directions.

Bauhaus teachers thus wanted to empty the students' minds of their preconceived imagery and devised a foundation course to provoke and radically rid the student of his conventions and open his mind for new experiences.

That, I think, must remain a pedagogical goal today. But the Bauhaus Foundation Course, which, after emerging in Germany, was taken to the USA and Japan, and later spread around the World, needs both continuity and a present day interpretation. This was my intention and the purpose of my practical exercises, which are the result of myexperience in the teaching of design.

The following exercise has a different background, far from Bauhaus teaching, which pursued a universal and anonymous language in design. It is intended to provoke the student to look and study the visual language of designers and architects who have left their mark on design history.

当一些年轻人决定学习设计之时，他们通常都是被一些令他们印象深刻的东西所吸引住了，或者被他人激励了，又或者发现自己的才能之所在，并决定继续发展下去，才会做出这样的决定，这些都是合情合理的。然而他们对于什么是设计，或者设计师具体是做什么的，通常是一知半解，甚至是毫无概念。

他们都清楚，设计是关于产品、建筑、书籍或海报等方面，而且都在脑海中构思过设计的轮廓。当他们期待学习设计时，他们会得到从事专业工作的法则和秘诀，殊不知他们必须静下心来学习、选择和评价自己所面对的一切，然后必须把它们得到的东西转化为全新的、属于个人的经验。当他们开始研究时，他们都已经被各种图例、前人所谓的点子与经验、态度，和封闭不前的传统规约压得喘不过气来，这些都成为了他们发展创新性和拓展新方向的拦路虎。

因此，包豪斯学校的老师们把学生们脑中既定的思维模式全部清空，并构思出一种全新的基础课程，来激发学生们寻求新体验的灵感，帮助他们尽快摆脱传统规约的束缚。

关于这一点，我想包豪斯的教学理念在今天依旧是富于实用性的。包豪斯基础课程发源于德国，但是后来在美国和日本得到应用，进而推广至全世界。然而我认为，虽然它应该继续得到推介，但是在今天的时代背景下，它也需要全新的解读与改良。这就是我编写这些实践性练习的初衷和目的。这些练习都是基于我多年来教授设计课程的经验而作。

接下来的这个练习，有着与包豪斯的教学方法(追求设计中的一种普世而无名的表达方式)完全不同的背景。该练习旨在激发学生观察并研究设计史上成就斐然的设计师和建筑师的视觉语言。

Famous architects and designers in the past have always been recognized for the personal language in their work. This recognition has been the result of exceptional achievements that exceeded the stylistic canon of their time.

At the beginning of the 20th century, design and architecture were striving for a universal and non-subjective international visual language. In the twenties the designers wanted to retreat behind their work and let the products speak for themselves. The study of everyday culture, freed from personal ambitions, began and left its mark on design thinking at the beginning of the 20th century

Today there is a striving for individualism that has changed the ideological landscape in the field of design yet again. At the moment, designers’ names (the famous ones at least) have become household words that can be successfully marketed. The buyer follows and supports this by going in for well known brands and designs and this has led to an overvaluation of brand names and a regrettable neglect of anonymous and serious design work

Personal language, generally accompanied by ambition and often with vanity, has become part of European design ideology and is a matter of permanent díscussion.

To enlarge your personal repertoire I have found that it is helpful to study the great masters, not for the sake of copying, but in order to understand the thoughts behind their work and to discover their stylistic mastery.

历史上家喻户晓的建筑师和设计师都以其在作品中体现的富有个人色彩的“语言”而被人熟知。而他们的作品之所以取得了杰出成就，就是因为其风格大大逾越了他们所处时代的所谓“标准”。在20世纪初期，设计和建筑领域都在寻求一种普世、非主观且国际化的视觉语言表达方式。

到了20年代，设计师们开始想从自己的作品中“隐退”，让产品自己做自己的“代言人”。20世纪初，抛弃个人主观意识，对日常的普通文化进行研究开始在设计圈内流行起来，并在设计思维领域成功占有一席之地。

时至今日，对个人主义的推崇，再一次改变了设计界原本的意识形态土壤。现在，设计师的名字(至少是那些知名人士的)已经变得家喻户晓了，并且拥有很高的市场宣传价值。消费者对此趋之若骛，乐于接受名牌的产品和设计，而这就会导致对名牌商品本身的价值估计过高，从而遗憾地忽视了那些无名的或较为严肃的设计作品。

个人语言通常都伴随个人意愿，也往往与自负和虚荣为伍。它已经成为欧洲设计理念的一部分，并成为设计圈内永恒的研讨话题。

为了扩展你的知识储备，我觉得向那些大师们学习是非常有帮助的，不是为了再复制，而是为了理解他们作品背后的思想，以及探索他们作品风格的秘密。
