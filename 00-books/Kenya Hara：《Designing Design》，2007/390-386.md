![](images/6791243e83098f1e73248d9b9a4b6c6c00a7802dd8da28e7af6c9f9a4b7d2c7a.jpg) Footprint Landscape—Stepping on the Shimanto River Produced by Kyoko Nakamura, Kazuko Nomoto and Kaori Hashimoto This team documented the Shimanto River viewed through footprint-shaped windows. They photographed the ground-level Shimanto River landscape through a full-scale footprint-shaped mask of black acrylic. By reconfiguring the photographs of a tremendous number of footprint-shaped ground surfaces, they present the full range of sensory experiences available to us at the riverside. The ground is cut into the shape of a footprint because, although the visual representation is done with photographs, this team's approach is to stimulate, through the sense of sight, the tactile perception one senses from the ground when walking barefoot. When we see an image of a fish exhibited in an ordinary square-framed photograph, we concentrate only on the fish itself.
