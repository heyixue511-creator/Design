![](images/62341c0d212e82ec3087f7faf67bc2abe5b1cd4b83b9f5c09847e73fe8e3c648.jpg) Tokyo, Japan—April2000 ## The RE-DESIGN Exhibition Travels the World To conclude the story of the re-design solutions, I don't think it's necessary to interpret all 32 subjects and their solutions. I believe the reader has fully understood the project's goals through the several examples introduced already. Our daily surroundings seem completely wrapped in design: our floors, walls, TV monitors, CDs, books, beer bottles, lighting appliances, bathrobes, coasters, and so on and so forth. All are the outcome of design work. The talent of the designer is to reexamine these daily surroundings at any time, with a fresh eye, as if they were as yet unknown. I used to wonder what kinds of novel things would be created in the 21st century, and what kinds of innovations we'd experience, doubtless one after the other. But today I would say that this very idea should be left behind in the previous century. Instead, a new era should arise, as the ordinary that we think we know so well is made unknown, one object after another.
