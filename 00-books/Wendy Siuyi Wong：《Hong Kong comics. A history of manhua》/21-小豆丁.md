Chu-yi Sing Comics Collection, 1988 Editor: Dong Pui-sun

A famous businessman, Yau Da-shing, inspired the creation of Chu-yi Sing, the main charecter of this comic. Although it originally related the good side of businessmen, the focus later shifted to more ordinary, working-class people. Politically, the comic supported common citizens and included mild critiques of social inequality.

Manhua Hard and Soft Angel Theater, 1989 Art: Ho Kin-chung

Script: Hard and Soft Angel

Hard and Soft Angel were popular radio disc jockeys who had gained attention and an audience with their loud banter and sarcastic jokes. They both had backgrounds in graphic design and friends who were already drawing comics, so their transition to the manhua world was easy.

213-214.

Media Tour of Cahiers Du Cinéma, 1989 Editor: Unknown

This comic book featured work by young, alternative manhua artists—including Ho Ka-chiu, Jek na-si, and TSSW Leung—who wanted to communicate their thoughts and ideas on contemporary life. Graphically, their comics experimented with different styles and formats. Although they started the comic with high hopes, the project was short-lived.

215–217.

One Woman, Three Markets, 1989 Art/Script: Chan Ya

Chan Ya was a newspaper columnist, and this was her first and only attempt to use comics to express her opinions on issues of gender politics. Her strip first appeared in a pro-China newspaper in early 1989. The political climate was relatively liberal at the time, and she was able to criticize the mainland government, especially about the democracy protests in Beijing. The climate shifted drastically after the June 4, 1989, massacre in Tiananmen Square, and the strip ended. The main character was an unattractive woman who made straightforward observations about the world around her. One Woman, Three Markets was a rare attempt by a female artist to produce a comic strip on serious political issues.

218–220.

Three Little Guys, 1990 Art/Script: Tou Sam

Three Little Guys follows the adventures of three naughty boys, led by A-giu, a similar version of whom appears in the artists’ earlier comic One Is Nice. Here, the artist tried to depict the adult world from a kid's perspective.
