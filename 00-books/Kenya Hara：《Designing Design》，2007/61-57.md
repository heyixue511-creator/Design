Tadasu Ohe refuses to design architecture that trumpets its concept, instead exercising his skills by bringing the issues into an elegant, sophisticated space. His macaroni proposal, based on the motif of waves, included three types: ripple, loop, and surf. The photo shows RIPPLE. The ring part maintains the look of ordinary macaroni and the grooves are designedto hold the broth. Kogure's recipe for this macaroni is Ravioli: Fruit of the Sea. Sealed between two layers of wavy pasta is time itself. “Fry mussels, salmon and scallops in butter, steam them in white wine and add egg yolk. Seal the mixture tightly between the waves." As the ravioli hovers between plate and palate, you can imagine the flowing waves of time and the cosmos. According to Ohe, this dish could convince you that there is nothing in the world that follows a straight course.
