The grid that follows presents the data on the surface of the rock; on the sides, the grid is conventional. The two displays compare the effect of religion, taking into account party affiliation, on a person's vote for president in 1956 and in 1960 (when a Catholic ran for president). Note there is no reliable slope associated with religion in 1956, once party is controlled; in 196o, a systematic effect is found. Reading the slopes in the other direction shows the persistent effect of party in both elections: Philip E. Converse, "Religion and Politics: The 1960 Election," in Angus Campbell, Philip E.
