Of the 131 pocket knives that were offered for sale by Montgomery Ward & Co. in 1895, 17 were described as ladies' knives. Although it is unlikely that there was any significant difference in the mode of cutting practised by American men and women, the ladies' knives were all smaller and had pearl or white handles; the men's knives were larger, and many of them had horn handles. The catalogues of nineteenth-century department stores and mail order houses reveal many other examples of designs which distinguished the sex of the user.
