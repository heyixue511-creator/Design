![](images/af59724e7e9ebedb3ed302a095e7b99c72e6ab29433021298af881facfecfca3.jpg) Jacob Jensen, Beosystem 1200 record player for Bang & Olufsen, 1969 # jacob jensen 6 . 192 oehage, Denmark ![](images/e897e8e71d464fd333f60ddbefa109011ed6a598b2145b9861ed5aea30a5b625.jpg) The Danish product designer Jacob Jensen studied industrial design at the Kunsthåndværkerskolen (School of Arts & Crafts), Copenhagen, where he graduated in 1952. Between 1952and 1959, he was the chief designer for the first Danish industrial design consultancy, which had been founded in 1949 bySigvard Bernadotte and Acton Bjørn. From 1959 to 1961 Jensen lived in the USA, where he established a design practice with, among others, Richard Latham (b. 1920). On returning to Copenhagen in 1961, Jensen founded his own industrial design practice, and three years later began designing audio equipment for Bang &Olufsen. In response to the company's mission to “create a European Hi-Fi format which communicates power, precision and identity” Jensen and his assistant David Lewis (b. 1939) introduced the use of linear dial controls to its products that were modelled on a slide-rule and communicated the impression of absolute precision. With the superb clarity of their control layouts and extruded anodized aluminium casings, Jensen's sleek, high-performance designs set the aesthetic and technological standards for audio systems. His Beogram 1200 hi-fi system(1969) was awarded a Danish ID Award for its harmonious balance between “apparatus” and “furniture”, while his Beogram 4000 record player (1972) was notable for incorporating a tangential pick-up arm. Although best known for his pioneering designs for Bang & Olufsen, Jensen has also designed products for other Danish and European manufacturers, including the E76 pushbutton telephone (1972)for Alcatel-Kirk, an office chair(1979)for Labofa, ultrasound scanning equipment (1982) for Bruel & Kjær, and a wristwatch(1988)for Georg Jensen. One of the most famous Scandinavian industrial designers, Jensen has received over one hundred international awards for his innovative work. Believing that, “Design is a language understood by everyone”, Jacob Jensen's revolutionary products, which synthesize functionalism and elegance within a simple yet classical vocabulary of form, have changed forever our ideas of how everyday objects can be experienced.
