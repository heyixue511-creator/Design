# 70s-80s FILM & TV In 1972, Stuart Hood succeeded Keith Lucas as Professor when Lucas became Director of the British Film Institute. Under his Professorship, independent work was prioritized over forging links with industry. Consequently much experimental work emerged from the course at this time. The work produced reflected an interest in political and structural issues and semiotics. However, the course still embraced a wide range of approaches, from straightforward documentary through narrative to abstract film. Although some important advances had been made in terms of equipment, the expense of film production, coupled with inflation, was proving problematic. Richard Guyatt, then Rector, was keen to see the Department return to the craft' of mainstream film. He set up a working party which recommended that an emphasis be placed on practical film, video and animation production.
