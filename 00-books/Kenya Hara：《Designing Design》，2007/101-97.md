Before they started wearing shoes, humans walked barefoot. The sole of the foot acted as an interface with the ground. In those days, through our sensitive feet, we truly picked up a great variety of information from the ground. We might have also learned that certain insects were beneath our feet or that if we dug in a certain spot, we'd find a spring.
