# GAETANO PESCE b.1939 ITALIAN DESIGNER OF PROVOCATIVE FURNITURE SUFFUSED WITH SOCIAL COMMENTARY. Eccentric objects and environments that incorporate lively color, personality, and often social commentary are the stock-in-trade of this designer. His UP5 chair (i969) may have been the first design object with an overt political agenda: its bulbous form suggests a female torso, and the ottoman a ball and chain—imagery suggesting the persecution of women in modern society. Moloch (ı97ı) is an ordinary desk lamp writ large, the grand scale making it into an iconic artwork. Even in his less explicit designs, Pesce always has something to say, since his intentions go beyond the mere creation of aesthetically pleasing objects Though much of his furniture is unorthodox in form, he considers function an essential part of design, saying, "If it is not practical, it is not a service to those who use it ""r, Born in La Spezia, Italy, Pesce received his training at the luav architecture school in Venice, and at that city's experimental Istituto di Disegno Industriale. He opened a studio in Padua in 1959 and was a founder of Gruppo N, an avant-garde collective that experimented with programmed art, and was involved in the Antidesign activities of Gruppo Strum and UFO\*. He has continued to explore different forms of visual communication throughout his colorful and multifaceted career; while active as an interior designer, he has experimented with kinetic and serial art, worked with light, movement, and sound'in audio-visual and film projects, and taken on public and private architectural projects in many countries. In 1972 Pesce was one of the twelve designers invited to create a domestic environment for the New York Museum of Modern Art's seminal exhibition "Italy: The New Domestic Landscape." His contribution was a module he envisioned as ruins from a subterranean city from the year 2ooo, excavated a thousand years later—a disorienting enclosure divided into irregular room-like spaces.26 Pesce's most widely recognized work, however, has been in furniture design, which he has been doing since i968, most often exploring the use of plastics and new technology. He has designed for established frms like B&B Italia and Cassina. They include the provocative Tramonto a New York sofa (i98o), which suggests a setting sun hidden behind skyscrapers, and Feltri (i986), an armchair of resin-infused felt whose back and arms adjust to the occupant's taste. The chairs vary in intensity of color depending on how the dye settles in the mold, and in firmness of structure depending on the amount of hardener in the mix. Passing on his unorthodox views to the next generation, Pesce has taught at the University of Venice and Pratt Institute, New York, as well as in universities in Strasbourg and Brazil. Pesce was based in Paris for some time, but since 1983 has lived and worked in New York, where he has continued to experiment with silicone and other fluid materials in a SoHo studio and a Brooklyn workshop, creating objects whose distortions and irregularities point to the alienation of people by consumer culture. These sometimes bizarre but always intriguing objects have ranged from furniture and accessories to jewelry, watches, and shoes.
