They are the Kiro-san Observatory and the Water/Glass guesthouse. ![](images/4279773170323237bd31a0e70444c57d9648cac3f79dff1a5da5fc91896bb2c7.jpg) One of Kuma's characteristic works is the Water/Glass guesthouse on the Atami coast in Shizuoka Prefecture. The structure is situated halfway up a bluff extending into the ocean. Its external appearance is also invisible without taking a ship out and searching for the structure through binoculars. Instead, visual dynamism is injected into the interior of the building; there is a waterfilled terrace on the eaves covering the outer edge of the glass structure. When its surface is viewed from inside the room, it creates continuity with the Pacific Ocean afar. The structure is designed as an instrument serving only to view the outside from the inside, candidly banishing the idea of an external appearance.
