In 1951 she became the first female silversmith to establish her own studio in Sweden. Based in Stockholm, she initially concentrated on the design of wooden jewellery. The same year she received a commission for silver jewellery fromOrrefors and in 1954 was awarded a silver medal at the X Milan Triennale for her innovative designs in this medium.Believing that “A simple silver coil that highlights the line of the neck is more beautiful than all the diamonds in the world” and that “Diamonds are a girl's worst friend”, Bülow-Hübe often created plain ring-style necklaces incorporating pendants of pebbles and fossils as well as shimmering glass amulets. Bülow-Hübe moved to Paris in 1956 and two yearslater established a studio in Biot, France employing five assistants. While on the beach there collecting pebbles she met Pablo Picasso, who arranged for a solo exhibi tion of her work in Antibes in 1958. Duringthis period her designs, such asthe exquisite freeform Mobile necklace (1954–1959), became increasingly sculptural. In 1960 she was awarded a gold medal at the XII Milan Triennale and the coveted Lunning Prize. From 1967 she designed an extensive range of jewellery and watches for Georg Jensen in gold, silver and stainless steel. Typically, her designs for Georg Jensen, such as her well-known bangle-style wristwatch (1962), reflected the new sculptural confidence of the period. In 1968 she moved to Germany and established a design studio first in Wolfsburg, then in Wendhausen. While in Germany, Bülow-Hübe designed porcelain for Hutschenreuther and glassware for Löhnberg glassworks. She moved to Jakarta, Indonesia, in 1978, where she opened a studio and worked as a design consultant for a social foundation. In 1983, she was named an “Honorary Smith” by the Society of Contemporary Swedish Silver. Today, Bülow-Hübe continues to produce designs for Georg Jensen that are notable for their forward-looking, sensuous and sculptural qualities. ![](images/d4b63e41eeca795c77dabccb895d1464c08551b212ea23ce5a55d7967b917aad.jpg)
