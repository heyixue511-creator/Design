For Japan today, which faces a growing number of dilapidated apartment blocks and vacant spaces in office buildings, relying on infill renovation will become quite important. MUJI has begun by redesigning a floor, walls, ceilings, waterworks, storage, etc., not building a structure from scratch, but exploiting the potential of existing buildings in as flexible a manner as possible. The interior designer Tokujin Yoshioka proposed the first infill space, a very spacious single room, made by removing as many partitions as possible and devising a rational, effective storage method. This conceptual space, using accordion-fold plywood walls concealing a storage area, is a model of extremity; it can be freely re-designed to correspond with changes in the residents' lifestyles. This space also fully presents the possibilities of our modular product group.
