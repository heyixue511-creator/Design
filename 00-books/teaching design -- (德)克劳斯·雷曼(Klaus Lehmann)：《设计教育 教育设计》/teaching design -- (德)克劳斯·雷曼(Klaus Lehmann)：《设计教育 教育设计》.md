中国学生向我提出的问题

# What students in China ask me

Chinese people are very proud of their traditions. They can be proud of achievements that havedominated Asian life and culture for centuries. Confucius, more than 2000 years ago, named the ethical standards that still govern Asian life today. Confucianism today is supported byofficial politics and Confucius Institutions are being established in an effort to establish a Chinese identity. China is in the wake of industrial success and thepolitical ambition to become a prominent member of the political and industrial world. It was Deng Xiaoping who opened China after years of isolation, opening it to the western world and stimulating a new era of industrial progress. He put China on the world map again. Western civilisation has since swept into China, rousing expectations and creating opportunities, but at the same time causing irritations and problems of identity for the Chinese.

In my activities in China I have always stressed the fact that I can merely report what has been going on in Europe and try to explain the ideas behind these events. I have also warned my audience not to adopt Western thinking and behaviour uncritically. This I consider would represent an attempt to curry favour with Western values, in a wayself-imposed colonialism. It would bea poor sign for Chinese culture, which has after dark ages of isolation, managed to appear on the world map again and must be considered a great achievement.

Their concern. It is the ever-recurring question of how to marry Western technologyand Chinese values-a general, imprecise, but a very emotionally perceived problem. The official attitude-Western technology and Chinese values-is too simple: the Chinese have already adopted Western values just as the Western world has reflected and now is learning from Asian thinking. The integration of practical achievements,technology, style, or industrial culture always involves values, which are the driving forces behind it. Technological achievements and values are interdependent and cannot be separated.

中国人对自己的传统感到十分自豪。他们引以为豪的成就，几个世纪以来对整个亚洲的生活方式和文化都产生了巨大的影响。两千多年前孔子的道德标准与规范至今仍然影响着亚洲人的生活。时至今日，孔子的“儒家思想”已经获得了官方的支持，而在世界各地建立的孔子学院则旨在建立中国的身份认同感。因为中国的工业发展已经取得了巨大成就，而且中国目前正致力于成为世界政坛与经济领域的强国。在与外界隔绝多年后，邓小平开始了改革开放，并开启了一个工业大发展的新时代，是他把中国重新带上了世界的舞台。西方文明从那时起，开始风靡中国大地，带来了期望、创造了机会，然而同时，也带来了中国人的烦躁不安，以及对自我身份认同感的怀疑。

在中国做讲座的时间里，我一直强调一个事实:我只能向大家介绍欧洲所发生的事情，并解释这些事情背后所蕴含的意义，仅此而已。同时我也警告听众们不要不加批判地全盘接受西方的思维和行为方式。关于这一点，我可能是代表了一种以自愿接受的殖民主义视角，对西方价值观进行阿谀奉承的尝试。这对在经历了多年的与世隔绝后，力图重新与世界接轨，并创造新成就的中国文化来说，并不是一件好事。

对于他们来说，如何将西方的科技与中国的价值观结合起来，是一个经常需要面对的、无法回避的问题，也是一个普遍的、不精确的，但却深植于人们情感之中的问题。官方对这个问题的态度似乎非常简单：中国人目前已经接受了西方价值观，就像西方世界正在学习亚洲人的思维方式一样。实践成就、技术、风格或工业文化的整合总是要包含价值观的融合，而它又是所有结合过程的幕后推手。技术的成就和价值观是相互依存、不可分割的。

在讲座中我让中国听众们客观地评估西方价值观中有什么适合中

In my lectures I have left it to my Chinese audience to evaluate critically what is suitable for their own culture, encouraging the young generation to find a Chinese way and telling them that it would be their challenge to master it. On second thoughts-and this occupies my thinking-I found this insufficient and therefore have added a few reflections.

The transformation of traditional cultural values into a modern industrial society that wants to claim its position in a global context, needs, I feel, an acknowledgement of one's own culture-of what I am, of what has left its prints on me, of what has coined my thinking and what governs my life today. Identity is at the bottom of it all.

Identity must be redefined, not within one's own culture but in relation to competing cultures, reflecting what others think aboutyou, how they see you, and whatthey expect from you. It should sharpen what is original, what is, in commercial terms, “the unique selling point” of Chinese culture in the global competition ofcultural values.

Identity needs self-awareness.You cannot get it through your own efforts; just as in personal life, you need appreciation from outside to develop it. In the search for oneself the individual always finds himself“in a social context” according to the American sociologist Amitai Etzioni, and this can also be said about national identities.China will, so I hope, carefully watch how the world sees it from outside and the world willreadjust its picture of China. Is identity, I ask, the iconography of Chinese symbols in Chinese writing, the cloud pattern on the Olympic torch, the calligraphy of its past masters on modern posters, traditional Chinese porcelain copies, Xi Ching teapots, lacquer work, theacrobats, or the Pekingopera? Yes, it is all of these-but it is not. I feel the Chinese have plundered their past by merely copying and turned this so often into what is surely no more than airport art. China has not, unlike Japan, managedto give its past a modern expression. China has developed industrially in a breathtaking way and faces a fascinating challenge to master the situation culturally.

The architectural highlights that China is proud to show to the western worldare mostly designed by Western architects. I can see no sign yet of an originalChinese architecture that is up to international standing. I put it down to国文化的，鼓励年青一代的中国人去寻找具有中国特点的发展道路，并告诉他们坚守这条道路对他们来说，是一种巨大的挑战。事后我又想了想，感觉只有这些并不够，因此我决定再增加一些自己的新思考。

在如今的工业化时代，传统文化价值的转变需要在全球化的背景下宣扬自己的位置。我认为这种转变是对一个人所属文化的一种致意，它体现出身份、文化信息在这个人身上留下的烙印所包含的意义，以及制约个人想法和指导个人生活的因素。而身份则是所有因素立足的基础。

身份必须要重新定义，而且不能被限制在自己的文化之内，需要与其他文化建立联系，并反映其他人是如何理解一个人、如何看待你，以及他们期待从你身上得到什么。它需要明确什么是原创的，并且在文化价值的全球竞争中，什么是一个人的“独特卖点”(商业术语)。

身份需要一种自我意识，但只通过自己的努力也不能获得。这就像在个人生活中，一个人需要外界的肯定和欣赏，才能获得自我意识。美国社会学家埃米泰·埃兹奥尼说，在寻找自我的过程中，一个人总是感觉自己处在社会化的环境之中。而这也适用于国家认同的过程。因此我希望中国能认真观察世界是如何看待自己的，世界也会以全新的视角来审视中国。我想问：识别，就像中国书写中符号化的汉字、奥运火炬上的祥云、现代海报上古代名家的书法、中国西秦的茶壶、紫砂茶壶、漆器制品、杂技或是京剧一样，是中国象征的一种表现形式吗？是的，表面上是这样，但实际上不是。我感觉中国人仅仅通过复制掠夺了自己国家的传统元素，并把它们变成了一种庸俗不堪的“机场艺术”。与日本不同，中国想要以现代的表达方式展现自己的历史与传统。中国的工业发展非常迅速，然而其面临的保持文化的完整与独立性的挑战，也是前所未有。

中国人向西方世界展示的现代建筑大多都是由西方建筑师所设计。到目前为止，我还没有发现中国有任何原创的建筑在国际社会上崭露头角。我将在随后的独立章节中详细说明本人对建筑的意义以及fundamental attitudes about the meaning of architectureand its aestheticalimplications-a chapter by itself, and a challenge too.

I can, I admit, offer no shortcut and know that it will be a process that will need time, patience, and careful methods of cultural analysis. It will need a dialogue, a culture of discussion, and an intercultural exchange.

I would like my critical notes to be understood as a personal and constructive contribution to an ongoing cultural process in China, an appealto the many students I have had the privilege to meet and teach. I take their “everrecurring questions”very seriously and answer them with a philosophical note on cultural exchange.

But I am sure that if Chinese design wants to be more than a marketing instrument it needs a strong cultural foundation. Without it Chinese design will remain a regional fashion.

其美学内涵的基本态度，换句话说，这也是一种挑战。

对此，我承认不能提供任何的捷径，我也清楚，实现这一点需要时间、耐心和有效而细致的文化分析方法，同时还需要对话、关于文化的讨论，以及文化间的交流。

我想让我的客观性批判言论对中国目前的文化发展做出个人的建设性的贡献，使那些我有幸见到并传授知识的众多学生们感兴趣，并得到启发。我十分重视他们所提出的“持续不断发生的问题”，使用了文化交流中的哲学注释来回答每一个问题。

不过我确信，如果中国的设计事业想要成为市场营销的手段，就一定需要一个强大的文化基础。如果没有这个基础，中国的设计就不会被世界接受，只能局限在有限的区域之内。

鲁道夫·施泰纳(1861—1925)是奥地利哲学家。他是人智学和华尔道夫学校的创始人。华尔道夫学校的所有课程都是基于人智学理念所设立的，被业界认为可以当作国立学校的替代品。华尔道夫学校目前已经在许多国家得以设立。他的关于思想、灵魂和肉体的分类理念认为，人的头脑、心智和手需要得到平等的教育分配，即认知(思维)、艺术/创造(感觉)以及动手操作(行为)，这代表了教育的全能运作模式。除了在教育理论领域有所建树，施泰纳在其他领域也是一位颇有影响的改革家，如药品替代、生物类食物生产、舞蹈运动以及建筑等。他所设计的表现主义风格建筑影响了许多20世纪的建筑家，例如弗兰克劳埃德·赖特、勒·柯布西耶、汉斯·夏隆以及弗兰克·盖里等。

Against this background, I will take a big leap now andproceed to the 20th century and artistic education, where at myschool, the State Academy of Fine Arts Stuttgart,a prominent teacher, Adolf Hoelzel(1853 - 1934), revolutionized art education. He developed the concept of the “bildnerischen Mittel”-an expression that is hard to translate as there are no equivalent words in the English language-perhaps “performing media” comes close. Here, by the way, it is interesting to pause andnote that there are even differences in thinking and language in what might appear to be a homogeneous Europe.

AdolfHoelzel encouraged his students todiscover the effect of what the brush orthe pencil does and encouraged them to start their artistic discovery with the material and tools, untroubled by conventions orhistoric motives. One of his students wasJohannes Itten(1888– 1967), who took Hoelzel'sideas to the Bauhaus where he also introduced many of his own ideas, especially his theories on colour, and invented new elements for the teaching of foundations. At the Bauhaus, together with Moholy-Nagy, he developed what is known as the“Bauhaus Foundation Course”.OskarSchlemmer and Willi Baumeister, both students of Hoelzel, andothers like Paul Klee or Wassily Kandinsky, paved a new way of teaching in art and design at the Bauhaus. Let me quote a few key sentences from the BauhausManifestos, written by Itten around 1920, about the method of teaching. He writes,“The goal of the foundation course is to develop thecreative abilities of the student, to make him understand patterns in nature, and to make him understand the foundations of visual language”. He goes on to state that when learning, everybodyis hampered with a heavy load of prejudices and visual trash, which he has to get rid of “in order to free himself and to proceed to his own personal experience”.

The Bauhaus, as is widely known, pursued a holistic approach to education. It proposed that art is above any methodology and that practical experience is its foundation, as Walter Gropius stated in 1919. Bauhaus teaching has played its part in international education and has spread all over the World. It took a new direction at the Hochschule für Gestaltung Ulm(1955- 69), where modern educators introduced new elements while retaining a fundamental understanding that harked back to the Bauhaus Foundation Course.

接下来我将做时间上的大跨越：介绍20世纪的艺术教育状况。在我所就读的斯图加特国立造型艺术学院，一位著名的教师阿道夫·赫尔策尔(1853—1934)在艺术教育方面做出了革命性的贡献。他提出了一种理念“bildnerischenMittel”—这个词汇很难翻译，至今还没有相对应的英语表达方式，或许“performingmedia”(显像媒介)稍微贴切一些。在这里我想稍作停顿并指出，在看似同质化的欧洲，实际上无论在语言或是思维方式上，各国之间均存在程度不等的差异。

阿道夫·赫尔策尔鼓励他的学生去发现毛笔或铅笔作画所产生的不同效果，进而鼓励他们摆脱传统规约或历史因素的束缚，使用手中的材料和工具，开启奇妙的艺术发现之旅。约翰·伊顿(1888—1967)就是赫尔策尔的学生之一，他把老师的理念带到了包豪斯学校，在那里他又获得了许多自己的艺术灵感与理念，包括色彩理论。同时他还创造了为基础教学服务的新方法。在包豪斯学校，他与莫霍利·纳吉合作设立了著名的“包豪斯基础课程”。赫尔佐的学生奥斯卡·施莱默和威廉·包梅斯特，以及保罗·克里和瓦西里·康定斯基等人，都在包豪斯学校探索出了一条艺术设计教学的新路。下面让我引述伊顿在1920年左右撰写的《包豪斯宣言》中，有关教学方法的关键语句：“基础课程设立的目的是发展学生的创新能力，让他们从本质上了解不同的形式与视觉语言的基本要素。”他进而谈到在学习时，大家都会被多种多样的偏见与视觉垃圾所困扰，所以必须尽全力摆脱它们的影响，“让自己自由发挥，进而获得更好的个人体验”。

众所周知，包豪斯学校遵循一套全能化的教育模式。1919年，瓦尔特·格罗皮乌斯曾经提出，艺术比任何方法论都重要，实践体验就是艺术的根基。包豪斯的教学方法在国际教育界已经占有一席之地，并在世界各地得到推广。在乌尔姆设计学院(1955—1969)，包豪斯的教学理念又获得了新的发展。在这里，现代教育家们引进了全新教学元素，同时保留了与包豪斯基础课程中的基础教学相似的内容。

## What are foundations

There is no clear and universal definition of what constitutes foundations.

In any case they do not denote project work, where you design a real product, devise a concept or a system for solving a given problem. ## A methodological note

In my teaching of foundations I follow a principle also known by science: a problem in practice is analysed and translated into a general or universal question. It then is dealt with in an abstract manner, by experiment, as an exercise. Its results, in a third step, are evaluated, producing criteria. Its findings have a universal character and can be applied in practice to other problems, so providing a methodology for solving practical and formal problems.

I introduced the exercises as a foundation to project work, where the objective isto tackle a live project. Project work demands a lot of research, requires the student to master conceptual approaches, functional requirements, user requirements, and methods of manufacture, to consider ecological implications, human factors, or to meet marketing objectives. Mostly it leaves little room for experimentation or research into formal and aesthetic values.

The design-shape, appearance, and emotional value-are the gist of foundation exercises. Foundations therefore, forgetting functional requirements, aim to concentrate on the exploration of form and structure. Foundation exercises are meant to provide the student with a repertoire of formal options for practical applications. They are developed, unlike the exercises of the Bauhaus foundation course with its more artistic background, from practical problems in the design process. Because of their universal character, the student can 展创新性的最佳途径，它需要对话式教学，即学生们的参与，来共同发展 完善相关的标准，并且围绕“什么”、“为什么”以及“怎么样”等话题，进行 持续不断的探讨，以总结完善指导个人才能发展和激发潜能机制的措施。

我采取了一种实用主义方法。我的教学经验告诉我，只有非功利性的、抽象的、普遍性的练习模式才能将人的创新能力最大化地激发出来。在参与项目工作时，大家经常面临很大的压力，此时就需要一种之前曾获得的、既定的全能操作模式发挥作用了。而获得这种模式就是基础教学的最终目的。

## ·方法论说明

在我教学设计基础的过程中，我都遵循以下科学的原则，即在实践练习中遇到的问题要经过详细分析，转化为一种可以普遍存在的问题。然后它就可以使用一种以实验为代表的、更加抽象的方式来进行进一步练习。接下来开始评估实验的结果，并创立新的标准。实验成果可以适用于各种情况，并在实践中解决其他类似的问题，因此这种做法为解决实际问题与正式问题提供了一套方法论。

我把练习当作是进行项目工作的基础，目的是为了承接真实的项目。项目工作需要做很多前期调研，要求学生们掌握主要的认知方法、功能要求、用户规章以及制作方法，思考人的因素是否会对生态产生影响或是否达成营销目的等。大多数情况下，进行形式与美学价值研究或实验的余地非常有限。

设计形状、外观和情感价值是基础练习的本质内容。因此，除了功能要求之外，基础教学把重点放在形式和结构的开发与研究上。基础练习旨在向学生提供关于实践应用范围选择的正确方法。不同于包豪斯更富于艺术特征的基础课程的练习模式，这里的基础练习通过在设计过程中发现实际问题来发展完善自身的系统。正是由于它们普遍适用的特征，学生们可以集中精力研究形式价值和视觉规则，训concentrate on formal values, on visual laws, train his creativity, and developcriteria for rational judgement. Foundation exercises are also the testingground for individual approaches, in which the student learns to find his ownand personal methodology.

## The Russian Constructivists-discovering the symbolic power of construction

In the short but influential movement of Russian Constructivism between 1920 and 1927 Rodchenko played a key role. ## Rietveld and his radical approach to mass production

Gerrit Thomas Rietveld(1888- 1964) was a Dutchcabinetmaker and later became internationally known as an architect. In 1919 he joined Holland's “De Stijl” movement, whose intention was to create an objective and universal language in art and design by omitting all personal manifestations. Its spiritual head was Theo van Doesburg(1883– 1931), who developed a theory called Neoplasticism by decomposing volumes into horizontal and vertical planes and reducing visual language to the primary colours of red, blue, yellow, and black, white, and grey. By doing this he wanted to keep the means of artistic expression pure and so create a new aesthetic. Many European artists belonged to the movement, of whom the best known is PietMondrian( 1872 – 1944).

When Rietveld built his“red-blue-chair”, which was only a prototype, he surely did not know that it would become an icon of modern design history. It showed the principles of Neoplasticism, using pure geometric elements and colour coding, red for vertical planes, blue for horizontal planes, and yellow for the exosed cut ends of the struts, and so related it to a virtual neoplastic space. Its elementary construction shows a radical approach to manufacture and mass production. Rietveld's constructions stimulated the Bauhaus designers and generations to follow.

## ·里特维尔德和他对于大众制造的激进手法

## Miesvan der Rohe and the beauty of construction

Mies van der Rohe considered construction as “the incorruptible guardian of the spirit of its time, objective and free from individualism and fantasy”. In his eyes construction was “a fundamental aspect of architecture” and design. He used the material according to its structural function, making the construction transparent. He liberated architecture from mass and made it as lightas possible.Structural order was for him a philosophical concept, employing the same principles as “a whole from top to bottom, down tothe last detail”". And, pursuing the rules of reason and rationality, structure, construction, and proportion, he developed it to an almost poetic quality, which in its reductionist aesthetic leaves us with a substantial heritage.

## Charles Eames-pioneering technology in furniture design

Much can be said about Charles Eames(1907– 1978), perhaps the most important American designer. There are so many facets to his and his wife's work that they cannot be dealt with in these short notes on construction. We will look at only one aspect, and that is his approach to furniture Design. Eames was fascinated by technologies and was most creative in finding new ones. Very early, about 1943, he experimented with plywood and inventedthe “Kazam!”machine, which enabled him to bendplywood in three dimensions. He turned his newly found technology into structures that he then used for his chairs. He introduced fibreglass technology into furniture making, designing seat shells around 1949, and aluminium castings for the supporting structure in the fifties and sixties. His innovations are marked by a strong interest in technology, but his greatest achievement is that he turned the new technologies into appropriate and pleasing shapes. His mastery of material and construction can be read from the sophisticated structural details of his furniture, and it is still on the market today!

## Frey Otto-studying nature and pioneering Cable Net Structures

Frey Otto(born 1925) was one of the early creative architects who studied structures in Nature. In one of his books he pays reverence to Wentworth D'Arcy Thompson, the famous English biologist whose 1917 book “OnGrowth and Form” stimulated the study of organic structures, by quoting him on the front page.

His institute was an experimental laboratory, where biologists, engineers, and architects met for interdisciplinary discussion long before it became fashionable. His studies include research into minimal nets, membranes, convertible roofs, and pneus(pneumatic structures) in Nature and technology. Many of his designs have been built and many architects and engineers have been inspired by himand have continued his work. He must be considered a pioneer of natural constructions and structural thinking. The essence of his work can be found in the publications of the IL-Institute for Lightweight Structures-at Stuttgart University.

## The Cube exercise

In order to experience ways of construction practically, I have devised some exercises that also illustrate the various points. If the exercises are done ina group, different individual approaches can be put on the table for discussion and students can very easily evaluate them themselves.Of course they will be asked to justify their evaluation and so are led to find criteriafor their judgement.

All basic exercisesare devised to train skills and to develop criteria.

Build a stable cube from identical elements.

This task has several teaching objectives. First of all, the student learns, eithermethodologically or empirically, that a cube can be divided into three, four, six, eight, etc, , elements.

Furthermore the student discovers the huge variety of geometric relationships within a cube. By making three-dimensional cardboard models, thestudent finds that there are stable and unstable cubesand, with very little outside help, determines the elementary principles of form and the laws of structural engineering. This exercise also demonstrates that for every problemthere are both elegant und inelegant solutions. By showing that an aesthetically pleasing result can be achieved only by matching structure, form, material, and manufacture, an important principle has been learned.

## Bridging exercise

This exercise is also about construction, about structural principles, about developing manual skills and understanding materials. It follows the principle of “learning by doing” as a didactic method and shows that “form follows function”-a principle that has become a substantialcriterion of rational design.

Bridge a given gap with different materials.

Have a look at bridges, pillars, and beams, at structures in paper, wood, or steel.

Enter into the challenge of bridging the gap with a minimum of material. You will be surprised how many examples you can find in everyday life. In discussing different approaches to thesame given problem, you will discover the vocabulary for the evaluation of form and structure.

# Note on wrapping, packaging, foldingand cladding (linked together but treated separately for better understanding)

Wrapping is a very elementary process to protect goods and gifts. Apart from its protective function its main function is decorative. Eastern tradition shows a variety of outstanding creative handmade wrapping with leaves, bamboo, strings, or paper. The Japanese book “How to wrap five eggs” has been an eye-opener to the Western design community.

Packaging demands additional functions.Packaging must be manufactured industrially, must be economical in the use of material, must be environmentfriendly, and after use should be returned to the cycle of materials. Food and gifts are usually wrapped in paperor cardboard, but also appliances, coffee machines, computers, hair dryers, and even technicalparts. Packaging has a temporary function and is discarded when its contents have reachedtheir destination. Packaging has become a vital selling point, for it is the first thing the consumer encounters and can be decisive in supporting thedesire to buy its contents. The packaging industry is a huge field for the creative designer.

Folding is a very elementary process, turning a flat piece of material(2 - dimensional) into a spatial structure(3- dimensional). We employ folding when we form a paper plane from a sheet of paper and give it a new function. But there are innumerable examples of new products having been generated by folding. Investigating the many possibilities and approaches, combined here in 2 exercises, is a rewarding fieldof research, which can be carried out using moderate means.

Cladding is another field of practical design, i, e. the encasing of components to give a permanent shape to a product. Usually the casing is made from a durable material; sheet or cast plastics or metal and forms the skin-the durable and permanent shape of the product. Cladding, treated in this way, can open new views of a classical design problem-the work on the shape and its meaning.

For further reading:

Package Design Now, English/French/German, Cologne 2008ISBN 978 -3- 8228-4031-3. A general review of international packaging: Origami.

“包裹”是保护商品和礼物最基本的操作步骤。它除了具有保护功能，还主要具有装饰功能。东方国家的传统中，有多种多样、精妙绝伦的手工制包装形式，如使用叶子、竹子、绳子和纸张等。对于西方设计界人士来说，日本人的一本书《如何包装五个鸡蛋》绝对让他们大开眼界。

“包装”包含了更多功能，也提出了更多要求。“包装”必须通过工业制造进行，在材料的使用上必须方便经济，整个过程必须环保，使用后，相关材料应该可回收，可被循环利用。食物和礼品、家用设备、咖啡机、电脑、吹风机甚至技术设备零件都通常使用纸张或纸板进行包装。“包装”发挥的功能只是暂时性的，当其所包装的物体展现出来时，它的使命也就结束了。“包装”目前已经成为商品至关重要的一项卖点，因为它会给消费者留下第一印象，并左右消费者的购买意愿。“包装”产业是富有创新性的设计师施展才华的广阔空间。

“折叠”也是一种基本的操作过程，即将平整的一片材料(二维)变成一种立体结构(三维)。当我们将一张纸变成一架纸飞机时，我们就采用了折叠的手法，赋予纸张以新功能。然而通过折叠，还会产生很多新的产品，相关的例子不胜枚举。在本书这两个练习中，我们调查研究这些可能性与操作方法，这是一种很有意义的研究模式，可以通过适度的手段来达成。

“覆层”是实际设计过程的另一领域，即将某种材料的镀层附在产品表面，以使其永久保持外形。通常情况下，产品表面永久性的覆层都是用持久耐用的材料制作，如金属板、塑料等。用这种方式制成的覆层可以为解决长期以来的设计难题—形状与内涵，提供新的解决视角。

## The Packaging Exercise

A simple exercise to train inventiveness and understanding of material can be done by packaging a simple product with paper or cardboard. If you understand the characteristics of paper, you have already learned a lot Packagíng products is a specialist job, which usually is done by specialised designers, but isopen for everybody who has goodideas and knows how to handle the material. Packages have become a prominent selling point. The package must first of all fulfil its function to protect and support a product. But in addition it conveys a meaning. It can tell the consumer what is inside, arouse his interest, display lifestyle, convey intelligence or wit, simplicity, tradition, abundance, or ecological consideration. A package is thegarment of a product and, just as human clothing, aims to clad the product and support its personality.

Design a package for ping pong balls with paper or cardboard using only a cutteras tool.

## Approaches:

Check its practical functions; protection, handling, transport, stacking, or recycling. ## The Folding Exercise

Folding is a very elementary process, turning a flat piece of paper, cardboard, a sheet of metal, or even plywood, if you apply hinges to it, into a threedimensional object. It is related to construction, for it creates a stable structure, a space saving object, an artistic expression, an ornamental object, or a sculptural effect.

Design a stable construction from a flat material, cut and joined by hinges, that first forms a flat package, but when unfolded you could either sit or lie on or use as a table or shelf.

The folding exercise has many authors. I was inspired by Werner Schmidt, an architect in Switzerland, who made it part of a workshop at the State Academy in Stuttgart and whose results were shown at the Furniture Fair in Milan.

I have adapted the idea to basic resources for my workshops in China. Its objective is to experience construction, function, human factors, and aesthetics. It should provide the opportunity to ask a great number of questions and to discover new criteria.

## Approaches:

Cardboard or foam-core sheets joined by adhesive tape are a good start. Concentrate on their structural and aesthetic qualities. Encourage new technologies and applications. Open the mind for new discoveries.

## ·折叠练习

折叠是一种非常基本的操作过程，即将平整的纸张、纸板、一张金属片甚至胶合板(需安装折页)变成一种三维立体结构。它与构成息息相关，因为它创造了一种稳固的结构、节省空间的形体、艺术化的表现手法、具有装饰性的物件或者是具有雕塑感的效果。

使用平整的材料设计一种稳固的结构，切割，并用折页连接，这样首先就做成了一个平整的包装。而当拆开它时，你可以坐在上面、躺在上面，或把它当作桌子或架子使用。

关于折叠的练习，我的灵感来自许多人。比如华纳·施密特，一位瑞士建筑师。他把这项练习作为斯图加特国立学院工作坊实践的一部分，而后，实践的成果在米兰举办的家具展会上展出。

## The Cladding Exercise

Cladding components, whether they are mechanical or electronic, is an essential part of a designer's everyday job. It involves finding the form for the skin around components and turning it into astructure with a meaningful shape.

Think of a car with an engine, passenger compartment, and luggage space. Thinkof a hair dryer, with heating unit, fan, motor, and handle, or a coffee machine with water tank, heating unit, coffee filter and container for the coffee. After its structure has been settled, the distributed volumes need a skin or housing. The components must be tied together and begiven a logical, pleasing and convincing shape. The cladding exercise aims to stimulate different solutions to a standard design problem.

Design a skin or housing around two given volumes.

## Approaches:

Havea look atyour environment; there are innumerable examples that you might view in a new way. There are basically two different ways: the simple box in which you can put anything-the tectonic approach or, like the human body with the skin closely following theskeleton and muscles-the biological approach. There is no one single way. You might ask yourself whether to follow the given shape closely or to be generous without being afraid of packaging some air. You might consider whether youwant to show what is underthe skin and wonder what could be a meaningful shape. Youmight think ofdifferentways of generating a shape and try them.Expand your design skills by testing a variety of approaches andso develop your professional repertoire.

## ·覆层练习

为物体覆层，不管是使用机械方式还是电子方式，都是设计师每日工作中必不可少的组成部分。它包括找出可以包裹物体四周的镀层的形式，然后再把它变成一种合适而富有意义的结构。

设想一下，一辆汽车由引擎部分、客舱部分和后备箱部分构成。再想一下，一个吹风机由加热装置、风扇、发动机和手柄组成，或者一部咖啡机，由水槽、加热装置、咖啡过滤器和盛放咖啡的容器构成。当覆层的具体结构确定之后，那么这个物体就需要一层外膜或外罩。该物体的组成部分必须牢固地连接在一起，并保持一种合乎逻辑的、令人愉悦和信服的形状。覆层练习旨在为标准的设计问题寻找不同的解决方案。

为两个物体设计一层外膜或外罩。

方法：

观察你周围的环境，一定存在你有意愿以新的方法去审视的数不胜数的例子。一般来说有两种不同的方法：一种是简单的方法，如同盒子一样，你可以放任何东西进去无论是构造方式，或是生物方式都可以(就像人体的皮肤，与骨骼和肌肉是紧密相连的)。事实上并不存在一种简单的方法。你可以问自己，是否应该忠实地遵循物体本来的形状来设计包装，或者大方一些，不在意包进去一些空气。你或许会思考：是否应该展示“皮肤”下面的东西?什么形状才是富有意义的?你可能会想到制作“皮肤”外形的各种不同的方法，并去尝试。你可以通过尝试各种不同的方法来拓展自己的设计技能，从而扩展自己的专业知识储备。

“这是一种抽象思维的方式，它让我们从全新的视角观察周围的物体。”(唐明明，一年级学生)

# A note on the value of geometry

In many lectures I have taught about geometry and mathematics in design and architecture, speaking about their origins in Greece and the Arab world, their relation to aesthetics and music, their implications for the European cathedral builders and artists in the Middle Ages, preoccupations with them in the Renaissance, about Japanese architecture based on Tatami mats. I have discussed the“Golden Section” right up to Le Corbusier's Modulor and present day applications. Geometry occupies a prominent place in European design thinking. It is a chapter for which I have not yet found an Asiatic equivalent.

If you want to understand the European preoccupation with numbers, geometry, regular figures and their meaning for the 21st century, you should go back to the Greek philosopher Plato(427 – 348 BC) and his book Philebos, in which he describes why geometric figures and volumes are considered beautiful: “I want to try to talk about the beauty of shape. . .”

"I mean straight lines and curves and the shapes that can be made from them , flat or three-dimensional, made with the lathe or the square. They are not beautiful for a certain reason or purpose, but because of their true nature, giving pleasure by their own being , totally free from superficial showmanship. Colours too, in this way, are beautiful.”(Plato). Platonic thinking has influenced European art and design over the centuries.

His philosophy was in the minds of Bauhaus teachers who returned to the square, triangle, circle and the platonic volumes as described by Paul Klee, Johannes Itten, Wassily Kandinsky and others. “Such pure abstract beings, with their own life, are the square, the circle, the triangle, the trapezium, or the numerous shapes... that can be derived from them. ”(Kandinsky). Their ideas governed practical production of their time and still have an influence on today's production.

我在之前的多次讲座中，都探讨过设计和建筑行业中的几何学和数学，讲述过它们是如何从希腊和阿拉伯世界发源，以及它们与美学和音乐的关系，它们带给欧洲教堂建造者以及中世纪艺术家们的启示，文艺复兴时期它们带给人们的灵感，基于榻榻米垫子来探讨日本建筑等等。我从黄金分割开始，一直讨论到勒·柯布西耶的著作《模数理论》和当今社会中的应用等话题。在欧洲设计思想中，几何学占据了至关重要的位置。而这一点，我至今还没有在亚洲找到相似的情况。

如果你想要通过数字、几何学、规则的形状来了解欧洲式全神贯注的工作作风，以及它们在21世纪所体现出的意义，你就应该先去了解希腊哲学家柏拉图(公元前427—348)以及他的著作《斐利布篇》。他在这部著作中描述了几何图形和立体模型为什么如此美丽的原因：“我想试着谈论形体之美，我指的是直线、曲线，还有由直线和曲线构成的、二维或三维的、用车床或直角尺做出的形状。或许从某种原因或意图来看，它们并不美丽，但是因为它们的本质是纯粹的，给人以愉悦的感觉，丝毫不肤浅、不浮夸。而色彩也正是因为这一点，令人感到美丽无比。”(柏拉图)柏拉图式的思维方式，几个世纪以来一直影响着欧洲艺术和设计的发展。

包豪斯学校的老师也受到了柏拉图哲学的影响。根据保罗·克里、约翰·伊顿、瓦西里·康定斯基的描述，在教学过程中，这些老师们都使用了正方形、三角形、圆形以及柏拉图式立体模型。康定斯基曾说：“这些抽象的形体是那么纯净，正方形、三角形、圆形、梯形，以及其他各种形状…每个都是生机勃勃的。”他们的思想统领着他们所处时代的实际生产过程，时至今日仍具有相当的影响力。

关于另一个有关几何学的话题是“黄金分割”:它决定了物体的比

Another topic, related to geometry, is the “Golden Section”, which determines proportions,harmony, andtheir mathematical description. It can be found on many European classical buildings of antiquity. The Pythagoreans in ancient Greece related circle geometry to musical intervals and found in it a visual representation of harmony in music. Numbers were considered the key to understanding the cosmos.

Around 1200, Fibonacci, an Italian mathematician, related a sequence of numbers he had discovered to the Golden Section and to natural growth, as can be found in plants and shells. The sketches of Michelangelo(1475- 1584), the greatuniversal Italianartist, reveal his preoccupation with the topic. Numbers and measurements were a secret well kept by the cathedral builders in the Middle Ages, for they were a treasured tool and the key to their much admired buildings. The Swiss/French architect, Le Corbusier(1887– 1965) , took it up and devised his“Modulor”, an order of numbers related to the Golden Section and the human body, and used it for his buildings. The historical examples are, in addition to their epistemological and practical value, loaded with metaphysical interpretations and can open the door to understanding Western design ideology.

Today we have to strip away its historical ballast and find out what we can make of it. The interest of a designer in mathematics and geometry differs from the interest of a scientist, whose ambition is to describe the world In many lectures I have taught about geometry and mathematics in design and architecture, speaking about their origins in Greece and the Arab world, their relation to aesthetics and music, their implications for the European cathedral builders and artists in the Middle Ages, preoccupations with them in the Renaissance, about Japanese architecture based on Tatami mats. I have discussed the“Golden Section” right up to Le Corbusier's Modulor and present day applications. Geometry occupies a prominent place in European design thinking.

Today we have to strip away its historical ballast and find out what we can make of it. The interest of a designer in mathematics and geometry differs from the interest of a scientist, whose ambition is to describe the world in numbers. The artist or designer looks for theirvisual expression, “the contradiction between physical facts and psychic perception” as Josef Albers, (1888 - 1976) a Bauhaus teacherand famous educator inthe US put it, describing the different intentions. And, looking at abstract art in Europe, it remains a fascinating question of whether or when geometry, its patterns, its mathematical order, or its expression are considered beautiful or even become art.

例、和谐性以及它们的数学特性。黄金分割可以在许多欧洲古代建筑中找到实例。古希腊毕达哥拉斯学派的人把圆几何学与音程相联系，从而发现了用视觉手法展现音乐中和谐因素的途径。人们普遍认为，数字是开启寰宇奥秘的钥匙。

在1200年左右，意大利数学家斐波那契把他所发现的一种数列与黄金分割和自然界的生长(如植物和贝壳的成长)相联系。意大利久负盛名的艺术家米开朗基罗(1475—1584)的素描作品，都显现出这位大师对黄金分割的痴迷。在欧洲中世纪，教堂建造者心中都留存着关于数字和测量方面的奥秘，因为它们被奉为一种珍贵的工具，是了解建筑本身特性的关键所在。瑞士籍法国建筑师勒·柯布西耶(1887—1965)采用了黄金分割定律的精髓，进而构想出了“模数理论”的概念。它是一种有关黄金分割和人体相关的一组数字排列秩序，在他设计建筑时使用。这些历史上的具体实例具有非凡的认识论和实际意义，许多人对它们做出了近乎超自然化的解读。了解这些，也就等于开启了了解西方设计思想的大门。

时至今日，我们暂且要把这些历史的沉淀放在一边，思考一下我们是否可以从中获得有用的经验。设计师们对于数学和几何学的兴趣点与想要使用数字来描绘世界的科学家们截然不同。艺术家或设计师们寻求的是一种视觉表达的方式，美国著名教育家、包豪斯学校教师约瑟夫·亚伯斯(1888—1976)曾这样描述他们不同的兴趣点：“他们之间的矛盾所在，就是物理现实与心理感知之间的冲突。”综观欧洲抽象艺术的现状，有个令人感兴趣的问题仍然悬而未决：几何学、几何结构、其数学排列秩序，或这种表达方法从何时开始，或者是否真的成为了美丽的化身、艺术的浓缩？

总结：

首先，几何学是一种实用工具，让设计师可以使用更精确的方式与外界进行沟通。它是技术作图的基础，也是每位设计师或建筑师所必须掌握的技能。它是一种语言，让我们更清晰地了解构思灵感与复制

## The Grid-Exercise

With this exercise I want to focus your interest on geometry, dealing very basically with grids and their value in design. Of course it needs some basic knowledge about grids,which can be found in any book on geometry.

Start with a simple 2– dimensional problem:

Design a regular plane tessellation that does not reveal its original grid.

(A plane tessellation is a way of dividing a plane into regular shapes that cover it withoutgaps)

“. . . a very abstract exercise, but it taught me to look at shapes.”(First Year Student)

The objective is to show the usefulness of grids and to discover them as practical tools. It should help you to understand that a design based on geometric structures reveals an aesthetic coherence. Restrictions are more helpful in teaching than complete openness.

## Approaches:

Start with pieces of paper on which you have copied a grid or try it on your computer. A grid can be considered as a plane with regular shapes. By cutting away a piece of one ofits shapes and addingthe cut off piece to another side you have found the clue. In no time you can produce many variations. Comparing the different solutions will raise many questions. Does it, as is the objective of the exercise, conceal its origin? What is a good and what is a bad solution, and why? Whatcriteria do you find for your judgement? What happens when you distort the grid?

Look for tessellations in your environment. Try inventing new ones. Can you think of practical applications?

# A note on Produc Semantics

Product Semantics entered design discussions in the eighties, throwing a new light on the role of the designer and the role of the product as a means of communication. The subject caused uneasiness among members of the design community as they realizing that what was considered good and timeless design was far away from the needs of consumers, who saw more in the product than only its practical side. It was the introduction of life style into design thinking, described by the French sociologist Pierre Felix Bourdieu (1930– 2002) among others. It also stimulated a new view of consumers, who it was realized were not a homogenous community but a set of groups with different expectations that depended on their social background and education. Consumers were categorized into target groups and their lifestyle became a matter of market research and intensive design consideration.

The topic was founded in the theory of science, where semiotics, the science of signs, and semantics, dealing with signs and their meanings, became an important part of it. The theory of semiotics goes back to Charles Sanders Peirce(1839– 1914), Charles William Morrisin the US(1903–1979), and to Max Bense in Germany(1910 – 1990). Jochen Gros and Bernhard Buerdek developed it into a practical tool for designers in the eighties, calling it product language.

Product language deals with the non-verbal communication between user and product, showing how it can be used in formal design to make the product understandable to the user. Abraham Moles speaks about the readability of the world'. The basic idea behind this is the realisation that the best products are self-explanatory and do not need a manual to use them(this is the objective of the Push-Pull-Turn-Exercise).

Apart from formalfactors, shape, structure, proportion, direction, texture, or colour i. e. formal language, the use of metaphors has been introduced and widely used in what became post-modern design. 该话题的基础是科学理论，其中主要研究符号及其具体含义的符号学和语义学是至关重要的部分之一。符号学理论最早由美国的查尔斯·桑德斯·皮尔士(1839—1914)、查尔斯·威廉姆·莫里斯(1903—1979)以及德国的马克斯·本译(1910—1990)提出。在20世纪80年代，约肯·格罗斯以及伯恩哈德·布尔德克把该理论发展成为可供设计师们使用的一种实用工具，被称为产品语言。

产品语言指的是用户和产品之间的、无需用言语表达的一种交流形式，通过外在的设计向用户展示使用方法，从而让产品的相关信息更容易被用户所理解与认同。亚伯拉罕·莫尔斯提出了“可读化世界”的说法，其基本要义是，人们已经意识到，最好的产品具备自我推介的功能，不需要说明书、手册之类的东西(这是“推拉转”练习的目标)。

Metaphorical design must be seen in close relation to post-modern design, that as an antimovement to functional design, called for more emotionality in the product. In the words of McCoy it is “an expansion of the possibilities for imagery in design beyond the implied limits of BauhausModernism”. Though controversial in its intention, strongly dependent on cultural understanding, and difficult to apply, I would like to describe some principles of metaphorical design:

—decodable mechanical prínciples recall practical experience and are translingual and trans-cultural.

—symbols of human or animal gestures are part of a universal body language. —familiar abstract symbols originated in a particular culture but have become universal signs through a process of globalisation and communication.

—symbols of technology, the master models of technological achievement, have become globally understood metaphors.

—emotional qualities of materials appeal to our senses of touch and feeling and give us sensual experiences.

—stylistic or historic metaphors are embedded in the culture in which they originated.

"Product semantics is not a new style. It is a serious study of the meanings that emerge in human interaction with objects.”(Klaus Krippendorf)

## A note on the design of Information

There is no universally established methodology in the field of design; it is a living body and new elements are added from time to time, Sometimes a new look at the world of science or technology, or sometimes a new insight or a political situation has provoked new thinking and methods to be invented and subseguently introduced into teaching. One of these cases is the way of looking at the product as a means of communication(Theodor Ellinger, 1966) that might have had its roots in the then upcoming theory of Semantics. This view spurred a lot of thinking and had practical implications because if the product is a means of communication it has to rely heavily on its design-its form, shape, and structure-in order to express its communicative qualities. Design activity now is not merely a question of form, it is more important to design the context of the product, which wants to be on stage and made public. Instead of “How are things made?”comes the question “What does it mean to us?”(Bürdek) and this opens many new horizons for the design and demands special qualifications from the designer, making him a “steward of thedisciplines”(Carnegie Mellon University, USA).

These developments include the move away from a established design attitude to an anti-movement to functional design, the development of product language (Offenbach), the new challenge of microelectronics, in which function no longer comes before form. “Form follows function”, Sullivan's famous saying, which has been a rock of functional design, is no longer any help. Electronics can be packaged in any shape and this characteristic demanded a new approach.

Postmodern design, though of very specific Western origin, swept like a mighty wave across the World and became merely a style, forgetting its original intentions. The discovery of emotional qualities opened the door to many subjective interpretations like “Form follows emotion”.

在设计领域，并不存在放之四海而皆准的方法。设计本身充满着活力，新的元素一直都在不断被注入其中。有时候，科技世界的新发展、新的洞察力或者新的政治形势，都会引发新的思维方式或方法的出现，进而在教学过程中被使用。其中一种例子就是把产品当作一种交流方式的看法(西奥多·艾琳格，1966)，其在随后出现的语义学体系当中找到了理论依据。这种观点引发了许多人的思考，并具有实用的现实意义，因为如果产品成为一种交流的方式，就必须强烈依赖于其本身的设计状况：外观、形状以及结构等，以便于传达产品的相关信息。当今时代，设计活动已经不只关注于外在形态的问题，而更关注于产品的内涵，而这才是需要被大力宣传和推广的部分。以往类似“东西是如何制造出来的”这样的问题已被“这东西对我们来说意味着什么”所取代(布尔德克)。而这个问题为设计开启了更多全新的视角，同时对设计师们也提出了更高的要求，他们要成为“多学科的全能战士”(美国卡内基梅隆大学)。

这些领域的发展包括从既有的设计思维与态度向功能化设计的反向转化，产品语言的发展(奥芬巴赫设计学院)，微电子领域的新挑战等等，这其中都存在一个共同点，即外表比功能更重要。沙利文的名言“形式追随功能”，在过去曾被视为功能化设计的金玉良言，如今已经派不上任何用场。电子产品可以被包装成各式各样的形状，而这种特性需要使用一种新方法来审视。

虽然其起源于西方世界，后现代设计的浪潮已经席卷全球各地，成为一种风格，而其原本之初衷却早已被人忘却。设计中情感特征的发现使得对“形式追随功能”之类的主观话语的阐释有了一种全新的解读。

A lot could be said about the many attitudes, philosophies, andtheories that have originated from this new look at the product and its communicative function, about the opening of design to related discíplines and the number of design activities that have sprung up as a result, like corporate identity, event design, exhibition design, network design, home page design, or visualisation design-to name only a few of the activities that have become established professional services.

In my exercises, I want the problem to be boiled down to simple questions. In the design of information, where you are dealing not only with hardware elements,but with their structure and their perceptual qualities, with what you want to communicate, and with what the product's meaning should be. To accomplish your taskyou can use the structure of communication, hierarchical intentions, practical elements, and the shape, form, and texture of the product to express and make it understood. Think of a mobile telephone, a stereo installation, a digital camera, or an alarm clock, which all require understandable operations.

# Notes on personal language and style

When young people decide to study design, they usually have been drawn to it by something that has impressed them, bysomebody who encouraged them, or by discovering what they think their own talents are and wanting to develop them. That is all legitimate, but their knowledge of what design is or what designers do, is often very rudimentary or even nonexistent.

They know that design is about products or buildings or books or posters and have already preconceived pictures in their mind. They expect that when studying design they will get rules and recipes for their professional work, not knowing that they will have to study, select, and evaluate what is offered to them, and turn it into a new and personal experience. When they start studying, they are already loaded with pictures, preconceived ideas, views, attitudes, and a closed set of conventions that prevent them from develop creativity and opening themselves to new directions.

Bauhaus teachers thus wanted to empty the students' minds of their preconceived imagery and devised a foundation course to provoke and radically rid the student of his conventions and open his mind for new experiences.

That, I think, must remain a pedagogical goal today. But the Bauhaus Foundation Course, which, after emerging in Germany, was taken to the USA and Japan, and later spread around the World, needs both continuity and a present day interpretation. This was my intention and the purpose of my practical exercises, which are the result of myexperience in the teaching of design.

The following exercise has a different background, far from Bauhaus teaching, which pursued a universal and anonymous language in design. It is intended to provoke the student to look and study the visual language of designers and architects who have left their mark on design history.

当一些年轻人决定学习设计之时，他们通常都是被一些令他们印象深刻的东西所吸引住了，或者被他人激励了，又或者发现自己的才能之所在，并决定继续发展下去，才会做出这样的决定，这些都是合情合理的。然而他们对于什么是设计，或者设计师具体是做什么的，通常是一知半解，甚至是毫无概念。

他们都清楚，设计是关于产品、建筑、书籍或海报等方面，而且都在脑海中构思过设计的轮廓。当他们期待学习设计时，他们会得到从事专业工作的法则和秘诀，殊不知他们必须静下心来学习、选择和评价自己所面对的一切，然后必须把它们得到的东西转化为全新的、属于个人的经验。当他们开始研究时，他们都已经被各种图例、前人所谓的点子与经验、态度，和封闭不前的传统规约压得喘不过气来，这些都成为了他们发展创新性和拓展新方向的拦路虎。

因此，包豪斯学校的老师们把学生们脑中既定的思维模式全部清空，并构思出一种全新的基础课程，来激发学生们寻求新体验的灵感，帮助他们尽快摆脱传统规约的束缚。

关于这一点，我想包豪斯的教学理念在今天依旧是富于实用性的。包豪斯基础课程发源于德国，但是后来在美国和日本得到应用，进而推广至全世界。然而我认为，虽然它应该继续得到推介，但是在今天的时代背景下，它也需要全新的解读与改良。这就是我编写这些实践性练习的初衷和目的。这些练习都是基于我多年来教授设计课程的经验而作。

接下来的这个练习，有着与包豪斯的教学方法(追求设计中的一种普世而无名的表达方式)完全不同的背景。该练习旨在激发学生观察并研究设计史上成就斐然的设计师和建筑师的视觉语言。

Famous architects and designers in the past have always been recognized for the personal language in their work. This recognition has been the result of exceptional achievements that exceeded the stylistic canon of their time.

At the beginning of the 20th century, design and architecture were striving for a universal and non-subjective international visual language. In the twenties the designers wanted to retreat behind their work and let the products speak for themselves. The study of everyday culture, freed from personal ambitions, began and left its mark on design thinking at the beginning of the 20th century

Today there is a striving for individualism that has changed the ideological landscape in the field of design yet again. At the moment, designers’ names (the famous ones at least) have become household words that can be successfully marketed. The buyer follows and supports this by going in for well known brands and designs and this has led to an overvaluation of brand names and a regrettable neglect of anonymous and serious design work

Personal language, generally accompanied by ambition and often with vanity, has become part of European design ideology and is a matter of permanent díscussion.

To enlarge your personal repertoire I have found that it is helpful to study the great masters, not for the sake of copying, but in order to understand the thoughts behind their work and to discover their stylistic mastery.

历史上家喻户晓的建筑师和设计师都以其在作品中体现的富有个人色彩的“语言”而被人熟知。而他们的作品之所以取得了杰出成就，就是因为其风格大大逾越了他们所处时代的所谓“标准”。在20世纪初期，设计和建筑领域都在寻求一种普世、非主观且国际化的视觉语言表达方式。

到了20年代，设计师们开始想从自己的作品中“隐退”，让产品自己做自己的“代言人”。20世纪初，抛弃个人主观意识，对日常的普通文化进行研究开始在设计圈内流行起来，并在设计思维领域成功占有一席之地。

时至今日，对个人主义的推崇，再一次改变了设计界原本的意识形态土壤。现在，设计师的名字(至少是那些知名人士的)已经变得家喻户晓了，并且拥有很高的市场宣传价值。消费者对此趋之若骛，乐于接受名牌的产品和设计，而这就会导致对名牌商品本身的价值估计过高，从而遗憾地忽视了那些无名的或较为严肃的设计作品。

个人语言通常都伴随个人意愿，也往往与自负和虚荣为伍。它已经成为欧洲设计理念的一部分，并成为设计圈内永恒的研讨话题。

为了扩展你的知识储备，我觉得向那些大师们学习是非常有帮助的，不是为了再复制，而是为了理解他们作品背后的思想，以及探索他们作品风格的秘密。

The training without giving enough emphasis to material and function and the method of only emphasizing enlightening students solely from form runs against the aim of industrial design. Prof. Lehmann's projects start from practice, exploring students' creativity and freeing them from the limits of form. 雷曼教授的训练课题从设计本质出发，开发学生们的创造力，使其抛开形式框框的束缚。它既是正确设计思维的引导过程，也是材料构造的认识过程；既是对工业化大生产的认识过程，也是模型制作的训练过程。
