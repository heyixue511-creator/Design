## Miesvan der Rohe andthe beauty of construction

Mies van der Rohe(1886 –1969) must be mentioned as oneof the fathers of modern architecture.After his work in Germany and his term as director of the Bauhaus, which he decided to close in 1933 because ofthe influenceof the fascists, he emigrated to the USwhere he became Director of the Armour Institute, later the famous IllinoisInstitute of Technology(IIT). He left his mark in Germany, for in 1927 he was in charge of the famous Weissenhof Estate in Stuttgart and in 1929 he was in charge of the German contribution to theWorld Exhibition in Barcelona, where he designed the Barcelona Pavilion, which has recently been reconstructed. In the USAhe has left many buildings that are considered monuments of modern architecture.

However it is not my intention to honour the work of a great architect, others have done that, but to find out what construction meant to him. Mies van der Rohe considered construction as “the incorruptible guardian of the spirit of its time, objective and free from individualism and fantasy”. In his eyes construction was “a fundamental aspect of architecture”and design. He used the material according to its structural function, making the construction transparent. He liberated architecture from mass and made it as lightas possible.Structural order was for him a philosophical concept, employing the same principles as “a whole from topto bottom, down tothe last detail". And, pursuing the rules of reason and rationality, structure, construction, and proportion, he developed it to an almost poetic quality, which in its reductionistaesthetic leaves us with a substantial heritage.

密斯·凡·德·罗(1886—1969)是现代建筑的始祖之一。他曾在德国工作，担任过包豪斯学校的校长。但由于法西斯主义的影响，1933年包豪斯学校被关闭。后来他移民到美国，担任阿莫尔学院(即后来著名的伊利诺伊理工大学)的校长。在德国他拥有自己显赫的工作成就：1927年他负责管理著名的斯图加特魏森霍夫住宅区;1929年他代表德国，负责在巴塞罗那举办的世界博览会工作，并设计了巴塞罗那馆，时至今日该馆已被重新修建。在美国，他设计建造了许多被誉为现代建筑学典范的建筑。

然而，我在这里并不是想歌颂一位伟大建筑师的功绩，其他人已经做过这些了。我只是想找出构成对他的意义是什么。密斯·凡·德·罗认为，构成是“一个时代精神永恒的守护者，客观独立、并完全不受个人主义和虚幻主义的影响”。在他看来，构成是“建筑和设计的基础部分”。他根据结构功能的不同来选择建筑材料，使得整个建造过程变得透明。他把建筑学从沉重的束缚中解放出来，让它变得尽可能地轻量化。结构秩序对他来说，是一种哲学理念，即“从顶部到底部，看作一个整体，抓住每一个小细节不放”。而且，他遵循合理性、结构、构成和最佳比例原则，并把它们整合成一个富于诗意的统一体，蕴含着还原论美学因素。他把这种富于表现力的艺术遗产留给了后人。

Buckminster Fuller, a holistic approach to design, engineering and building technology

The work of Buckminster Fuller(1895- 1983), an American engineer, embraced science, engineering, architecture, design, and art and has had great repercussions, especially in America and Japan. Very early he was considered one of those ingenious designers whose work stimulated many fields, not only design and architecture, but also teaching, logistics, medicine, the media, and molecular biology. He was the first to draw attention toour limited resources, inventing the slogan “Spaceship Earth”and paid attention to the social needs of under-developed countries. He made fundamental contributions to the theory of structures, designed beautiful “tensegrity” structures, and above all, is known as the inventor of the Geodesic Dome. Many of these have been built in the US andelsewhere and are a demonstration of mass production and building technology. Apart from his social and theoretical background, he was a very practical engineer, believing in the value of concepts, using models and experiments to make his structures understandable. He is the father of structural thinking and prototypical for the art of construction.
