## Akio Okumura: i flutte Akio Okumura is known for residential designs based on rational thought, including the installation of photovoltaic (solar energy) systems, and for his profound designs of other objects in the living environment, such as furniture and fireplace surroundings. This architect has long studied how we live. Okumura's macaroni design satisfies the teeth's desire for the density of al dente and the tongue's love of the smoothness of noodles. The rising crest of a wave is designed to be cooked just enough to retain a somewhat firm texture, while the part of the wave that tumbles down upon the shore is cooked soft and smooth. His solution generates both of these textures simultaneously. We can easily imagine their graceful dance in soup, and the bite-size penne are easily eaten with a fork. Truffle Pasta is the attendant recipe. The word “truffle” symbolizes elegance, grace and exclusiveness. If this pasta is ever commercially produced, both the macaroni and its recipe will surely whet our appetite the most.
