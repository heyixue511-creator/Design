# VICTORIAN THINGS ## ASA BRIGGS ## Victorian Things ## Asa Briggs Victorian People and Victorian Cities, Asa Briggs's earlier studies of nineteenthcentury Britain, are now firmly established as classics. Remarkable for their breadth of scholarship and their readable style, they were a major influence behind the resurgence of interest in all things Victorian. Victorian Things, the product of many years' research and reflection, thus completes a trilogy. Through a series of linked studies, it presents an entertaining yet profound analysis of Victorian material culture and values. What do commemorative objects tell us about the values of the people who made and bought them? What was the effect of the camera's invention on the way people looked at things? How are such industries as coal, iron, and paper connected within the great Victorian web of capitalist enterprise? These are the kinds of questions explored through such material manifestations as museums and exhibitions, spectacles and cameras, commemorative artifacts, home furnishings and decoration, matches, hats and other headgear, coal, postage stamps, telephones and typewriters. The book may be read simply for the vast panorama it gives of the Victorian world, but it also raises important theoretical issues concerning the meaning of objects, cultural anthropology, and the development of taste. Asa Briggs has been professor of modern history at Leeds University and professor of history at the University of Sussex, where he was Vice-Chancellor from 1967 to 1976. Lord Briggs was made a Life Peer in 1976 and became Provost of Worcester College, Oxford, later in the same year. Since 1978, he has also been Chancellor of the Open University. He is the author of many books ón nineteenth and twentieth century social and cultural history.
