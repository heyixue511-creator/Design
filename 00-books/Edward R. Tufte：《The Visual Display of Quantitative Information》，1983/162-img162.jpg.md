The way to increase data density other than by enlarging the data matrix is to reduce the area of a graphic. The Shrink Principle has wide application: ## Graphics can be shrunk way down. Many data graphics can be reduced in area to half their currently published size with virtually no loss in legibility and information. For example, Bertin's crisp and elegant line allows the display of 17 small-scale graphics on a single page along with extensive text.
