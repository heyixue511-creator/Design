In the word “exformation," the prefix is changed from “in" to“ex," reversing the meaning of “in." The meanings of “ex" include "not," "out of," “outside,"“eliminated," “prior"”and others. This is the source of the concept of converting the known to the unknown. Notice that “exterior" is already widely used as the counterpart to “interior," so my coined term, “exformation," may well make sense to the general public. ## Exformation—1 THE SHIMANTO RIVER The Object of the Process After I demonstrated the idea to the students, we looked for a theme through which to practice exformation. Any theme will do, be it the Orient Express, the Champs-Elysées, water, a refrigerator, or a honeymoon; anything we make light of with preconceptions, thinking to ourselves, "Yeah, it's probably like this..." is a potential theme. We talked over a lot of possibilities and settled on the famous Shimanto River, known as one of Japan's clearest and most beautiful waterways. Because the Shimanto River is acknowledged throughout our country but is not world famous, I would like to explain a little about it. Japan is a country more than 90% of whose landmass is covered in mountains and at the same time, a country full of sharply inclined and fast-flowing rivers.
