![](images/99c59f7da0a921d61b892bfc54b3bcffdcaa7a82c4aeab1eaa5ad9e9b1fe5df8.jpg) ▶InterDesign, locomotive design for ASEA-Traction 1985 # interdesign (founded 1982 Stockholm, Sweden) ![](images/f971045af0bf8c8f1403db83b8082d5b2cdc6f599ab141dd406f9a1fd4597612.jpg) In 1982, Tom Ahlström (b. 1943)and Hans Ehrich (b. 1942)established InterDesign as a subsidiary of their other design practice, A&E Design, which had been founded in Stockholm in 1968. This earlier design consultancy was highly celebrated for its design for disability and won numerous awards for its life-enhancing and ergonomic products. InterDesign was established for the development of more industrial-type products – from transportation to packaging to consumer products. In 1997, the venture became an independent industrial design consulting company and has since become one of Sweden's leading industrial design practices. Although mainly specializing in plastics, InterDesign has worked in a wide range of fields and materials. It has designed sanitary ware for Gustavsberg (1984), a locomotive for ASEA-Traction (1985), a range of toothbrushes for Colgate(1986), automatic injection devices for SHL Medical (1997), as well as dental floss and tooth pick dispensers for Jordan, electric circuit breakers for Norwesco, and lighting fixture components for Belysia. In 1992, InterDesign received the Swedish Environment Design Award for its returnable milk bottle (1992)designed for Arla. In the development of its product solutions, Inter-Design uses state-of-the-art computer-aided design (CAD) systems to rapidly determine product structure, and stereolithography to validate the ergonomics, aesthetics and integration of components – all before tooling is engaged. InterDesign states: “Designing the first product for a company is not without consequences. Designing the second and subsequent products means implementing a design strategy that includes product image in perfect coherence with user ergonomics. The whole must satisfy and attract the direct client (the distributor) as well as the client of the client (the end user). This is the way to build fidelity, notoriety and competitive differentiation." InterDesign's work exemplifies the Scandinavian approach to industrial design – the meticulous development of qualitydriven product solutions that are informed as much by human needs as by material, technological, and commercial concerns. ![](images/18b2d3d75f61f9180257a34074bab85b83960c218b66beae3b4fe14786fedd5e.jpg) ![](images/ae35cd26a9b92e458c317fce9f253ef105ba8339d971a9cf950f8f43d4bbd14a.jpg) 4InterDesign, toothbrushes for Colgate, 1986 4InterDesign, Peninject autoinjector forScandinavian Health, 1995 ∇InterDesign, returnable milk bottle for Arla, 1992 ![](images/4943ff5c9f88250bfc311d80de96b4795ea79ed39d9843f7848c3f203af102ba.jpg)
