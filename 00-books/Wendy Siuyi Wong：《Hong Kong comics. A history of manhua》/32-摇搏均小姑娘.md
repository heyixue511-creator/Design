## 92.

## Sing Tao Night Daily Pictorial Supplement, 1957

This Sunday newspaper supplement published translated American comics, work by Chan Chi-dor, Lee Ling-hon, Kwan Shan-mei, and Ding Gong, and a column, Good Children, by Wu Sin, who later headed the Sing Tao Newspaper Corporation. She used the name Big Sister Lily to relate to her readers and established a kid's club, the Good Children Sing Tao Club.

## 93.

## Youth Paradise Bimonthly, 1957

This half-illustrated, half-text variety magazine was targeted at teenagers and addressed topics of interest to more mature children. The cover and children's song section were drawn by the editor of Children's Paradise, Law Guan-tsiu.

## 94–97.

## Friends of Children's Bimonthly, 1957

This publication, edited by Cheung Yat-keui, one of the creators of Children’s Paradise, was targeted at elementary school children. Its contents were quite similar to that magazine's, although its drawings were somewhat fess appealing.

## 98–99.

## Little Companion Pictorial, 1960

One of the many imitators of Children's Paradise, Little Companion was aimed at kids in the middle elementary grades. Writers included Wu Shu-yu, whose Little Dumb Elephant was clearly influenced by Disney's Dumbo. Other contributors included Tang Chik-kin, Wong Chak, and Chi Sing.

## 100–106.

## Children's Weekly, 1960

Published every Saturday, this was the first full-color, tabloid-size children's newspaper in Hong Kong. Weekly features included news, art, stories, letters to the editor, comics, games, and stories contributed by readers. In addition to comic strips and stories, it published interviews with working mothers and their children. Children's Weekly was a quick best-seller, reaching a circulation of6,000 to 7,000 within a short time. It struggled after its first few years; in 1963 it became a booklet-size biweekly and then folded altogether in 1966.

## Little Friends Pictorial, 1959

Little Friends was one of the three best-selling children’s magazines in the late 1950s, along with Children's Paradise and Children's Corner Pictorial Fortnightly. Its most popular columns included Panda Baby, Grandpa and Grandchild, Fanfan and Little Brother's Quiz, and The Story of Siu-keung. Each issue also featured a center spread drawn by Hui Guanman. Little Friends folded in 1992, after 33 years and more than 700 issues.
