Shuko distanced himself from the contemporary tendency towards splendor and things that originated on the continent and discovered an aesthetic within wabi. Joou Takeno, a philosophical successor to Shuko, advanced the quest for a uniquely Japanese perception, in which a simple form projects the complexities of human inner nature. For example, in contrast to the richly ornamented ivory Continental chashaku (tea powder scoop), a simple scoop carved from a piece of bamboo delighted Takeno.
