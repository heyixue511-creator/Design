Like most foundries, Deberny & Peignot had grown by amalgamation, the result of a merger between the enterprise begun by Alexandre de Berny (the son of Balzac's mistress), then under the direction of Robert Girard, the designer of Astrée (1921), with the concern founded by Gustave Peignot in the 186os. Gustave Peignot died in 1889, and his son Georges initiated a spirited programme of type designs by contemporary graphie artists, first Eugéne Grasset, whose ugly type which bears his name (1898) was followed by those of Georges Auriol (1863-1938). Auriol (1901-4) is the only art nouveau face which can be read in long texts, and combined with the ornaments from the same hand in the books on typography by François Thibaudeau still looks fresh and lively. After this, Georges Peignot's later commissions were a disappointment, typified by Naudin (1913-20), based on the lettering of the illustrator Bernard Naudin.
