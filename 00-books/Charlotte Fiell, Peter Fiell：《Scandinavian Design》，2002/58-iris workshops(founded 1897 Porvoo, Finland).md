The founding of the Iris Workshops by the Swedishborn furniture designer, Louis Sparre(1866–1964) and the celebrated Swedish-Finnish artist Axel Gallén (1865-1931), who later changed his name in 1905 to the more Finnish sounding, Akseli Gallén-Kallela, together with the Anglo-Belgian ceramicist Alfred William Finch(1854–1930), a friend of the celebrated Art Nouveau designer Henry van de Velde (1863–1957), must be seen within the context of the strong nationalistic movements that were emerging in Scandinavian countries during the 1890s, which stressed a romantic involvement with nature and the historic past.
