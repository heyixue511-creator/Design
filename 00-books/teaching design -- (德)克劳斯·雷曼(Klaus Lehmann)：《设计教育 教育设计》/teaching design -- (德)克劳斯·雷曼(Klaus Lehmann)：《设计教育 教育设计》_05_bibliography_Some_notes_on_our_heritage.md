## Some notes on our heritage

The heritage of the Bauhaus is to be found in modern design teaching everywhere. The workshop, or laboratory, a central part of Bauhaus teaching, was the playground for experiment in the acquisition of manual skills and played a central role for generating new ideas. It is still a part of our educational system.

Asked about what was the most stimulating element in their studies, former students always name, alongside their individual teacher, the workshop as the moststimulating field for innovation and the vital contribution to their personal development. Modern media have naturally changed the creative process, but the reliance on the computer as the sole tool seems to me to be an impoverishment and represents a grave loss. Modern research on the workings of the brain andon the nature of creativity in general seem to underline this position and the demand for a new practical theory of education. That is a great challenge. My personal teaching experience leads me to believe that the workshop or laboratory, where the learner works with materials, tools, and machines, cannotbe replaced by the computer. “Hands on”is a vital part of artistic education.
