While culturally backward as a whole, Russia in the first decade of this century di have a smal avantgarde that in many respects was better informed about development in the West—particularly the work of Matisse and Picasso—than were many of its contempora ies in Paris. Cubism and Fauvism were certainly strong early influences on the work of Kasimir Malevich, whose Suprematism promulgated the ideal of a purely abstract painting.
