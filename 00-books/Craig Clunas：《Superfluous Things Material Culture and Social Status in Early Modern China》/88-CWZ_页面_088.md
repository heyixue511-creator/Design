The individual words had their Ming meanings at least by the late Bronze Age and were in use as a pair of complementary opposites by the later Han dynasty. In the case of ya, however, this was a late meaning, following on from its use as a name for one of the genres i n the earliest collection of verse, the Book of Songs.<sup>22</sup> The grammarians of the Han gloss the word as equivalent to zheng, 'regular', 'proper'.
