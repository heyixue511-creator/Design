## Recapitulating:

—Geometry, first of all, is a practical tool that allows the designer to com municatein a precise manner. It is the basis of technical drawing, that everydesigner or architect must master. It is the language that takes us between the conceptual idea and its reproduction, between architect and builder, between designer and engineer.

—Geometry is a means of generating shapes through spatial manipulation, which is the intention of the following exercises. It therefore represents a practical tool for many design problems.

—Studying the relationship of geometry to aesthetics reveals the spiritual cosmos of number and shape in European philosophy and its repercussions on design ideologies.

—Mathematics and geometry are,beyond their practical use, a wonderful training ground for abstract thinking, plausible reasoning, and distinct patterns of thought.

". . . when society is organizing itself, it cannot be done without the ordering principals ofgeometry."(Werner Oechslin)
