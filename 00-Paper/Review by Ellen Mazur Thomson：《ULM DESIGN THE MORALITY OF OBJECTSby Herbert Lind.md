Lindinger has structured the text and illustrations in Ulm to and content. He divided each page horizontally. The upper portion contains extracts from documents, in-house magazines, and other publications, as well as interviews and essays written by participants during the life of the school. Here too are photographs of the studios, of classes, students, faculty, and examples of their design. The upper section is arranged in chapters following the school's curriculum, the basic course and five departments. On the lower portion of the page, in distinctive blue type, contributors in the present examine the past. In the form of essays, interviews, and editorial statements, they analyze the political, social, and intellectual context in which Ulm operated and, in effect, create a dialog with it.
