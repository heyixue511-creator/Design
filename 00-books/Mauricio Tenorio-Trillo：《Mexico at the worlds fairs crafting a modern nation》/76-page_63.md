Most of Mexico's regional and national elites were at some point involved with Mexico's presence at world's fairsfrom Francisco Madero (the father), who was a distinguished personality at Paris in 1889, to Justo Sierra in Atlanta's exhibition of 1895 and Paris 1900. 89 By the end of the Porfirian era this team was so specialized and so capable of producing a complete image of the nation in a very short period of time thatas I will show in chapter 11even the first revolutionary government requested its help when attempting to join San Francisco's Pan-American world's fair of 1915.
