![](images/734954b6f63263ece7daa096ed5ccdb058e557f809a223d9704f3a90ca347b00.jpg) ## Panasonic Design Company: Gel Remote Control For the HAPTIC Exhibition, the electrical appliance manufacturer Panasonic Design Company proposed a remote control that when switched off droops as if it were dead. Have you ever seen the melting clocks of the Spanish Surrealist Salvador Dali, with one hanging limply in the branch of a tree? The Panasonic remote control looks like that when it's off. But when it's switched on, it becomes animated. First, it begins to breathe, as if asleep, its belly rising and falling, resuscitated from death to breathing life. When someone reaches out to use it, the sensor instantly feels the hand's approach and the whole thing begins to glow. Then it stiffens. Precisely attuned for use, it solidifies to the perfect hardness. A concept model, this remote control is not ready for commercial production, but it's a unique idea. The focus in this design is not on its appealing form, button layout or logo placement, but the way it feels to the touch. This is a great example to show how, with the birth of the haptic focus, the breadth of design's possibilities expands in unexpected directions.
