FriedI Holzer-Kjellberg, FK-Service tableware for Arabia, 1953 ![](images/189e034226ce958adfd32ff277d3fe4a488643c71f57c2afb9d7fe1a745c8130.jpg) # friedl holzer-kjellberg (active in Finland, b. 1905 Loeben, Austria – d. 1993) Born in Austria, Friedl(Elfriede) Holzer-Kjellberg studied at the Kunstgewerbeschule (School of Arts and Crafts) in Graz. After her formal academic training she moved to Helsinki and in 1924 began working as a designer for Arabia, for whom she was eventually appointed artistic director in 1948. Her ceramic designs for the company were awarded a gold medal at the 1935 Bruxelles “Exposition Universelle et Internationale" and a silver medal at the 1937 Paris “Exposition Internationale des Arts et Techniques dans la Vie Moderne”. She experimented for ten years before perfecting her famous rice-pattern technique, which involved the meticulous cutting of rice-like patterns into an extremely thin porcelain body so as to leave delicate “open” incisions. These were then covered with a colourless glaze, which produced areas of subtle translucence. Holzer-Kjellberg's exquisite and extremely delicate rice-pattern series reveals the influence of the art of Japan, which was widely felt across the whole of Scandinavian ceramic design in the 20th century. Comprising numerous bowls and a tea service, when first launchedin the early 1940s the rice-pattern tableware was an instant success. It remained in production with Arabia until c. 1974, but due to popular demand was re-introduced in 1986. Apart from her porcelain products, Holzer-Kjellberg also designed a number of one-off stoneware pieces. Having won a gold medal at the 1954 (X)Milan Triennale and the Pro Finlandia Medal in 1966, she continued designing for Arabia until 1970. ![](images/16f330477085e353925736bb4184219f46fca53948ac857c1e1b8679582742b2.jpg)
