# A note on Produc Semantics

Product Semantics entered design discussions in the eighties, throwing a new light on the role of the designer and the role of the product as a means of communication. The subject caused uneasiness among members of the design community as they realizing that what was considered good and timeless design was far away from the needs of consumers, who saw more in the product than only its practical side. It was the introduction of life style into design thinking, described by the French sociologist Pierre Felix Bourdieu (1930– 2002) among others. It alsostimulated a new view of consumers, who it was realized were not a homogenous community but a set of groups with different expectations that depended on their social background and education. Consumers were categorized into target groups and their lifestyle became a matter of market research and intensive design consideration.

The topic was founded in the theory of science, where semiotics, the science of signs, and semantics, dealing with signs and their meanings, became an important part of it. The theory of semiotics goes back to Charles Sanders Peirce(1839– 1914), Charles William Morrisin the US(1903–1979), and to Max Bense in Germany(1910 – 1990). Jochen Gros and Bernhard Buerdek developed it into a practical tool for designers in the eighties, calling it product language.

Product language deals with the non-verbal communication between user and product, showing how it can be used in formal design to make the product understandable to the user. Abraham Moles speaks about the readability of the world'. The basic idea behind this is the realisation that the best products are self-explanatory and do not need a manual to use them(this is the objective of the Push-Pull-Turn-Exercise).

Apart from formalfactors, shape, structure, proportion, direction, texture, or colour i. e. formal language, the use of metaphors has been introduced and widely used in what became post-modern design. Uri Friedlaender, GB, Winfried Scheuer, Germany, Reinhard Butter, USA, Michael Mc Coy, USA, Susann Vihma, Finland and others have described it and made it popular in the design profession. 产品语义学的概念于20世纪80年代进入到设计学领域并为设计师和产品作为信息传递方式的角色带来了全新视角的解读。产品语义学在设计界众多人士中引发了不安的情绪，因为他们意识到，他们眼中的优质而永恒的设计与消费者的需求相去甚远消费者们需要的远不只是产品本身的实际用途。法国社会学家皮埃尔·菲利斯·布迪厄(1930—2002)认为，产品语义学将生活方式引入设计思维之中。产品语义学同时也提供了全新的消费者视角，即消费者群体不再被视为同质化的群体，而是基于社会和教育背景不同的、具有不同期望值的若干群体。消费者被分为不同的目标群体，而他们的生活方式就成为了市场调研以及设计思维的重要议题之一。

该话题的基础是科学理论，其中主要研究符号及其具体含义的符号学和语义学是至关重要的部分之一。符号学理论最早由美国的查尔斯·桑德斯·皮尔士(1839—1914)、查尔斯·威廉姆·莫里斯(19031979)以及德国的马克斯·本译(1910—1990)提出。在20世纪80年代，约肯·格罗斯以及伯恩哈德·布尔德克把该理论发展成为可供设计师们使用的一种实用工具，被称为产品语言。

产品语言指的是用户和产品之间的、无需用言语表达的一种交流形式，通过外在的设计向用户展示使用方法，从而让产品的相关信息更容易被用户所理解与认同。亚伯拉罕·莫尔斯提出了“可读化世界”的说法，其基本要义是，人们已经意识到，最好的产品具备自我推介的功能，不需要说明书、手册之类的东西(这是“推拉转”练习的目标)。

除了形态因素，如形状、结构、比例、方向、质地或色彩(即形态语 言)之外，隐喻的手法已经引入到后现代设计领域中，并且得到了广泛 应用。英国的尤里·弗里德兰德，德国的温弗里德·舒尔，美国的莱茵 design object in order to give it a deliberate expression. Metaphorical design must be seen in close relation to post-modern design, that as an antimovement to functional design, calledfor more emotionality inthe product. In the words of McCoy it is “an expansionof the possibilities for imagery in design beyond the implied limits of Bauhaus Modernism”. Though controversial in its intention, strongly dependent on cultural understanding, and difficult to apply, I would like to describe some principlesof metaphorical design:

—decodable mechanical prínciples recall practical experience and are translingual and trans-cultural.

—symbols of human or animal gestures are part of a universal body language. —familiar abstract symbols originated in a particular culture but have become universal signs through a process of globalisation and communication.

—symbols of technology, the master models of technologicalachievement, have become globally understood metaphors.

—emotional qualities of materials appeal to our senses of touch and feeling and give us sensual experiences.

—stylistic or historic metaphors are embedded in the culture in which they originated.

"Product semantics is not a new style. It is a serious study of the meanings that emerge in human interactionwith objects.”(Klaus Krippendorf)

——可解码的机械化原则替代实践经验，并呈现跨语言化和跨文化趋势。

—人类或动物手势的符号表达被认为是可被普遍采用的肢体语言之一。

—熟悉的抽象符号起源于某一特定文化之中，但通过全球化的发展和交流的不断推进，已经成为被普遍接受的符号形式。

技术符号是技术发展过程中最主要的模型，且已成为被全球广泛接受的隐喻形式。

——材料的情感特质，可以激活感官，带来感性体验。

——风格化的或历史性的隐喻深藏于其发源土壤的文化之内。
