The photo on the right shows a temporary shelter made from paper tubes designed by Shigeru Ban at the time of the Great Hanshin Earthquake. ## Shigeru Ban and Toilet Paper The subject I gave the architect Shigeru Ban was toilet paper. Ban is known around the world for designing buildings using paper tubes, and he has genuine reasons for this approach. Ban has found that paper, which superficially appears fragile, actually has enough strength and durability to be used in permanent buildings. More importantly, he has focused on its flexibility as a building material that can be manufactured in simple, inexpensive facilities. Because these facilities impose only a small burden, paper tubes can be produced anywhere in the world. They conform to universal standards as well so that the same tubes can also be procured anywhere. And when they are no longer needed, they can be recycled. Ban focuses on the many latent elements of this material that could be vitalin the times ahead. Just after Japan's Great Hanshin Earthquake in 1995, Ban designed temporary housing as well as a church using these paper tubes. For a refugee camp in the Republic of Rwanda he lobbied the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees and convinced them to use paper tubes as structural material for the shelters. Using wood instead would have immediately depleted the forest resources, and shelters too sturdily built would have attracted permanent refugees. Paper tubes were best suited for the structures of simple tent-like shelters. At Expo 2000 in Hanover, Ban built the Japanese Pavilion using paper tubes as well, which made possible a gigantic arched space several dozen meters high. This pavilion was based on the concept of recycling the tubes as soon as the Expo was over. All of these projects allow us to appreciate his clear vision in realizing necessary architecture based on universal and rational ideas while avoiding the squandering of resources. The next project to be tackled by this architect, working with these concepts, was toilet paper.
