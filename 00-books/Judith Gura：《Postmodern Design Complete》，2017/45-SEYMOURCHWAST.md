# SEYMOUR CHWAST b.1931 NEW YORK-BORN DESIGNER KNOWN FOR HIS VIVID ILLUSTRATIONS AND CARTOON IMAGERY. As co-founder of Push Pin Studios, one of the best-known graphic design firms of the midcentury vears. Seymour Chwast was a key player in the development of graphic design as a valid means of artistic expression and as a sophisticated communications tool. He pioneered the transition from strict realistic imagery and Swiss formalism to an altogether different type of art, using cartoons and visual puns, linking illustration and typography, and paving the way for what would blossom into postmodernist graphic design, In a career encompassing more than six decades, he has produced thousands of influential designs in magazines, posters, book jackets, album covers, advertisements, packaging, and children's books. Much of Chwast's early work anticipated Postmodernism's rejection of established norms, avoiding the conventional look of advertising and package design that had prevailed since the 192os He replaced it with a vocabulary of simple forms, bold color, and almost always humor, to make a point beyond what was written in the headline. He went on to study illustration and design at Cooper Union, where he met his future partners. Chwast's early jobs included stints at the New York Times, Esquire, and Condé Nast, but the three classmates decided that opening their own studio held greater promise, and established Push Pin in 1954. The partners collaborated on design projects but worked individually on editorial illustration. Their publication, the Push Pin Graphic, was begun in i957 initially as a promotion piece, but it became an influential periodical that presented radical new ideas to the design community. It continued for twenty-three years and a total of eighty-six issues, becoming a bimonthly in 1976 and ending publication in ı98o. Even with the diversity of design emanating from Push Pin, Chwast's own hand is almost unmistakable. His work reflects influences from Victorian, Art Nouveau, and Art Deco styles, filtered through a contemporary sensibility but undeniably rejecting conventional modernist aesthetics. His eye-catching posters helped to elevate the genre to a collectible art form, and throughout his career he has produced countless sketches, drawings, and limited-edition prints.
