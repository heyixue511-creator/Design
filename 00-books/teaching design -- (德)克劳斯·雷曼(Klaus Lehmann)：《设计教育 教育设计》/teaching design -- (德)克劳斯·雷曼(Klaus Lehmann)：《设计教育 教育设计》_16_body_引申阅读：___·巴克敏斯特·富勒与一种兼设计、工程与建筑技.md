Buckminster Fuller, a holistic approach to design, engineering and building technology

The work of Buckminster Fuller(1895- 1983), an American engineer, embraced science, engineering, architecture, design, and art and has had great repercussions, especially in America and Japan. Very early he was considered one of those ingenious designers whose work stimulated many fields, not only design and architecture, but also teaching, logistics, medicine, the media, and molecular biology. He was the first to draw attention toour limited resources, inventing the slogan “Spaceship Earth”and paid attention to the socíal needs of under-developed countries. He made fundamental contributions to the theory of structures, designed beautiful “tensegrity” structures, and above all, is known as the inventor of the Geodesic Dome. Many of these have been built in the US and elsewhere and are a demonstration of mass production and building technology. Apart from his social and theoretical background, he was a very practical engineer, believing in the value of concepts, using models and experiments to make his structures understandable. He is the father of structural thinking and prototypical for the art of construction.

## ·巴克敏斯特·富勒与一种兼设计、工程与建筑技术于一身的全能方法

美国工程师巴克敏斯特·富勒(1895—1983)的作品将科学、工程学、建筑学、设计学和艺术融为一体，在世界各地，尤其美国和日本，反响强烈。在很早以前，他就被认为是最富天才的设计师之一，他的作品为许多领域提供了灵感，如设计与建筑领域、教学、物流、医药、媒体以及分子生物学等。他第一个将目光投向我们世界上有限的资源，发布了口号“地球是一座宇宙飞船”，另外他还关注着不发达国家人民的社会需求。他为结构理论的发展做出了重要贡献，独创了一种美丽的“张拉整体”结构。除此之外，他还发明了“网格球顶”结构。带有这些结构的建筑已经屹立在美国和其他国家，充分展示了大众制造和建筑技术的发展成就。除了他的社会和理论背景之外，他还是一位实践工程师，相信理念的价值，并使用各种模型进行多种实验，使他创造的结构更加为世人所理解。他是结构性思维之父，是构成艺术家的典型。

## Charles Eames-pioneering technology in furniture design

Much can be said about Charles Eames(1907– 1978), perhaps the most important American designer. There are so many facets to his and his wife's work that they cannot be dealt with in these short notes on construction. We will look at only one aspect, and that is his approach to furniture Design. Eames was fascinated by technologies and was most creative in finding new ones. Very early, about 1943, he experimented with plywood and inventedthe “Kazam!”machine, which enabled him to bendplywood in three dimensions. He turned his newly found technology into structures that he then used for his chairs. He introduced fibreglass technology into furniture making, designing seat shells around 1949, and aluminium castings for the supporting structure in the fifties and sixties. His innovations are marked by a strong interest in technology, but his greatest achievement is that he turned the new technologies into appropriate and pleasing shapes. His mastery of material and construction can be read from the sophisticated structural details of his furniture, and it is still on the market today!
