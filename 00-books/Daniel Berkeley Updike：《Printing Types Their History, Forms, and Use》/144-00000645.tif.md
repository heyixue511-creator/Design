At the end of the seventeenth and the beginning of the eighteenth century, what was the condition of printing at Paris ? There was a certain Pierre Jacques Blondel who, about 1724, wrote a sarcastic memoir on Parisian booksellers and printers,1 which, though not, perhaps, to be taken too seriously, casts light upon the situation at that time. It is amusing to find the writer begin, as we are apt to do to-day, by telling about the wonderful old times of long ago.
