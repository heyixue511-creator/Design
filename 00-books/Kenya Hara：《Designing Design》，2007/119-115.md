![](images/619871e942b37177bac19ee1eb877939f4f212728d0ec98955c70eb232a5514c.jpg) ## Matthieu Manche: Mom'n Baby The French artist Matthieu Manche, who lives in Tokyo, designed an electric outlet. The outlet has several plugs, but as they're not arrayed geometrically, they evoke an organic image of proliferation through successive cell division. Manche used a material employed for artificial body parts involving the replacement of flesh, not just bones and muscles. These are called epitheses. This material is applied externally to supplement damaged or lost body parts like breasts and ears. An expert in crafting epitheses helped finish the outlet so that it has a startling, fleshlike texture, one that can hardly be imagined from its lovely appearance. The first sample actually looked too realistic to be exhibited, so the final product was colored in somewhat abstractly, like a cute Kewpie doll.
