Instead of discussing every aspect of each design introduced, an approach that might quickly become tedious, I have chosen to treat design in a series of essays, with each chapter developing one theme. As no object is dealt with exhaustively, I must leave it to the reader to pursue the other themes that arise from any particular design. Although the chapters should each stand on their own, they are intended, taken together, to suggest the significance of design in our culture and the extent of its influence on our lives and minds.
