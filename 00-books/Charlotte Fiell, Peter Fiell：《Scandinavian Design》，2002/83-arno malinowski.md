Lindahl, from 1914 to 1919 and then studied at the Kongelige Danske Kunstakademi (Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts) between 1919and 1922. From 1921 to 1935, he designed various ceramic objects for the Royal Copenhagen factory, including a number of scantily clad porcelain figurines, which featured oriental blanc-de-chine glazes. His figurines such as the De fem verdensdele (The Five Continents), Islandsk pige (Icelandic Girl) and Bruden (The Bride) were produced as limited editions and were remarkable for the delicacy of their modelling. In 1925, he also decorated a dinner service, the Neo-Classical form of which was designed by Christian Joachim and was adorned with painted and gilded animal motifs, such as a leaping ram and a rather curious heron-like bird in the Art Deco style. This tableware was shown at the 1925 Paris “Exposition Internationale des Arts Décoratifs et Industriels Modernes” and was awarded a Grand Prix. Malinowski also produced a number of stoneware pieces for the Royal Copenhagen factory, which all had a robustness that contrasted strongly with the extreme delicacy of his porcelain designs and which were based on abstracted natural forms, such as seedpods or fruit. In 1933, he was awarded the coveted Eckersberg Medal and three years later began designing for the Georg Jensen silversmithy. Malinowski's well-known pierced silver Faun and Butterfly brooches (1959) epitomized not only the strong linearity and graphic quality of his work, but also that of the Art Deco style in general. Malinowski also designed the silver and enamel Kongemærke (King's badge brooch) emblem commemorating the 70th birthday of King Christian X that was worn as a sign of patriotism by literally thousands of Danes during the Second World War. The form and decoration of Malinowski's work revealed the pervasive influence of Modernism in Scandinavia during the 1920s and 1930s, while the exquisite refinement of his designs – whether in porcelain, stoneware or silver – demonstrated his superlative craftsmanship and masterful handling of materials. ![](images/c48375004d635609e1bbaa7426d8c072ea74f64ba2ddb70bb00de8e5e3ff5acc.jpg)
