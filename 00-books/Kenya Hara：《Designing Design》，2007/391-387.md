The fish presented as an object to be stepped on will arouse a conglomerate of whirling senses, including our tactile perception. If this were instead a fish on a plate, other sensory perceptions would be aroused. I've mentioned this interrelation of the senses in the “Haptic" chapter. In short, this team's unique perspective is that it tried to present the river as an object to be stepped on. I think a method for realistically portraying the sense of touch will evolve hand in hand with the sophistication of technology, but the research done by this team artlessly foretells the future of haptic interaction.
