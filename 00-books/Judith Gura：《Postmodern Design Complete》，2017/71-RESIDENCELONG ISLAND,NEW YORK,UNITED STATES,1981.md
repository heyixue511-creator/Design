# RESIDENCELONG ISLAND,NEW YORK,UNITED STATES,1981 ROBERT A. M. STERN POSTMODERN MEETS SHINGLE STYLE IN A TIMELESS BEACHFRONT VACATION HOME. For a beachfront home in New York's fashionable Hamptons resort area, the firm of ROBERT A. M. STERN (also see under “Architecture") breathed new life into the traditional shingle style of early twentieth-century vacation homes with this highly refined interpretation of Postmodernism. The result: a house that is clearly historicist in its inspiration, but just as clearly contemporary in its execution. The main feature of the boldly symmetrical form is a tall arched window facing the water, evidence of what the architect describes as the building's "Palladian pretensions." The design and siting consider both the location, at the crest of a dune, and the desire to maximize light and views. An overscaled stoop leads up to the main floor and provides a perfect front-porch site for viewing the sunset, while the primary living rooms face a generous expanse of sea and sand. The master bedroom, on the top level beneath the high pitched roof, also looks out on the water, through a grand-scale arched window. Beautifully sited in a clearing in the middle of a 3o-acre (ı2-ha) lightly wooded site, this house designed by Venturi, Scott Brown and Associates is both a family residence and a showcase for a fine collection of Pop art paintings and Art Deco objects. Two shades of green brick in an Op-Deco pattern transgress the many-windowed mien of a conventional Georgian country house, in an assertive architectural statement underscored by the bold curve of the facade. On one side, a low flight of stairs leads up from the sunken auto court into a two-story gallery that displays some of the larger artworks. The gallery opens on to a series of other rooms, and to the garden outside. In the living room, a vividly patterned carpet shares the stage with artworks and Art Deco pieces, while admitting the view through the wall of windows, On the opposite side of the house, the kitchen overlooks a children's playroom and the library.
