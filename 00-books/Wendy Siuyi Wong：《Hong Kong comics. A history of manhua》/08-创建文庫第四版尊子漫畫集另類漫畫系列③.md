## 81-82.

Black Material Comic Collection, 1989

Art/Script: Zunzi

In this book, Zunzi explored the student democracy protests and Tiananmen Square crisis of June 1989. He divided the comic into several categories—historical background, politics, etc.—and accompanied each with explanatory text. Any discussion of Tiananmen Square was firmly suppressed on the mainland, so the topic was considered sensitive, or “black material," in Hong Kong.

## 83-85.

100 Caricatures of China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan   
Politicians, 1993   
Art/Script: Zunzi

These caricatures of Chinese, Taiwanese, and Hong Kong politicians include key mainland figures such as Deng Xiao-ping, Mao Ze-dong, Li Pang, local politicians Martin Lee and Chris Patten, and Taiwanese leaders Jiang Jie-shi and Lee Dung-wei. Each caricature is presented as an individual postcard, with the politician's year of birth and title on the reverse. The postcards are perforated, so they can be removed from the book and mailed.

## 86-88.

Zunzi’s Manhua: Hong Kong, China, UK, 1994 Art/Script: Zunzi

The editorial cartoons in this collection were selected by the author from the period between 1991 and 1994. He takes a strong prodemocracy position and often satirizes the hard-line attitude of Chinese government toward political reform and human rights.

## 89–91.

Grandpa Deng I, 1994

Grandpa Deng II, 1997

Art/Script: Zunzi

The main character of this strip, originally published in Next Magazine, is Deng Xiao-ping, portrayed as a chubby, retired “grandpa" still actively controlling China through younger colleagues such as Li Pang. In addition to Grandpa Deng, other key political figures appeared in the strip, which satirized the incompetence of the officials and ridiculed their actions. The strip ran for about six years, until Deng's death.
