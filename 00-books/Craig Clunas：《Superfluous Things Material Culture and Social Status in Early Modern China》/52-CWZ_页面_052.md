It is, in effect, a total inventory of the property of a man named Yan Song ( 1480- 1 565; DMB, 1 586-9 1 ), who as a Grand Secretary from 1 542 to 1 562 was one of the effective leaders o f the national p olitical elite. Having long been a focus of hostility w ithin the o fficial world, he was dismissed from office and died in disgrace, his entire property being forfeit, as was standard, to the state .
