![](images/563f64250a86706e7ca441e7f93495fdd53960ca820ecaee6bd06b26bc4cdfdd.jpg) Stefan Lindfors, Boy glassware range for littala, 1999 Stefan Lindfors, Scaragoo table lamp for Ingo Maurer, 1988 # stefan lindfors (b. 1962 Maarianhamina, Åland, Finland) ![](images/aba4c47ce49a9028ec5d8294348a94239a4af55f59a990ea6cee46cff31b6d39.jpg) The Finnish designer and sculptor Stefan Lindfors studied interior and furniture design at the Taideteollinen Korkeakoulu (University of Art and Design) in Helsinki from 1982 to 1988. While still a student he received a silver medal for his work at the 1986 (XVII) Milan Triennale. After graduating, one of his first commissions was to design the News Studio for Yleisradio (the Finnish Broadcasting Company). In 1988, he designed his widely publicized Scaragoo table lamp, which was constructed of aluminium, steel, plastic and concrete. This curious insect-like halogen lamp, which is activated by touch, became one of the best known Post-Modern Scandinavian designs once it was put into production by the German lighting manufacturer Ingo Maurer in 1990. As a subversive design that blurred the boundary between functional objects and works of art, the Scaragoo led Lindfors to become widely recognized as one of the leading protagonists of avant-garde design in the late 1980s and early 1990s. In 1990, he designed the interior of the café at the Taideteollisuusmuseo (Museum of Industrial Arts), Helsinki, and three years later the interior for the Marimekko shop in Helsinki. Apart from his various interior projects, Lindfors has also produced numerous product designs for among others, Martela, Asko, P.O. Korhonen, Vitra, Skanno, Kalevala, Belus and Alessi. In 1992, he received perhaps the highest Nordic cultural accolade – the Väinö Tanner (Trailblazer) Award. Four years later, he created a sculpture entitled Winged Victory for the Swedish Pavilion at the Olympic Games in Atlanta, and also designed the “Freedom of Speech” sculpture exhibition at the Finnish Embassy in Washington DC. In 1997, Lindfors designed his EgO range of ceramics for Arabia and the following year contributed designs to the first collection of Hackman tools, for which he won a Design Plus Award in 1999 and a Good Design Award in 2000. By bringing a highly attuned artistic sensibility to the design of functional objects, Lindfors produces work that is quirky but nonetheless distinctly Scandinavian in character. ![](images/44d8c3d3cd9561386744fe1451e7dc3d8bbf08fd8c7eb431e88dc52bc2734977.jpg)
