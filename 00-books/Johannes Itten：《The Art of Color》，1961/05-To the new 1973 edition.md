# To the new 1973 edition When preparing the first edition of “The Art of Color” in 1960, author and publisher faced the difficult problem of finding the best way of reproducing the color. This could only be achieved by producing the various color illustrations and tables in different printing processes done by several printers - partly with special colors - and tipping them in the book. In 1970, when the students’ edition was planned, this complicated method of production could not be retained. Thanks to advanced technology, the printing quality of the students’ edition was in many ways an improvement over the large edition. Thus, a standard was set for a new large edition. For the present new printing, the entire production was adapted for offset. The reproduction of all the illustrations was done by Seiler & Jehle, Augsburg, without whose valuable collaboration the book could not have appeared in its present form. We gratefully acknowledge here this accomplishment, as well as the excellent printing by J. Fink, Stuttgart-Kemnat. All together, care was taken to leave the work untouched. Contents and sequence of pages have been retained, some of the illustrations enlarged and improved according to existing copies. The aim of the new edition is to make Johannes Itten’s color theory available again, improved and strictly adhering to his principles of presentation. Zurich, April 1973 Anneliese Itten
