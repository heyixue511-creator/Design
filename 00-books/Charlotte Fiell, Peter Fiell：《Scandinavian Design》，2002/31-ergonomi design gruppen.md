![](images/f6d0d7b1a8817f6d4fd74ad60814c60a10136158d313623d567f96c4e534155d.jpg) ▶Ergonomi Design Gruppen, welding equipment for Hörnell International, 2000 # ergonomi design gruppen (founded 1979 Stockholm, Sweden) Widely recognized for its work in the area of design for special needs, Ergonomi Design Gruppen (EDG) evolved from two Swedish design groups that had been founded in the late 1960s. In 1979, Designgruppen and Ergonomi Design officially amalgamated to form the 14-member Ergonomi Design Gruppen, based in Bromma.The group is dedicated to the research and development of safe, reliable and efficient design solutions based on ergonomic principles for the working environment and handicapped people. It studies usersystem problems bybuilding full-scale models to evaluate designs in experimental situations, and collaborates with medical experts as well as patients to hone its solutions. Ergonomi's most notable design for (dis)ability is the Eat/Drinkprogramme of cutlery, drinking vessels and plates (1980), which was manufactured by RSFU Rehab – a private company that was initially set up as a grant-aided foundation to advise on and supply various medical aids. The Eat/Drink range was designed so that itcould be used by people who had only single-hand function. The programme's development and manufacturing costs were subsidized by grants from various insurance companies and public institutions, so that itcould be sold as inexpensively as possible. Indeed, had it not been for this private and public funding, the programme would never have got off the ground as no independent manufacturer could be found that was prepared to produce this landmark realneed design. Apart from RSFU, Ergonomi Design Gruppen has also been commissioned by other state-funded bodies such as the Institutefor the Handicappedand the Work Environment Fund to research and develop products for particular disadvantaged sections of society. The group has also designeda number of award-winning products for industrial manufacturers, including the Genotropin Pen for Pharmacia & Upjohn, an ergonomic coffee pot for SAS, the Ergo range of hand tools for Sandvik, a baby carrier for Baby-Björn, and an ergonomically-resolved stapler for Esco. Among the founders of the group, Maria Benktzon (b. 1946)andSven-Eric Juhlin (b.1940) both undertook research in 1972 into muscular ability and its relationship to the actions of gripping and holding, and have since specialized in design for disability. Ergonomi Design Gruppen is guided by the belief that, “Design is not only appearance. ![](images/da5ac4b73d47650b1e8846405d1c3d111df918fe98efb9dc652ef782ee706598.jpg) ![](images/b2ff964a92822a0a8d9eb0f79cfdcc460893d23aa6fc885c0916c15fa75d5975.jpg) It is form, function and economy. Conscientious product development can increase sales, reduce production costs, open new markets and improve quality profile. Design becomes a strategic tool for success." Ergonomi Design Gruppen is also renowned for machines, tools and protective gear designed to reduce the risk of repetitive strain injury and accidents. Central to its work is the idea of social inclusiveness – a philosophy that is at the core of Scandinavian and in particular Swedish society, where there is a progressive approach towards the provision of services and products for various disadvantaged groups of people. Like the advanced welfare state found in Sweden, the origins of this ethical approach to design can be ultimately traced to the Christian doctrine of Evangelical Lutheranism, which stresses reason and moral rectitude.
