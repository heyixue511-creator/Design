# ZANONE RESIDENCE MEMPHIS, TENNESSEE, UNITED STATES, 1985 MEMPHIS POSTMODERN FURNISHINGS TAKE CENTER STAGE IN THIS COLLECTOR'S HOME. This otherwise unexceptional midcentury house is furnished with what is surely one of the most impressive collections of MEMPHIS (see under "Edited Design") furniture extant. Even more impressive is the fact that photographer Dennis Zanone lives with all of it, every day, without any desire to trade, sell, or make use of it for any commercial purpose. "I just love this stuff," he says happily. Zanone first saw the furniture in i984, in his hometown, when the inaugural United States tour of the first Memphis collection came to what was then the Brooks Museum of Art (now Memphis Brooks Museum of Art), and was immediately captivated. Since then, Zanone has been a single-minded collector without a particular cause, except to sing the praises of what he feels are thoroughly admirable and functional objects that should not be dismissed as mere attention-seekers. He sleeps in the Tawaraya boxing ring couch, speaks on an ETTORF SOTTSASS (also see under “Masters of Postmodern Design") telephone, sits comfortably in the Bel Air chair, and stores books and objects in colorful angular shelves and cabinets. His solution to the problem of finding coordinating furnishings is not to try; he avoids anything that might compete or distract from the collection—meaning no curtains on the windows, no carpets or area rugs, and only an occasional postmodern graphic on the wall. It works perfectly: the Memphis objects look very much at home in the company of their peers. Zanone's living room has been photographed many times and featured in countless publications, eliciting both admiring and critical comments. Unfazed, he understands and accepts that not everyone shares his taste.
