![](images/2b72f75c685a1cee6912690b46af02bdc27e1db81a6b5fab45edcf3eff6adbae.jpg) ## MayumiMiyawaki: PunchingMacaroni Mayumi Miyawaki says that if an architect is designing macaroni, it should show in the pasta's shape. His proposal calls for punching out a solid square pattern, an intelligible form that bears some mark of the architect. On the other hand, he has set its thickness at one millimeter with due consideration to the final appearance of the cooked macaroni; it would waver voluptuously on the plate and be plenty ready for the sauce. Miyawaki has designed a great number of residential buildings. According to him, the architect's occupation destines him to design buildings one at a time, each of which is a first experience. Therefore, when he was offered an opportunity to design macaroni, which used to be only a food to him, he was happy to take it on. Anyone engaged in residential design has to be extremely familiar with the kitchen and with cooking. Miyawaki wanted to make a crucial contribution to the project, so he proposed not only the macaroni design but also its recipe, Quattro Formaggi, which satisfied “Chef Kogure"as well.
