## The Folding Exercise

Folding is a very elementary process, turning a flat piece of paper, cardboard, a sheet of metal, or even plywood, if you apply hinges to it, into a threedimensional object. It is related to construction, for it creates a stable structure, a space saving object, an artistic expression, an ornamental object, or a sculptural effect.

Design a stable construction from a flat material, cut and joined by hinges, that first forms a flat package, but when unfolded you could eithersit or lie on or use as a table or shelf.

The folding exercise has many authors. I was inspired by WernerSchmidt, an architect in Switzerland, who made it part of a workshop at the State Academy in Stuttgart and whose results were shown at the Furniture Fair in Milan.

I have adapted the idea to basic resources for my workshops in China. Its objective is to experience construction, function, human factors, and aesthetics. It should provide the opportunity to ask a great number of questionsand to discover new criteria.

## Approaches:

Cardboard or foam-core sheets joined by adhesive tape are a good start. Concentrate on their structural and aesthetic qualities. Encourage new technologies and applications. Open the mind for new discoveries.

## ·折叠练习

折叠是一种非常基本的操作过程，即将平整的纸张、纸板、一张金属片甚至胶合板(需安装折页)变成一种三维立体结构。它与构成息息相关，因为它创造了一种稳固的结构、节省空间的形体、艺术化的表现手法、具有装饰性的物件或者是具有雕塑感的效果。

使用平整的材料设计一种稳固的结构，切割，并用折页连接，这样首先就做成了一个平整的包装。而当拆开它时，你可以坐在上面、躺在上面，或把它当作桌子或架子使用。

关于折叠的练习，我的灵感来自许多人。比如华纳·施密特，一位瑞士建筑师。他把这项练习作为斯图加特国立学院工作坊实践的一部分，而后，实践的成果在米兰举办的家具展会上展出。

我已经在我的中国工作坊的基础资源库中适当采用了这项练习的做法，目的是让学生们体验构成、功能、有关于人的因素以及美学因素的作用。它会给学生们提出大量问题，以及发现新标准的机会。

方法：

由胶带连接的纸板或者泡沫板即可。集中注意力观察它们的结构与美学质量，并鼓励应用新的技术应用成果。广开思路，探索新发现。
