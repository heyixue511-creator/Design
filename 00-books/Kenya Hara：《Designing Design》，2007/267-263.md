The photos show extreme cases, yet imply the possibility of rational storage management. Further developing this idea in practice, we designed the "wooden house" as a tangible product, with a vaulted ceiling from the first floor through the second. The idea of a “concrete house" was also entertained. Although they're both actual houses, it's better to think of them not as a largescale product (house) but as a lifestyle produced by editingthe entire range ofMUJI products, from flooring to walls to doorknobs, lighting appliances, spigots and storage tools. Just as ingeniously created daily goods nurture health in our lives, a congregation of MUJI products will produce a certain form of living style. With this goal, we have to reconsider our entire family of products. When we're finished,I believe that we will have arrived at a form for our lifestyles, a "house" that's congenial to Japan today.
