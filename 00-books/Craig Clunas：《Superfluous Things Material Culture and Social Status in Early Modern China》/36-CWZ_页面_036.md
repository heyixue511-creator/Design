This lengthy diversion into the byways of bibliography may not seem to be strictly relevant, but its importance to the main discussion can be briefly summarized. The eighteenth-century editors of the Imperial Catalogue were quite correct to point out the dependence of Wen Zhenheng's Treatise on Superfluous Things (c. 1 6 1 5-20) on Tu Long's Desultory Remarks on Furnishing the A bode of the Retired Scholar ( 1 606).
