## 221-224.

## Monthly Comic Magazine, 1990 Editor: Yuan Kin-toa

Although it claimed to be Hong Kong's first pure manhua magazine, it was actually the first serious critical magazine about manhua. It included a mix of reviews and commentary about Hong Kong manhua and reports on foreign comics. There were also features introducing new manhua artists such as Li Chitat, whose elaborately detailed drawings were praised by readers. Unfortunately, this rare example of a sincere magazine about manhua was short-lived, publishing only five issues in 1990 and three more in 1993.

## 225.

Tin Ha Magazine, 1991

Editor: Cola Boy

This magazine was also about comics, although it had a less serious tone than Monthly Comic Magazine. Named after Ma Wing-Shing's comics company Tin Ha Publishers, it reported manhua and manga industry news and discussed comics trends. It lasted for three years before folding in mid-1993.

## 226-227.

Humorous Cartoons by Ma Long, 1991 Art/Script: Ma Long

Ma was an active cartoonist for various daily newspapers. This book collects the single-panel cartoons he drew in 1991; about 132 cartoons are included. The collection does not include his satirical works.

## 228–229.

Daily Pictorial, 1991

Editor: Unknown

This newspaper began just as the success of comics dailies was fading. Its aim was to print local comics, entertainment news, and the latest Japanese manga. Although it began with a big promotional splash, it only lasted for three days.

## 230.

Double Star Comics, 1993

Editor: Law Wing-hon

The young artists who produced this comic book were more interested in promoting themselves than making money. It was influenced by Japanese manga, but it had a more lighthearted, humorous tone.

## 231.

Comic Alley, 1993

Editor: Seung-gun Siu-keung

This magazine was led by Seung-gun Siu-keung, an experienced artist from the 1980s. He helped new artists experiment with and improve their drawing skills, and allowed the magazine to publish alternative and avant-garde comics.
