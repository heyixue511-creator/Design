Paul Overy describes Kandinsky's early work, and then goes on to discuss his relationship to the contemporary European art movements, up to his death in 1944. The main body of the book is devoted to a discussion of those of Kandinsky's paintings which developed out of his novel ideas on composition and communication, and the author draws on his sometimes difficult writings to illuminate their meaning. He shows how Futurism, Cubisın, Fauvism, and Expressionism paved the way for a break with the past, and how Dadaism and Surrealism were a bridge to the present. In doing so he provides a basic understanding of Pop Art, New Abstraction, Hard-Edge, Op Art, New Tendency, Programmed Art and Happenings. A guided tour through the jungle of modern art, and an original, intelligent and exceptionally well-illustrated one.'
