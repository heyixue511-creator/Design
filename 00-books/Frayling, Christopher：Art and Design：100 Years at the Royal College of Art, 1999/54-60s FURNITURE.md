# 60s FURNITURE During the 1960s, the RCA became the centre of British Pop art and design. This was partly due to the increasing interaction between different courses, which led to a convergence of the fine, graphic and applied arts, and to a more experimental and innovative approach to both fine art and design. The generation of students at the College in these years revolutionized the visual currency of the day, introducing youth, fun, sex and fashion to the high street. One of the RCA pop groups of the period coined the phrase Cool Britannia’, but it was meant as a joke. Students turned away from traditional notions of what constituted good design’to experiment with different materials, forms and uses in furniture design.
