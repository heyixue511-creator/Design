# ŠÍPEK HOUSE AMSTERDAM, NETHERLANDS, 1988 BOŘEK ŠÍPEK INNOVATIVE TOWNHOUSE THAT KEEPS CLOSE COMPANY WITH CONVENTIONAL DUTCH BUILDINGS. A true original needs a house that's something special, and this one that architect and designer BOŘEK ŠÍPEK (also see under “Architecture") created for himself and his family, in the city where he spent much of his time, fits the bill perfectly. The bright exterior makes the first statement, its geometric form suggesting an exaggerated, modern take on a traditional peaked-roof cottage. Cheek-to-cheek with its neighbors, the house has only front and rear exposure, but its tall glass-paned door and windows and large skylights ensure that maximum light floods the interior. Throughout the house, whimsical touches reveal the humor that pervades this designer's work: abstract floral cut-outs on the narrow doorways, a sky-and-clouds painted vault in the dining room, and a rear hall floor enlivened with a broken tile pattern. The kitchen, however, is resolutely serious, a showplace of stainless steel and white tile that is too beautiful to mask from view; it opens directly into the dining room.
