## THE PROJECTS Here's how the first year of the exformation project took shape as the “Exformation Shimanto River." Simulations—If the RiverWere a Road Produced byShinsaku Inaba, Sousuke Matsushita and Hirofumi Mori This team worked on image simulations. Their final plan concentrated on creating composites of asphalt roads on the water's surface by taking pictures of various scenes up and down the river. Our memory of the size and texture of a familiar object functions as a measuring stick by which we can infer the size or shape ofsomething new or unknown to us. The photograph of a dinosaur fossil next to a cigarette pack sets up a standard for the fossil's dimensions. The same is true of our memories of asphalt roads. Without realizing that we're committing anything to memory, by virtue of repeated expe- ![](images/5eb91e398fd44fe50b28bbc3227a1eab3f83427ce17a2ced6437b721a691da63.jpg) riences of moving down the road in a car, we recall the textures of road surfaces, the width and length of the white line, and the margin between the lanes. These memories seems ambiguous yet are more precise than we expect. The white lines on the road are not only a boundary but also act as a scale of perception, helping the driver sense his own speed and the distance between his car and the car in front of him, from the speed and changing pitch of the lines moving along in his field of vision. This team concentrated on the asphalt road as an object with which to measure the environment and used it as material for a visual rhetoric with which to illustrate the reality of the Shimanto River. At its head, the river's current has the width of a white line on the road, soon growing to that of a one-lane road. As soon as it comes to the middle reaches, it suddenly begins to meander.
