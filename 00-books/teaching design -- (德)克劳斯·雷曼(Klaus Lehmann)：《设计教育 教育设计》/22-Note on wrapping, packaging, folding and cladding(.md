# Note on wrapping, packaging, foldingand cladding (linked together but treated separately for better understanding)

Wrapping is a very elementary process to protect goods and gifts. Apart from its protective function its main function is decorative. Eastern tradition shows a variety of outstanding creative handmade wrapping with leaves, bamboo, strings, or paper. The Japanese book “How to wrap five eggs” has been an eye-opener to the Western design community.

Packaging demands additional functions. Packaging must be manufactured industrially, must be economical in the use of material, must be environmentfriendly, and after use should be returned to the cycle of materials. Food and gifts are usually wrapped in paperor cardboard, but also appliances, coffee machines, computers, hair dryers, and even technicalparts. Packaging has a temporary function and is discarded when its contents have reachedtheir destination. Packaging has become a vital selling point, for it is the first thing the consumer encounters and can be decisive in supporting the desire to buy its contents. The packaging industry is a huge field for the creative designer.

Folding is a very elementary process, turning a flat piece of material(2 - dimensional) into a spatial structure(3- dimensional). We employ folding when we form a paper plane from a sheet of paper and give it a new function. But there are innumerable examples of new products having been generated by folding. Investigating the many possibilities and approaches, combined here in 2 exercises, is a rewarding fieldof research, which can be carried out using moderate means.

Cladding is another field of practical design, i, e. the encasing of components to give a permanent shape to a product. Usually the casing is made from a durable material; sheet or cast plastics or metal and forms the skin-the durable and permanent shape of the product. Cladding, treated in this way, can open new views of a classical design problem-the work on the shape and its meaning.

“包裹”是保护商品和礼物最基本的操作步骤。它除了具有保护功能，还主要具有装饰功能。东方国家的传统中，有多种多样、精妙绝伦的手工制包装形式，如使用叶子、竹子、绳子和纸张等。对于西方设计界人士来说，日本人的一本书《如何包装五个鸡蛋》绝对让他们大开眼界。

“包装”包含了更多功能，也提出了更多要求。“包装”必须通过工业制造进行，在材料的使用上必须方便经济，整个过程必须环保，使用后，相关材料应该可回收，可被循环利用。食物和礼品、家用设备、咖啡机、电脑、吹风机甚至技术设备零件都通常使用纸张或纸板进行包装。“包装”发挥的功能只是暂时性的，当其所包装的物体展现出来时，它的使命也就结束了。“包装”目前已经成为商品至关重要的一项卖点，因为它会给消费者留下第一印象，并左右消费者的购买意愿。“包装”产业是富有创新性的设计师施展才华的广阔空间。

“折叠”也是一种基本的操作过程，即将平整的一片材料(二维)变成一种立体结构(三维)。当我们将一张纸变成一架纸飞机时，我们就采用了折叠的手法，赋予纸张以新功能。然而通过折叠，还会产生很多新的产品，相关的例子不胜枚举。在本书这两个练习中，我们调查研究这些可能性与操作方法，这是一种很有意义的研究模式，可以通过适度的手段来达成。

“覆层”是实际设计过程的另一领域，即将某种材料的镀层附在产品表面，以使其永久保持外形。通常情况下，产品表面永久性的覆层都是用持久耐用的材料制作，如金属板、塑料等。用这种方式制成的覆层可以为解决长期以来的设计难题—形状与内涵，提供新的解决视角。
