![](images/b4853212ada2a6ce94386d7dc6f2311c5acf9696acfc65695f1812cdb09cdc90.jpg) ## LINDA PARRY Linda Parry trained as a textile designer at Liverpool College of Art, then completed a post-graduate degree in Art History at the Central School of Art and Design in London. Her first Museum appointment was at the City Museum and Art Gallery, Birmingham, in the Art Department, with special responsibilities for the nineteenth century collections. Since 1971 she has worked in the Textile Department of the Victoria and Albert Museum, specializing in Embroidery and European Textiles from the second half of the 19th century. Since September 1988 she has also been responsible for the Museum's twentieth century textile collections. She has written numerous articles, catalogues and other publications including William Morris Textiles (1988) and Textiles of the Arts and Crafts Movement, a companion book for the 1988 exhibition of the same name organised by her for the Museum. ## GILLIAN MOSS Gillian Moss is the Assistant Curator of Textiles at the Cooper-Hewitt, the Smithsonian Institution's National Museum of Design. She has organised exhibitions, written catalogues and lectured widely on textiles.
