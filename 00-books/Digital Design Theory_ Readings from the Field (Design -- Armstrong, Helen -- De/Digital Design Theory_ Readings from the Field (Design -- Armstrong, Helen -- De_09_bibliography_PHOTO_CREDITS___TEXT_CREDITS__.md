## ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Helen Armstrong views design from across the spectrum as a practicing designer, college professor, and published author. She is an associate professor of graphic design at North Carolina State University. In addition to teaching, she wrote Graphic Design Theory: Readings from the Field (Princeton Architectural Press, 2009) and cowrote, with Zvezdana Stojmirovic, Participate: Designing with User-Generated Content (Princeton Architectural Press, 2011). She also works as principal of her own company, Strong Design. Her design work — for such clients as Johns Hopkins, T. Rowe Price, and Euler ACI— has won regional and international awards. Her work has been included in numerous publications in the United States and the United Kingdom, including HOW International Design Annual, The Complete Typographer, and The Typography Workbook.
