Which is to say that the important factors of the charm of sleeping outside are those that enhance sleeping itself, like the right amount of sunshine and wind; companions or friends; the pleasant touch of the grass and a beautiful green landscape. Once aware of these facts, the team collaborated in creating a portable napping tool they named "NAP." This was designed not for lodging or camping, but for a short-term outdoor activity—afternoon napping. After making their NAPs, they went out to nap, on the moor of Hokkaido (Japan's northernmost main island); in a meadow, on a ship, on an urban rooftop; on a very large scale pier and on a university campus. Every nap is chronicled in photographs. There is no evidence that required recording, but this was a kind of "resort hunting." The resort they were lookingfor, and found, is expressed in their recumbent forms, snoozing contentedly.
