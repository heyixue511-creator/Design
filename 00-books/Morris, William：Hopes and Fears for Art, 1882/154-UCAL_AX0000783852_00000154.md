This is the relieving a pattern of more than one color, but all the colors light upon a dark ground. This is above all useful in cases where your palette is somewhat limited; say, for instance, in a figured cloth which has to be woven mechanically, and where you have but three or four colors in a line, including the ground. You will not find this a difficult way of relieving your pattern, if you only are not too ambitious of getting the diverse superimposed colors too forcible on the one hand, so that they fly out from one another, or on the other hand too delicate, so that they run together into confusion. The excellence of this sort of work lies in a clear but soft relief of the form, in colors each beautiful in itself, and harmonious one with the other on a ground whose color is also beautiful, though unobtrusive.
