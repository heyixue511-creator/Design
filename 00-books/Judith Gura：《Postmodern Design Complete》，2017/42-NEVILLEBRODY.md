# NEVILLE BRODY b.1957 BRITISH DESIGNER WHOSE EXPERIMENTAL IDEAS LED TO INNOVATIVE MAGAZINE DESIGN. Neville Brody has said that he was "drawing before I could even walk."'5 His first graphic design project, made in grade school, was a brand identity for an imaginary postal service. Choosing graphic design over other art careers, he saw its potential as a means of communication that could influence the way people think, and what they think about. Brody, however, declined to limit himself by specializing in a single area. He has worked in print and digital, branding and environmental design, and typography. In all these he has been more insurgent than compliant, discarding traditional printing techniques to experiment with type and visual effects. The result has been a career that made him one of the best-known and most innovative designers of his generation. Brody attended Hornsey College of Art and the London College of Printing, opening his own ![](images/7626bc50f74f970ced72d711d43de7606a208eb079068a89ef75a88db4bffb4d.jpg) studio shortly after leaving. His first assignments were album covers for trendsetting bands like Depeche Mode and Cabaret Voltaire, and these highly visible projects led to assignments in other fields. Though influenced by predecessor art movements like Dada and Constructivism, Brody shaped an aesthetic of his own. Brody is most often linked with The Face, an iconoclastic publication launched in i98o that documented London's burgeoning youth culture, reporting on the commingled art, fashion, celebrity, and music scenes and becoming a trendsetting instrument in itself. The Face spoke to a generation that disdained the standards ![](images/1071505c899f078e24565b51d673e3792fa57c8bd7d72ae064bd2efe27e28d5c.jpg) of establishment tastemakers, adopting an aesthetic that was more mass-market than upper class. As the magazine's art director from i98 to i986, Brody upended the conventions of stuffy periodical design, with hand-drawn or mixed-up typefaces and oddly cropped images. The in-your-face, punk-influenced style established an unforgettable visual identity that became the prototype of what a modern magazine could (and should) look like. Following this, Brody became art director of another hip publication, Arenα (i987–9o), and has since worked on several others. Brody founded The Studio, his Londonbased firm, in ı987 and began working with computers to continue his ongoing exploration of typography and graphic design. The Graphic Language of Neville Brody, published in 1988 in connection with a retrospective exhibition at London's Victoria and Albert Museum, became a bestseller. In 199ı he launched the FUSE project, publishing experimental typefaces and posters and organizing conferences dealing with new concepts in typography and communications. In 201o Brody was named Dean of the Royal College of Art's School of Communication.
