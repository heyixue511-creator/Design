# ABCD EFGH IJKL MINOP QRST UVW! XYZ& Roman Extended. First shown by George Nesbitt in his 1838 specimens. This design was cut by the Page Company. Lowercase and figures are missing. This design was most popular during the first half of the century. The earliest specimens were more extended than the one shown here, and they did include a lowercase which is believed to have originated as wood type. Caslon cast an expanded Roman in 1835 which did not have a lowercase.
