A design that is fairly good but not excellent will never motivate the dynamics of commercial production or the market. The survivors in today's macaroni market are strongin design, masterpieces that have been supported andwon their place either on the plate at home or for the efficiency of their production. By dint of their happy passion in pursuing flavor, the Italians have sustained the evolution of macaroni. The various macaroni shown in the photos above are available around the world today. Seeing them afresh, I was profoundly moved. As I have said, some very strong design lurks in the objects around us that have gone unnoticed. Even architects, full of curiosity and intelligence, who see themselves as professionals skilled at problem solving, find no easy way into the worldof macaroni. If we were to trace the long path from production of the grain that is its raw material to the moment when steam is rising from it on the plate, we would surely find a “macaroni tale” on a global scale. This realization was the fruit of our project.
