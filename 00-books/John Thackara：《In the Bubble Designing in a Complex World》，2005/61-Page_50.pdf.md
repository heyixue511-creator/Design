Abla moved, but not at ease. She really had difficulties in following my drumming. Drum and dancer did not harmonize. ‘‘Stop!’’ Ali shouted, ‘‘you are wrong.’’ ‘‘Yes, I know,’’ I said. ‘‘It seems to me that Abla just started to hate me. Shall I play it a little bit slower or faster?’’ ‘‘No,’’ Ali replied, ‘‘you should not play faster or slower, you should play right. I know,’’ Ali said. ‘‘Following the machine is the best way to play exact, which also means to be always wrong. There cannot be a fit, as long as you look at Abla from the machine’s point of view.’’<sup>63</sup>
