# 80s ILLUSTRATION By the 80s the Illustration Department had garnered an international reputation. In 1981 there were over 15 exhibitions by past and present students, and in 1987 seven international exhibitions took place in cities as diverse as New York, Colorado and Hong Kong. Students began to embrace international horizons, travelling widely and engaging in illustrative reportage. Quentin Blake's influence continued to be felt. Each fresh intake of students was treated to a cliff-top assault course in Hastings followed by wine and chips at Blake's seaside home. The appointment of Dan Fern as Professor in 1986 brought new perspectives: students learned the tradition of designing and making items such as trade union banners and Turkish paper marbling, and were given studio presentations by performance artists. The work being produced by student illustrators was, according to Dan Fern, largely apolitical, noisy, urban, crowded and colourful, often executed on a large scale'.
