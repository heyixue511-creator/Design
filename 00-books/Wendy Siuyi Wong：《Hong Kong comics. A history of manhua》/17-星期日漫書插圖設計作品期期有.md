## 181.

Ms. Carefree, 1974

Art/Script: Ng Gam-hong

This comic reflected changes in how women were seen in Hong Kong; it features a modern, sexy female character influenced by television programs such as Charlie's Angels. The strip first appeared in a newspaper and was later reprinted as this booklet.

## 182.

New Wave Manhua Magazine, 1980

Editor: Tony Chan

In response to the “new wave” of Hong Kong film, a group of young artists, including Ma Wing-shing, Chu Jo-yee, and Sham Lak-hung, published this magazine with the hopes of creating a new kind of manhua. The most popular comics of the time were violent, fighting manhua, so this magazine gave Hong Kong comics a new and fresh perspective.

## 183-185.

Siu-keung Manhua Collection, 1981

This collection, put together by Seung-gun Siu-keung and a group of young artists, includes Sau-sing Chai (Longevity Boy), by Seung-gun, and the comic-book version of Jackie Chan’s film Drunken Master, by Ma Wing-shing. It also published the work of many rising artists. Since most comic books were collections of strips that had appeared in penny papers, the cover stressed that the contents had never before been published. Covers also used imagery from popular films such as Alien to attract readers.

## 186.

Humor World, 1981

Editor: Yeung Wai-pong

The majority of this publication's contributors were newspaper or editorial cartoon artists, including Tao Sam, Yat Muk, Zunzi, Yan Ting-hau, Mok Wah, Au Ching, and Lee Tak-hong. Its content was mostly cartoon strips, almost all drawn in à single-line style.

## 187.

Manhua New Trend, 1981

Editor: Cheung Wai-kit

One of many short-lived humor magazines, this title combined four-panel strips and short stories. The cover of the first issue used caricatures of movie stars to try to appeal to new readers.

## 188–189.

Sunday Comics, 1982

Editor: Ho Tai-yi

This variety weekly collected cartoons, illustrations, and comics. Although it was created by local artists, it's contents revealed how much Hong Kong manhua were becoming influenced by Japanese manga. Major contributors included Chu Jo-yee, Tang Punbong, Ko Cheung, and Fung Chi-ming.
