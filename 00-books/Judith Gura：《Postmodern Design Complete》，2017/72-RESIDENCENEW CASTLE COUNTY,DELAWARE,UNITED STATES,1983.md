# RESIDENCE NEW CASTLE COUNTY, DELAWARE, UNITED STATES, 1983 ROBERT VENTURI AND DENISE SCOTT BROWN VERNACULAR FORM AND POSTMODERN DETAIL COMBINE IN AN INVITING MUSIC-CENTERED HOME. Every project has special requirements, but the specifications for this house by Venturi, Scott Brown and Associates were particularly challenging. The wife, a professional musician, needed a music room that could accommodate an organ, two pianos, and a harpsichord, and which would also provide space for informal parties. The husband needed a study in a quiet part of the house, and both required large windows facing the woods to facilitate their avocation of birdwatching. Located at the edge of a valley, the house sits in rolling fields in an area where traditional fieldstone barns are a familiar sight. The architects drew on this vernacular form to make the house fit comfortably into the rural setting and suit the clients' unpretentious lifestyle. At the front, a Palladian-inspired crest sits on the gabled roof, announcing its postmodern credentials, and a similar element on stylized two-dimensional columns is grafted onto the rear facade. Inside, the conservatory takes center stage, with a soaring double-height space framed in pastel-trimmed, pointed arches. In the center of the house, the dining room is a light-filled pyramid-shaped atrium, punctuated by windows and cut-outs in the walls. The other rooms are comfortable, unpretentious, and altogether wellsuited to both the house and its occupants.
