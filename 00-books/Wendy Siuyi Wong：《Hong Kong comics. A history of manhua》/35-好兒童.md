## 158–162.

## Assorted pirated Japanese manga, 1977–79

Unauthorized reprints of Japanese manga, which sold for HK\$1, were very popular with children. The various titles—including Candy Candy, Ultraman, The Unbeatable Big Warrior Robot, and Robocon—targeted various age groups and both girls and boys. Most of these pirated comics did not credit the original artist or publisher.

## 163.

## Cute Girl, 1976

This short-lived picture magazine was named after a character in an ad for a Hong Kong noodle company. The two main characters, Cute Girl and Ox Boy, were based on traditional Chinese mythology. They act as “hosts” throughout the magazine. Features included The Dream of Cute Girl, The Story of Ox Boy and Weaver Girl, as well as a page for parents.

## 164–166.

## Big Brother Sunny, 1978

This illustrated magazine was inspired by the popular children’s TV show Tiu Fei-gei and revolves around its host, Big Brother Sunny. The program's young cast members also appear in it.

## 167–168.

## Sing Tao Good Children Happy Land, 1982

This addition to the Sing Tao Night Daily followed the format of its predecessor, the newspaper supplement introduced in 1957. Like the earlier version, it included Letters to Big Sister Lily and features such as Friend ofGood Children’s Activities, and Little Reporters, as well as illustrated stories. Its main manhua contributors were Lee Wai-chun and Ting Shui-leung.

## 169.

## Proverb Animation Gallery, 1987

These illustrated stories were based on a television program that explored Chinese proverbs. In each episode, a proverb was introduced, and its origin and meaning were discussed by the main characters, a panda scholar and a red robot named YY. After the series ended, the stories were collected into a bilingual book.
