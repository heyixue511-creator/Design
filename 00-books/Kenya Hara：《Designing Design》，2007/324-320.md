Their arrangement of wildflowers in the alcove illustrates only that the space is an empty space that is tended with care; the flower arrangement does not perform as an addition, or a decorative agent. The concept of nothingness as value is also accomplished with simple furnishings, which accentuate this place where the only ambient sound is the wind rustling the trees. Then, next to the sitting room of each suite there is the Japanese bath, always full of hot water. This cypress tub, filled to the brim for the purpose of savoring the garden from the water, reflects the trees as in a mirror. As the body slips in, wavering trees and water flow together over the rim Mukayu has no noise-emitting entertainment facilities. Instead, it has a library that is absolutely perfect for reading. Because the library also faces the garden, its light is from the green of the garden. All other light filters into the room through shoji (paper screens). When I stay here, I don't go out at all; I take pleasure in the quality of the empty time and space. It seems as if there must be other lodgings where we can do this, but in fact, there aren't many.
