When these doors are opened, the garden scenery evolves like an open picture scroll. The deep eaves that grace Togudo Hall with subtle shade, the light streaming through the shoji, the shoji latticework and the edging of the tatami mats: all effect a simple composition displaying one of the origins of Japanese space. This is why Dojinsai is a national treasure. In this room, Yoshimasa sipped tea and relaxed in tranquility. The founder of the tea ceremony, Murata Shuko, who was Yoshimasa's aesthetic advisor and communed with him through tea, probably visited him here.
