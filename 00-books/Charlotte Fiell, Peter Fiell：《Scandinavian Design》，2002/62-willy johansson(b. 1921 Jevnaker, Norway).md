![](images/04319d4d5f98a7aeb09787b080fb8eeba636d738b12be0b6d93ae2ddef2303a6.jpg) Willy Johansson, Atlantic vases for Hadeland, 1970 # willy johansson (b. 1921 Jevnaker, Norway) ![](images/d27793f97060bdecc3f6cf8c1ce0acec196191f42b0816e9d8b48a3e477544cc.jpg) Willy Johansson's grandfather came from the Swedish region of Småland, which was renowned for its production of high-quality glassware, while his father was a senior glassblower at the Hadelandglassworks. In 1936, Johansson followed in his father's footsteps when he began working in Hadeland's glassmakingworkshop. He later undertook formal artistic training at the Statens Håndverks- og Kunstindustriskole(National College of Art and Design) in Oslo from 1939 to 1942. After completing his studies, he returned to Hadeland and from 1942 to 1945 served an apprenticeship to the sculptor Ståle Kyllingstad(1903-1987) in the firm's sandblasting section. Johansson also designed glassware with decorative engraving for Christiania Glasmagasin from 1945 to 1947. He replaced Kyllingstad as head of the design team at Hadeland in 1947 and in so doing, became responsible for the design of both free-blown art glass and pressed-glass production wares. In 1954, Norway exhibited at the (X)Milan Triennale for the first time and Johansson was awarded a Diplôme d'Honneur for his glass designs, which included his well-known sandblasted vases that were blown into wooden moulds and had contrasting coloured “cores”. Three years later, he received a gold medal at the 1957 (XI) Milan Triennale for a group of clear coloured dishes that were exhibited in an “aerial” framework display specially designed by Arne Korsmo. The colour of these remarkable free-blown designs subtly intensified towards their outer edges, which were contained by Johansson's “trademark” opaque white rims. Like these large-scale designs, Johansson's bluegreen vase(1958) and hisMedoc decanter(1961) reflected his pursuit of simple forms and perfect proportions, which provided his work with a quiet monumentality.
