# JAVIER MARISCAL b.1950 SPANISH MASTER OF MANY DISCIPLINES. KNOWN FOR HIS WITTY AND COLORFUL DESIGNS. It is virtually impossible to label a designer whose career transcends disciplines. Bouncing between categories and styles with a jovial abandon, polymath Javier Mariscal has illustrated comics, posters, and books; designed textiles and a variety of other products from jewelry to furniture and ceramics; created paintings and sculptures; produced animation and multimedia works; and designed interiors. None of his designs reflects a style other than his own; all are agreeably extroverted, invariably colorful, and postmodern in their freedom of expression and their irrepressible humor. His best-known, and most copied, design was the friendly mascot Cobi designed for the 1992 Summer Olympics in Barcelona, which became so popular that it inspired a Spanish television series Mariscal was born in Valencia, Spain, and studied philosophy at the University of Valencia, followed by graphic design at the ELISAVA design school in Barcelona, graduating in i97ı, in the final years of the Franco dictatorship. His early work included a radical Pop art-style underground comic (i973) that was banned by the government, and a variety of graphic design projects. When Spain emerged into a less restrictive era, Mariscal designed a lively new graphic identity for Barcelona, a pictogram of the words BAR CEL ONA (bar, sky, wave). In i978 he designed a textile collection in dynamic, primary-hued patterns, and received commissions to decorate houses in Barcelona and Valencia. In 1989 he opened Estudio Mariscal, enlisting a team of illustrators, editors, animators, architects, and consultants, and broadened his activities to work on a variety of schemes, including visual identity and branding programs as well as logo design, editorial work, and corporate projects. He has collaborated with many other designers and architects, without losing the stamp of individuality that marks all his work. In addition to participating in a number of design exhibitions, Mariscal was given his first retrospective, "1oo Años con Mariscal," in i988 at La Lonja, Valencia.
