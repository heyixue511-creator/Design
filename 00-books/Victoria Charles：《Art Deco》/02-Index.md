This aesthetic movement came to embody dreams of industry and prosperity. In the whirl of the Jazz Age and the frenzy of the “Roaring Twenties”, the streamlined silhouette of the flapper girl was reflected in the architectural aesthetic of Art Decothe rounded curve was conquered by the androgynous straight line… Architecture, painting, furniture, and sculpture evolved into oeuvres enhanced with sharp lines and broken angles. Although fleeting and short lived, this movement still influences contemporary design today.
