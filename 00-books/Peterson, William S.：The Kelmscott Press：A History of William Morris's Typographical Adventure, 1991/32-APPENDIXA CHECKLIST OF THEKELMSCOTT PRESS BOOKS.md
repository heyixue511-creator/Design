# APPENDIXA CHECKLIST OF THE KELMSCOTT PRESS BOOKS ![](images/068b93b8f5ff07ca409155cd0f2263d0419589ae59c4036080e10b571eeec34a.jpg) HE following list includes all the books printed at the Kelmscott Press but not the ephemera such as advertising circulars. My A Bibliograply of the Kelmscott Press (Oxford, 1984) supplies much more detailed descriptions of each of the books. The books were all produced in one of several buildings in the Upper Mall, Hammersmith, except for Gotlic Architecture (no. 18), which was printed during the Arts and Crafts Exhibition at the New Gallery, London. Unless otherwise indicated, the books are printed in black ink only. The two sizes of Flower paper (16 × 11 inches and 16 × 22 inches) are designated Flower (1) and Flower (2) respectively. I have made no attempt to describe the initials, borders, and engraved title-pages in this brief list; suffice it to say that every Kelmscott book was decorated with wood-engraved ornaments designed by Morris, some much more fully than others. ## I.
