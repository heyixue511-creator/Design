## ·俄罗斯结构主义者：发现结构的象征性力量

1920至1927年间的俄罗斯结构主义运动虽然持续时间短，但影响力深远。亚历山大·罗德琴科在这场运动中起到了关键作用。他是俄国革命前先锋派的成员之一，1918年俄国革命后，他抓住了为全新的社会主义政权构建环境的机会。尽管目标并不十分明确，然而，生产者们认为构成是与工程有关的科技类术语，而另一些人则认为构成是一种艺术类术语，他们之间展开了激烈的理论性探讨，其本身也成为了一个富有趣味性的话题。一些激进分子建议卢德申科去理工学校进行学习，以在行业中发挥作用。卢德申科拒绝并坦言，艺术家不同于工程师，他们懂得如何欣赏建筑，并给予“艺术建筑师”一个全新的视角。

他把自己抽象而富于实验性的工作看作是一种关于材料、制作方法、色彩、空间、时间、形式和技术的研究。卢德申科一定是一位天才教师和精于摄影技术及凸版印刷术的全能设计师(用现代的话说)，他还擅长于激发学生为了无产阶级社会的早日实现而工作。虽然他的设计并未被大规模地做成成品，但是它们所流露出的道德风尚使他的作品变得真实。我知道，把卢德申科的故事放到一本关于构成的书籍中，是有一点跑题了，但是我必须说，俄国结构主义是一种艺术运动，它由关于工程和技术的幻想所驱动。他的作品富有强大的象征性力量，它所蕴含的语义价值远远超过其实用价值。

## Rietveld and his radical approach to mass production

Gerrit Thomas Rietveld(1888- 1964) was a Dutchcabinetmaker and later became internationally known as an architect. In 1919 he joined Holland's “De Stijl” movement, whose intention was to create an objective and universal language in art and design by omitting all personal manifestations. Its spiritual head was Theo van Doesburg(1883– 1931), who developed a theory called Neoplasticism by decomposing volumes into horizontal and vertical planes and reducing visual language to the primary colours of red, blue, yellow, and black, white, and grey. By doing this he wanted to keep the means of artistic expression pure and so create a new aesthetic. Many European artists belonged to the movement, of whom the best known is PietMondrian( 1872 – 1944).

When Rietveld built his“red-blue-chair”, which was only a prototype, he surely did not know that it would become an icon of modern design history. It showed the principles of Neoplasticism, using pure geometric elements and colour coding, red for vertical planes, blue for horizontal planes, and yellow for the exosed cut ends of the struts, and so related it to a virtual neoplastic space. Its elementary construction shows a radical approach to manufacture and mass production. Rietveld's constructions stimulated the Bauhaus designers and generations to follow.

## ·里特维尔德和他对于大众制造的激进手法

格利特·托马斯·里特维尔德(1888—1964)曾是一位荷兰家具木工，后来成为了国际知名的建筑师。1919年他加入了荷兰的“风格派”运动，该运动的目的是在艺术和设计领域，通过忽略所有个人主观的表征形式，创造出一种客观而普遍适用的语言形式。该活动的精神领袖是里奥·凡·杜斯堡(1883—1931)。他提出了一种被称作“新造型主义”的理论体系，该理论把立体分解成多个水平和垂直的平面，将视觉语言的表现形式简化为红、蓝、黄、黑、白、灰等基础色彩。通过这种做法，他试图将艺术表现的形式变得纯粹化，这样一来就诞生了一种全新的美学概念。许多欧洲艺术家都支持这次运动，其中属彼埃·蒙德里安(1872—1944)最为出名。

当里特维尔德构建自己的“红蓝椅子”，其仅仅是一个模型之时，他当然不会知道它会成为现代设计历史中的标志性符号。它完美地诠释了新造型主义的基本原则，使用纯粹的几何元素和色彩编码：红色代表垂直平面，蓝色代表水平平面，黄色代表裸露在外的支架杆切割末端，并将以上元素合并，建立起一个虚拟的新造型主义空间。其基础构成形式展示了一种可用于大众制造的激进手法。里特维尔德的构成方法得到了包豪斯设计师以及其后的追随者的认同，并赋予他们灵感。

## Miesvan der Rohe and the beauty of construction

Mies van der Rohe(1886 – 1969) must be mentioned as one of the fathers of modern architecture.After his work in Germany and his term as director of the Bauhaus, which he decided to close in 1933 because ofthe influenceof the fascists, he emigrated to the USwhere he became Director of the Armour Institute, later the famous Illinois Institute of Technology(IIT). He left his mark in Germany, for in 1927 he was in charge of the famous Weissenhof Estate in Stuttgart and in 1929 he was in charge of the German contribution to theWorld Exhibition in Barcelona, where he designed the Barcelona Pavilion, which has recently been reconstructed. In the USA he has left many buildings that are considered monuments of modern architecture.

However it is not my intention to honour the work of a great architect, others have done that, but to find out what construction meant to him. Mies van der Rohe considered construction as “the incorruptible guardian of the spirit of its time, objective and free from individualism and fantasy”. In his eyes construction was “a fundamental aspect of architecture” and design. He used the material according to its structural function, making the construction transparent. He liberated architecture from mass and made it as lightas possible.Structural order was for him a philosophical concept, employing the same principles as “a whole from top to bottom, down tothe last detail”". And, pursuing the rules of reason and rationality, structure, construction, and proportion, he developed it to an almost poetic quality, which in its reductionist aesthetic leaves us with a substantial heritage.
