On the one hand, the flow line from reception counter to waiting room, then to consultation room for a check-up, and then perhaps to the dispensary and finally to the payment counter, is quite clear cut. The role of signage is to indicate the flow line in such a way that it can be clearly figured out in the minds of visitors and patients. The red crosses laid out on the main points of intersection act as indicators as well as traffic control tools. The length of the trunk of a red arrow sign relates directly to the distance to the destination. After traversing the entire trunk, the patient stands before the door at the end of his journey, poised upon the arrow's head.
