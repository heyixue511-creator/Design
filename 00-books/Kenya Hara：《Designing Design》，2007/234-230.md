![](images/491c25823064aa35ddfa05084411a67268ead906b220ba79ad51240d78cc5f9f.jpg) ## What Ikko Tanaka Passed on to Me In August of 2001, Ikko Tanaka, MUJI's first art director, asked me to take over the art direction of the company and join its board. With the flow of time, the role of founding members also changes. The deliberation and decision that Mr. Tanaka, although he still held sway, wished to pass his role on to a new generation came as a surprise. For the rest of the day, I ruminated on what kind of vision would come to me. As I was considering the future of this brand through a panoramic global perspective, I became oddly elated. It wasn't expanding the number of overseas shops. What came to me instead was posing the MUJI concept to the great talents of the world. The next day I let Mr. Tanaka know that I would happily accept his offer. I also proposed that one more designer of my generation join the board.
