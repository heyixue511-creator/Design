It is perhaps worth noting too that , in a text at least notionally intended for publicatio n , he shares with the sumptuary laws and, indeed, with the writings of Wen Zhenheng and others what I am attempting to identify as a Chinese concern with precision of description of the physical attributes of things. H owever, if great wealth and corruption were widely suspected of being inalienably bound together, something which could only act as a brake on even the most 'rational' kinds of conspicuous consumption, then the corollary social failing of excessive meanness certainly existed, too .
