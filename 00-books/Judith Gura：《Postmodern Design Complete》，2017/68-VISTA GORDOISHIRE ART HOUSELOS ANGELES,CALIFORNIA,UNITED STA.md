# VISTA GORDOI SHIRE ART HOUSE LOS ANGELES, CALIFORNIA, UNITED STATES, 1984 PETER SHIRE FIRE-DAMAGED COTTAGE RESTORED TO MAKE THE MOST OF LIMITED SPACE. BELOW The compact living space is sparely furnished, with light-scale pieces. The restoration and renovation of a fire-damaged house in Echo Park, one of Los Angeles's early suburbs and the site of many cherished midcentury homes, became a family project for PETER SHIRE (also see under “Design") and his father Henry, who contributed most of the woodwork. The quirky exterior, suggesting both Postmodernism and its Deconstructionist offshoot, is of wood, metal, and corrugated fiberglass. Inside, white walls are sparked with polychrome paint and a wellchosen mix of light-scaled furniture that makes the most of limited space. The compact house is only 1,40o sq. ft (i3o sq. m), excluding the garage—ample for a city apartment, but tight for the customary expanse of most California homes. Taking that into account, the compact kitchen was outfitted with upper cabinets 18in. (45.7cm) deep on one side and just 6in. (i5.2 cm) deep on the other, and the bathroom commode was curved to give the feeling of more space. Shire's extroverted style ran free in the bathroom, where colorful handmade and hand-painted glazed tiles provide a lively change of pace after the restraint of the other rooms. A sure attention-grabber for passers-by, the house was designated a Historic-Cultural Monument by the City of Los Angeles. It remains in the Shire family; Peter's brother Billy now lives there, while Peter and his wife Donna are just a block away.
