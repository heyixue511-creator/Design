It appears that if good design is not ignored in some contexts, the purpose for the existence of the system will be nullified."s Consider figure 7.4, a door on a school in Stapleford, England: the latches are up at the very top of the door, where they are both hard to find and hard to reach. This is good design, deliberately and carefully done. The door is to a school for handicapped children, and the school didn't want the children to be able to get out to the street without an adult. Violating the rules of ease of use is just what is needed. Most things are intended to be easy to use, but aren't. But some things are deliberately difficult to use—and ought to be.
