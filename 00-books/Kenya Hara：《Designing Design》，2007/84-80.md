![](images/65fd333e256a2c616cd0c4427b742cfcd16f9e9d7bf77d3c92466da2211031d2.jpg) ## JasperMorrison: Wall Clock The product designer Jasper Morrison, in his thorough pursuit of a universal form, proposed this wall-mounted clock. It's molded out of a very soft paper called “WAVYWAVY." The paper is extremely thin and frail, but once it's fixed neatly to the wall and its hands are moving, it gains creative and structural stability. The appearance of the soft paper immediately after it's been molded isparticularly haptic. When I first asked Morrison to participate in the HAPTIC project, he had a superb response. "Haptic means to make the senses drool, doesn't it?" he said. When we're hungry, we salivate at the sight of an inviting dish or savory grilled meat. That's a gustatory response, but Morrison meant haptic as the experience of seeing something that made all the senses“drool."What a fantastic metaphor.
