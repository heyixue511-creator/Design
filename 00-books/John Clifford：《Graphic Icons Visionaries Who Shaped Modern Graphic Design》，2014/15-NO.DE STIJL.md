INAD MAD French avant-garde poet Guillaume Apollinaire created a book of poems in 1918 called Calligrames. Breaking with the standard horizontal lines of type reading from left to right, he arranged the text in ways to visually express the poems' meanings. “II Pleut" (“it's raining"), is pictured at left. It was a one-time experiment for him, but it influenced the typographic experiments of the Futurists and the Dadaists.
