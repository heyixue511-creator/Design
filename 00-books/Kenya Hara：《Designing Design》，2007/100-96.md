![](images/c9a5a696dbc2ab27ea05bbf0498e166f7cedd208af6862eb96a93a1ee2839935.jpg) ## ShuheiHasado: Geta Next come geta, traditional Japanese raised wooden sandals that are worn barefoot. The plasterer Shuhei Hasado works from his studio in Hida-Takayama, a mountainous area in central Japan. He is a superb plasterer who has taken the championship at the World Skills Competition.Envisioning the possibilities in combining traditional technology with creative frontiers, he has also taken on various new challenges. When I visited him at his studio, he showed me several samples of his experimental wall-plastering methods. His participation in the HAPTIC project was covering the surfaces of several pair of geta. The geta shown here received a moss treatment. Because geta are worn barefoot, imagine how it would feel to place your bare feet on this moss —thefeelingofsaturated moss....
