Such was the enthusiasm of the students that the chairs had to be quickly replaced with all-wooden ones. His pupils were to perpetuate his influence: Anna Simons, unable to study in Prussia as a woman, studied under Johnston and went on to lead her field in Germany; Irene Wellington became Johnston's natural successor, making inscriptions at his behest towards the end of his life; Dorothy Mahoney, who had been Johnston's assistant, continued the teaching of calligraphy at the RCA after his death. While teaching at the College, Johnston developed the famous typeface for the London Underground.
