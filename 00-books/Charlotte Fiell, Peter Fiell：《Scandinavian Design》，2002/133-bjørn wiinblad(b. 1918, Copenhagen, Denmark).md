Wiinblad trained at the Polytechnic in Copenhagen(1936-1939)and then later studied illustration at the Kongelige Danske Kunstakademi (Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts, 1940-1943). Following this, he worked as a ceramicist under Lars Syberg at Taastrup (1943-1946)and was later employed as a designer under Jacob Bang by the Nymølle faïence factory(1946–1956). In 1952, he established his own design studio at Kongens Lyngby, where for the first year he worked alongside Gutte Eriksen (b. 1918). From 1957 onwards Wiinblad designed numerous ceramic pieces for the renowned German porcelain factory Rosenthal, which are distinguished by a whimsical exuberance. Some of Wiinblad's designs possess the playful spirit of Scandinavian folk art, while others (often depicting religious subjects) capture the solemn beauty and mysticism of Byzantine art. His curious hybrid style, which mixes the age-old with the modern, came to epitomize the decorative revival that occurred in Scandinavian design during the 1960s. Wiinblad has also received numerous commissions for the decoration of restaurants and hotels, and has worked as a theatre designer for the Dallas Theater Center and the Pantomime Theatre in Copenhagen's Tivoli Gardens. ![](images/7422a5d8ab9fa62461d64c4c3a5f89a94dd31f4ca11701080bd6f3ca98febbe0.jpg)
