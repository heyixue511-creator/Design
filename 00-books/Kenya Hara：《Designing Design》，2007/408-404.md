## Loose Typography Produced by Kazu Yanagisawa Loose typography is the target of Yanagisawa's research. He attempted to glimpse the congenial appearance of letters that seem to cozy up to us, preparing us for relaxation. Modern typography, represented by Swiss typography, has been very successful because it is clearly measurable, and in the chaotic world of letter usage, this is important. However, typography is also part of a less ordered world; it always interacts with the sensitive human mind and is in continuous contact with naive reality, which can't be decoded by rules and systems. One more essential aspect of typography might be its groping for a fresher expression and wriggling to escape from order. Typography operates in close contact with human emotions and psychology. It might even be called a kind of research in which we gather the subtleties of communication that we would experience by delving into the absolute truth of what we really want to say, or, in some circumstances, stepping into a world full of unsophisticated images. It's loose but not corrupt. It would be great if you could sense this nuance from the embroidered clock.
