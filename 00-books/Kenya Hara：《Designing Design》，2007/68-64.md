From bottom left to right: Macaroni designed by Jun Harada and Hiroyuki Wakabayashi, and the book for the exhibition Architecture and Macaroni. This is how we ended up with 20 new macaroni designs. It wasn't long before our Japanese Architects' Macaroni Exhibition gained notoriety in Italy and was invited by the pasta machine maker “PAVAN"to join in the Milan pasta fair in 1997. The goal of this exhibition was to showcase the creativity of architects. Macaroni was simply a secondary theme. However, the architects' solutions are more or less producible. And the exhibition did achieve some popularity, at least at the pasta affair in Italy. But we haven't had any offers from manufacturers to commercially produce any of these designs. Pasta manufacturing machines are on an even larger scale than I expected. When I saw those machines in Italy, I realized anew that the image of food design I had entertained at the beginning of this project was too romantic. I came to recognize that macaroni has a specific design based on sophisticated planning. The various gaps between my expectations and reality included the mammoth quantity produced and the degree of elaboration and exactitude in planning.
