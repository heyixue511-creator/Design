## Data-Based Grids Very occasionally the grid can report directly on the data. This grid is formed by the location of measurement instruments; the plain dots register a zero reading, in contrast with the white background where no readings were taken. Erasing the grid would erase measured data (rather uneventful, to be sure). Such is not the case for most grid dots, ticks, and lines. ![](images/c89240aac11cf2251012ead0167e7e6111c886d8636d923c48feab39e157d531.jpg) K. V. Roberts and D. E.
