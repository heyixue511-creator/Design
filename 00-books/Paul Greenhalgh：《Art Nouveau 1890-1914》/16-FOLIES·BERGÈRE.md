Louis Nazzy wrote at the time: The walls of Paris have been dignified by the presence of this haloed cat, hieratic, Byzantine, of enormous size whose thin fantastic silhouette hangs high above the crowd in the streets.'5 As many contemporary commentators recognized, the printed image represented a democratic art accessible to all; it made Art Nouveau the style of the street. ![](images/f707f7efa3ca9fbf5dbc09b023943baf8f4bc427c4c1ff92c91cfda828781941.jpg) ![](images/2bcbb57c9c4e8ede3563b5c30f57827e21ac543044c017d51a1a2ef41705e2df.jpg)
