## Kanji Hayashi: SerieMacchel'occhi(Macaroni&Eye) Out of all the participants, Kanji Hayashi, who studiedarchitecture in Rome and is married to an Italian, is the most knowledgeable about Italy. He says, though, that the longer he stayed there, the more conscious he became of Japan. As Hayashi faced theItalians' powerful love for their country day after day, and their firm conviction in their deepest core that Italy is the most pleasant, beautiful country in the world, a strong desire to counter that claim began to simmer within. Hayashi's macaroni has a cross section that reads , or "eye." As the first macaroni he saw was the tube type, he was particular about creating a hollow macaroni while working to find a shape that would still yield the al dente consistency: soft but with texture. He assures us that even in a small factory in Sicily this type could be manufactured. The slicing of a long tube allows for various lengths: 20 cm Lungo, 7-8 cm Rigatoni and Penne, 4 cm Standard, and flat Letter.
