Rendition of the size and shape of the muscles would distract and obstruct the view. 'The expression conveyed by any visual form is only as clear cut as the perceptual features that carry it. A clearly curved line expresses its swing or gentleness with corresponding clarity; but a line whose overalì structure is confusing to the eye cannot carry any meaning. An artist may paint a picture in which a ferocious tiger is easily recognizable; but unless there is ferocity in the colors and lines, the tiger will look taxidermic, and there can be ferocity in the colors and lines only if the pertinent perceptual qualities are brought out with precision. Figure 118 is taken from a Dürer woodcut that shows Christ's head crowned with thorns. Direction, curvature, brightness, and spatial position are defined in such a way that each perceptual element helps to convey to the eyes a precise expression of anguish, which rests on such features as the heavy lid overhanging the staring pupil.
