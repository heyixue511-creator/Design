![](images/fb21c13792efb4bbe8e316305d2457737a1da631035e41f072d763e80978d3b6.jpg) ## Kengo Kuma: Cast-off Snakeskin Paper Towel Next is a paper towel proposed by the architect Kengo Kuma. Made of washi (traditional Japanese paper) so thin and light that if you toss it up, it will float in the air for a few moments. The paper is embossed transversely with a texture that, from its scale, suggests a snake about 2 m long. It's hard work to impress such soft paper one sheet at a time, but these towels are in fact finished by hand. Here, they're set neatly on a thick board of Japanese cypress. After washing up, dry your hands with one. That's what they're made for.
