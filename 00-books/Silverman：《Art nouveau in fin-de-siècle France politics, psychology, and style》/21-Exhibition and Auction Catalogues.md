in which they were promoted. At the center of Silverman's analysis is a powerful new interpretation of the art nouveau visual style that links it to French neuropsychiatry. Art nouveau artists, directly influenced by new theories of mental medicine, defined their organic art forms in relation to nervous palpitations, hypnotic suggestibility, and dream states. At the same time, psychiatric clinicians such as Dr. Jean-Martin Charcot were immersed in artistic concepts and practices, and Charcot's visionary enterprise brought him directly into the orbit of art nouveau. The book concludes with a consideration of specific works by two of the most compelling art nouveau artists, the glassblower Emile Gallé, and the sculptor Auguste Rodin. Included is a startlingly original reading of Rodin's masterwork The Gates of Hell.

This is cultural history at its very best. It will be of interest to all readers concerned with the history of modernism, liberalism, and the birth of the irrational in fin-de-siècle Europe.
