![](images/1513c6b2862ab6e3da1d0211fe2955a4c94490ade19e57d4471a0ced35458245.jpg) ## Nagasaki Prefectural Art Museum: Visual Identification ## Ripple-like Information The Nagasaki Prefectural Art Museum, completed in 2005, centers on vertical stone louvers. Long, narrow stone panels hung consecutively cover all external walls. The architect is Kengo Kuma, who in recent years has shown a tendency towards design that uses not closed, but flexible walls, and architecture exhibiting some translucence, with multiple layers creating profound depth. Depending on the project, he may use anything from bamboo to wood, glass or stone, but all of his work shares a mysterious and breezy semi-transparency of space.
