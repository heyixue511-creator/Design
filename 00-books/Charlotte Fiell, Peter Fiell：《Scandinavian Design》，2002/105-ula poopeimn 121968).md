![](images/2382a695f07d0c46db48450ea1ef940719972369704c7e007e44f463ef50d822.jpg) Ulla Procopé, Valencia tableware for Arabia, c. 1960 # ulla procoé Finihsh 921-1968) The Finnish ceramicist Ulla Procopé studied at the Taideteollinen Korkeakoulu (University of Art and Design) in Helsinki. In 1948, she joined the designplanning studio at Arabia, which was then under the directorship of Kaj Franck.Working alongside Saara Hopea and Kaarina Aho (b. 1925), she designed a number of utilitarian ceramic products such as her Oriental-style GA teapot (1953) and covered marmalade jar(c. 1957), which although industrially produced had strong handmade qualities. In 1957, she designed her popular flameproof stackable and multi-functional cookingset known as Liekki (Flame). This dark brown stoneware range was extremely innovative, with thepot lids doubling as serving dishes. Procopé also designedthe dishwasher-proof “oven-to-table”Ruska service in 1960, which had a mottled brown glaze that gave the feeling of handcrafted pottery even though it was industrially mass-produced. Around the same time, Procopé designed her well-known Valencia tableware range(c. 1960), which was inspired by traditional blue and white artisan pottery from Spain. More decorative than her earlier designs, this folk-style earthenware range remains in production today and is generally regarded as a classic Arabia design. After Franck, Procopé was the most prominent designer ofindustrially produced ceramic wares at Arabia, and as such was awarded a Diplôme d'Honneur at the 1957 (XI) Milan Triennale. Throughout her career, Procopé demonstrated through her best-selling product designs that industrially produced ceramics did not have to embody an impersonal machine aesthetic, but through the use of earth tones, semi-matt glazes and soft yet functional forms, could possess an engaging and characterful allure. In doing so, she had a significant impact on the public perception of Scandinavian Design. ![](images/6964b8e04ec89825a2d5c031dec23b904d99e2c14a82e8dab264a3db38dcd003.jpg)
