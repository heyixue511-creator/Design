## Printing, publishing and advertising The Ottoman period

Lebanon's long printing tradition began with several religious presses. At the beginning of the 18th century, the Patriarch of Aleppo founded a printing press in that city, and between 1706 and 171 it issued a number of religious publications for the Eastern Orthodox Church. About 2o years later, the Catholic convent of St. John the Baptist in Mount Lebanon inaugurated an Arabic press, which also specialized in religious works. The press published its first book in Arabic in 1735 with moveable type designed by the Catholic convert Abdallah Zakher (1684-1748), the press's founder.
