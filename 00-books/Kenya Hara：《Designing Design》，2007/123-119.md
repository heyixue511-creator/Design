![](images/bcf1cfcf04209c0e3d5d205c5cbb9b9cbdeb6e8becda9de2a6b2ed2b83423cf9.jpg) ## Keiko Hirano: Paper Wastebasket The photograph shows the paper wastebasket produced by the designer Keiko Hirano. Made of vulcanized fibers, when viewed flat, it looks like a paper coffee filter, but is generous in size when the two sides are sewn together. The fibers soften up in water, then harden when dried. Each wastebasket is crumpled by hand. Dry, the wastebasket is so hard that you'd expect it to clatter if it fell. This paper wastebasket is truly haptic in appearance. Imagine the rustle of a ball of paper tossed into it; it's a tactile inspiration.
