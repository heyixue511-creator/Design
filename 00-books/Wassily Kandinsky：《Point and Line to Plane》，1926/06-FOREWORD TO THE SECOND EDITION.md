# FOREWORD TO THE SECOND EDITION Since 1914, the tempo of time seems to have been increasing in speed. Inner tensions accelerate this tempo in all spheres familiar to us. One year is possibly the equivalent of at least ten years of a “quiet,” “normal” period. The one year which has elapsed since the appearance of the first edition of this book should be looked upon as the equivalent of ten years. Further advances in the analytical method and, bound up with this, in the synthetic approach of theory and practice—not alone in painting itself, but in other forms of art expression, as well as in the “positive” and “spiritual” sciences —demonstrate the correctness of the principle which forms the basis of this book. The further development of this book at the present time would have involved the addition of numbers of special examples and comparisons, which would have increased its size to such an extent that, for practical reasons, it had to be abandoned. Consequently, I decided to leave the second edition unchanged. Dessau January 1928 Kandinsky
